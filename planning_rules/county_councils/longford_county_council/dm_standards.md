# Longford County Council — Development Management Standards

**Authority:** Longford County Council
**Plan:** Longford County Development Plan 2021-2027 (adopted 19 Oct 2021; effective 30 Nov 2021)
**Chapter:** Chapter 16 — Development Management Standards (Volume 1 Written Statement, p.403+)
**Source URL:** https://www.longfordcoco.ie/services/planning/longford-county-development-plan-2021-2027/volume-1-written-statement.pdf
**Fetch date:** 2026-06-13

> Extracted from Volume 1 Chapter 16 (pdftotext). "DMS16.x" = numbered Development Management Standards. Only stated standards recorded. No inference.

## Residential Density (16.4.5.1)
- DMS16.17: 30 units/ha in Longford town centre
- DMS16.18: 12 units/ha in other towns and settlements
- DMS16.19: rural villages/nodes — take cognisance of prevailing scale/pattern/services

## Building Height (16.4.5.2)
- Greater-than-prevailing heights need strong urban design rationale (Design Statement); determined by prevailing height, proximity of housing, streetscape, impact on protected structures/ACAs.

## Site Coverage (16.4.5.3, DMS16.22-16.23)
- Residential: 50%; Retail: 60%; Industrial & Commercial: 75%; Town Centre Zones: 80%
- DMS16.23: generally not to exceed 80% (higher only in limited circumstances, e.g. public transport corridors).

## Plot Ratio (16.4.5.4, DMS16.24)
- Town Centre / Brownfield: 1.0 to 2.0
- Inner Suburban: 0.5 to 1.0
- Outer Suburban (close to public transport / remote): 0.35 to 0.5

## Private Open Space — Dwelling Houses (16.4.5.5, DMS16.25)
- One Bedroom: 48 m²
- Two Bedroom: 55 m²
- Three Bedroom: 60 m²
- Four Bedroom or more: 75 m²

## Apartment Standards (DMS16.58) — unit floor / storage / private open space
- Studio: 40 m² / 3 m² / 4 m²
- Two Bed (3 person): 63 m² / 5 m² / 6 m²
- Two Bed (4 person): 73 m² / 6 m² / 7 m²
- Three Bedroom: 90 m² / 9 m² / 9 m²

## Minimum House Floor Areas & Internal Storage (DMS16.43)
- One Bedroom: 55 m² / storage 3 m²
- Two Bedroom: 85 m² / 6 m²
- Three Bedroom: 100 m² / 9 m²
- Four Bedroom or more: 110 m² / 10 m²

## Public Open Space (16.4.5.6, DMS16.26-16.27)
- Greenfield sites: minimum 15% of total site area
- DMS16.27: relaxation possible where density <8 dwellings/hectare

## Overlooking / Separation (16.4.5.7, DMS16.32-16.35)
- DMS16.32: minimum 22 metres between directly opposing rear windows at first floor (detached/semi/terraced)
- DMS16.33: 35 metres normally required for overlooking living-room windows/balconies at upper floors
- DMS16.34: reductions possible for single-storey/innovative schemes
- DMS16.35: ground-floor window not less than 1 m from the boundary it faces

## Refuse / Bins (DMS16.47-16.48, 16.4.5.12)
- Each house: screened storage for at least 3 'wheelie' bins
- Bins not immediately adjacent to front door/ground-floor window unless screened alcoves provided

## Bicycle Storage (DMS16.49-16.56)
- Storage via utility/store room near front, rear access, or sheltered parking at a public space
- Estate store required for developments of 10+ dwellings

## Street Lighting (DMS16.62-16.65)
- No trees/vegetation within 7 metres of a public light column
- Public lighting scheme for residential developments of 8+ units; all new lights LED; services underground

## Car Parking (Table 16.1, DMS16.124+)
- Houses: (not separately listed — apartments below)
- Apartments / Flats: 2 per dwelling
- Retail: 1.25-2 per unit; 1 per 25 m² within speed limit / 1.5 per 25 m² outside
- Offices: 1 per 30 m² within speed limit / 1.5 per 30 m² outside
- Financial Institutions: 1 per 20 m²
- Retail Warehousing: 1 per 35 m²
- Cash & Carry: 2 per 90 m² + loading
- Industry: 1 per 30 m² or 1 per 4 employees (greater)
- Warehousing: 1 per 100 m² or 1 per 4 employees (greater)
- Hotels/B&B/Guesthouses: 1 per 2 bedrooms within speed limit / 1 per bedroom outside
- Restaurants: 1 per 7 m² within / 2 per 7 m² outside; Take Away: 6 per unit
- Service Station: 1 per 100 m² + shop requirements
- Surgeries/clinics: 3 per consulting room; Hospitals/Nursing Homes: 1 per bed / 1 per 3 bedrooms

## Rural Housing — Site, Access & Sightlines (16.4.x rural)
- Minimum acceptable site size for a dwelling using an individual treatment system (septic tank & percolation area or equivalent): **0.2 ha**. Increased where adjacent systems exist or percolation tests borderline (upper/lower 20% of permitted limits).
- Sight distances from rural vehicular entrances: Local Roads 90 m; Regional Roads 150 m; National Roads 230 m.
- Maintenance agreements (min. 5 years) for treatment systems before first occupancy.

## EV (Apartments, DMS16.61 / Annex A5)
- Car, EV charging points and bicycle parking to be addressed for apartment schemes.
- A5.2 (Climate Action annex): all one-off housing to install a minimum of 1 EV home charge point.
