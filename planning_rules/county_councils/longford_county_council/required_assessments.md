## Appropriate Assessment (16.2.3)
- Required for all plans/projects (incl. Council's own Part 8 proposals) under Article 6(3) EU Habitats Directive (1992): consider/determine nature-conservation implications on the Natura 2000 network (incl. in-combination effects) before any decision.
- Approval only where, on best scientific evidence, no significant adverse direct/indirect/secondary effects on the integrity of any European site.
- If AA Screening concludes AA required, applicant must prepare a Natura Impact Statement (Stage 2 AA).

## Environmental Impact Assessment Report — EIAR (16.2.4)
- Schedule 5 of Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) lists mandatory development thresholds where EIAR is required.
- Sub-threshold: Council may request a discretionary EIAR by notice in writing where likely significant environmental effects.

## Other Assessments (16.2.5) — may be required depending on location/type:
- Flood Risk Assessment
- Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA) & Road Safety Audits (RSA)
- Architectural Assessment
- Archaeological Assessment
- Landscape Appraisal and Visual Assessment
- Ecological / Biodiversity Assessment
- Infrastructure Assessments, particularly for larger residential developments over 50 units

## Site Suitability (Rural)
- Effluent disposal / percolation: minimum site 0.2 ha; full site-specific details certified by suitably qualified individual; EPA wastewater treatment manual compliance.

## Pre-planning (16.3)
- Pre-planning consultations facilitated/encouraged under Section 247 of the Planning and Development Act 2000 (as amended).
