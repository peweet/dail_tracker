# Required Assessments — Monaghan County Council

**Authority:** Monaghan County Council
**Plan:** Monaghan County Development Plan 2025-2031
**Chapter:** Chapter 15 — Development Management Standards (section 15.1.3 "Assessments Required" and 15.22 Flooding)
**Source URL:** https://monaghan.ie/planning/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2025/11/Written-Statement.pdf
**Fetch date:** 2026-06-13

Triggers enumerated verbatim in the chapter:

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR) — 15.1.3
- EIAR required for development proposals that exceed the mandatory thresholds set out in Schedule 5 of the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended).
- Sub-threshold EIAR may be required where the Planning Authority determines the type/scale of development would require an EIAR to ascertain environmental impact.

## Appropriate Assessment (AA) / Natura 2000 — 15.1.3
- AA required when a development proposal has the potential to impact upon the qualifying interest of a European Site (Natura 2000 Site).
- Approval only where, on best scientific evidence, the proposal will not give rise to significant adverse effects on the integrity of any Natura 2000 Site (alone or in combination).
- Where AA screening cannot rule out significant adverse impact, a Natura Impact Statement (Stage 2 Appropriate Assessment) by a suitably qualified competent professional is required.

## Additional assessments (15.1.3, "may include, but are not limited to")
- Flood Risk Assessment
- Traffic and Transport Assessment, Quality Audit, Road Safety Audit
- Architectural Impact Assessment
- Archaeological Impact Assessment
- Visual Impact Assessment
- Ecological/Biodiversity Impact Assessment
- Infrastructural Assessment
- Hydro-geological Assessment

## Flood Risk Assessment / Justification Test (15.22)
- All development proposals in flood risk areas should be supported with a site-specific Flood Risk Assessment (FRA), per DECLG The Planning System and Flood Risk Management Guidelines (2009) and Circular PL2/2014.
- Sequential approach + Justification Test applied per the vulnerability-vs-flood-zone matrix (Figure 15.1 / Table 15.6):
  - Highly vulnerable development (incl. essential infrastructure): Justification Test in Flood Zone A and Flood Zone B; appropriate in Zone C.
  - Less vulnerable development: Justification Test in Zone A; appropriate in Zones B and C.
- A Strategic Flood Risk Assessment (SFRA) was prepared to inform land-use zonings.

## Architectural Heritage / Protected Structures (15.3.1)
- Proposals for development works to protected structures or within the vicinity of a protected structure must comply with Section 15.3.1 (PSP 1). Demolition of a protected structure not granted except in exceptional circumstances. Architectural Impact Assessment is among the additional assessments listed (15.1.3).

## Site Suitability / Wastewater (rural — 15.9 / 15.10)
- On-site wastewater treatment and disposal must comply with the EPA Code of Practice — Wastewater Treatment and Disposal Systems Serving Single Houses (PE10) (2021), implying a site assessment / percolation testing where on-site treatment is proposed.

## Plan-level statutory assessments
- The plan itself was subject to Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA) and Appropriate Assessment as part of the review process (referenced in Chapter 1, sections 1.14 AA and 1.15 SFRA).
