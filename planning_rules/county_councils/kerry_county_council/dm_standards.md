## Residential density
- No single dwellings/ha figure; density and layout treated as design-led (1.3+). Building height per Urban Development and Building Heights Guidelines (2018).

## Maximum site coverage (1.5.4.8)
- Site Coverage = footprint of development / area of site.
- Maximum **85%** (or up to existing site coverage on brownfield) for **urban areas**.
- **65%** site coverage in **rural areas ("one-off" housing)**.

## Minimum separation distance (1.5.4.10)
- Between directly opposing above-ground-floor (first-floor) windows: separation distance of **22 metres** generally observed for new reciprocal-overlooking housing (informed by topography, design, housing type/mix). Innovative dwelling layouts may allow reduction.

## Public/communal open space
- Residential schemes: open space provided at a minimum rate of **15% of total site area**, complementing the residential layout, centrally located.
- Certain facilities (e.g. specified care/residential): at least **20% open space** of overall site area, with at least **15 sqm open space per resident**.

## Bicycle parking
- 1 bike storage space for every car space; Shops 1 storage space per 100 sq.m.; secure bicycle parking indicated in all cases.

## Electric Vehicle
- Developments to comply with the Building Regulations (Part — Energy Performance of Buildings) Regulations 2021 for Electric Vehicle recharging infrastructure. Car parking design dimensions per DoEHLG/DoT/DTO Traffic Management Guidelines (Section 16).

## Sightlines / access (rural)
- Comprehensive details of adequate **sightlines and stopping distances** required per road category. Where road has **80 km/h or greater** speed limit, a **215 m minimum visibility distance** is required.
- Entrance gates shall be **recessed 4.5 m** behind the line of roadside hedgerow, with side boundaries splayed at **45 degrees** to the public road carriageway.
- Where satisfactory sightlines can only be achieved by removing extensive hedgerows/trees/ditches/stone walls, an alternative site should be sought.

## Building lines / setbacks
- Existing roadside boundaries (hedges, sod banks, stone walls) retained where possible subject to traffic safety; where a setback is required the area between new and old boundary shall be levelled, drained and surfaced.

## Rural housing — Site Area (1.5.10.1)
- Standards apply to residential development in **non-serviced areas** (no public wastewater/water).
- **Site curtilage** required to accommodate/renovate dwellings should reflect the size and scale of the development, integration into landscape, proximity to public roads and adjoining dwellings, and must accommodate a **wastewater treatment system with designed minimum separation distance in accordance with the EPA Code of Practice: Domestic Waste Water Treatment Systems (2021)**.
- No single fixed minimum site-area figure is set — it is performance-based (driven by WWTS capacity and EPA separation distances).
