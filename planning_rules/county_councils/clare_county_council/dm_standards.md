## Rural One-Off House — Minimum Site Area (A1.4.1)
- **Minimum site area: 0.2 ha (0.5 acres)** required to accommodate a rural dwelling.
- On-site wastewater assessed per EPA Code of Practice: Domestic Waste Water Treatment Systems (p.e. <10) 2021.

## Residential Density (A1.4.2 / Core Strategy)
No single dwellings/ha table; density per the Sustainable Residential Development in Urban Areas Guidelines (2009) and Urban Design Manual (2009). Lower-density development must not exceed 20% of total planned housing stock in any settlement. Compact growth targets: 50% of new housing in Limerick City suburbs (in Clare) within existing built-up footprint; 30% across the rest of the county.

## Separation / Privacy Distances (A1.4.2)
- Minimum **1 m** separation between side walls of adjacent buildings (greater for multi-storey).
- Rear garden depth: normally **11 m minimum** (larger may be required; shorter permitted for high-density/infill/older-persons housing where no overlooking).

## Apartments / Building Height
Per "Design Standards for New Apartments — Guidelines for Planning Authorities" (2020) and "Urban Development and Building Height Guidelines" (2018). All new apartments shall meet or exceed minimum floor areas in these guidelines (no local override numbers).

## Design Statement trigger
Required for developments in excess of 3 dwelling houses OR >300 m², and for sites in key locations.

## Childcare provision guide
One childcare facility for a minimum of 20 childcare places per approximately 75 dwellings may be appropriate.

## Road / Street Widths (A1.6.1)
| Road category | Carriageway width | Footpaths |
|---|---|---|
| District Distributor Road | min 6.5 m | two × 2 m + grass margin |
| Local Collector Road (req. for >80 dwellings; no direct dwelling access) | min 5.5 m – max 6.0 m (excl. cycle lanes) | two × 2 m |
| Access Road (<80 units; no direct access road may serve >80 dwellings) | 5.5 m (relax to 5 m for small numbers/street) | 1.5 m |
| Cul-de-sac (generally max 35 dwellings) | 5.5 m | two × 1.5 m (single footway if <20 dwellings; none if <10 and pedestrian priority) |

NRA 41/95 (TII/NRA) is the reference for access design.

## Sight Distances — Entrance Visibility (A1.6.2)
- **X distance (setback):** 2.4 m from edge of hard-surfaced roadway (relaxation to 2 m on very lightly trafficked roads).
- **Y distance** measured along near edge of roadway, per Table A2:

| Design speed of major road (km/h) | 120 | 100 | 85 | 70 | 60 | 50 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Y distance (m) | 295 | 215 | 160 | 120 | 90 | 70 |

## Sustainable Mobility / Transport Statement trigger
Housing developments of **10 units or more** shall be accompanied by a Transport and Mobility Statement.

## Bicycle & Vehicle Parking Standards (A1.6.3, Table A3)

**Residential (car parking)**
- With garage: none additional; without garage: 1 space/unit
- Town centres: 1 space for 1 & 2 bed units; 1 space for 3 bed units
- Other areas: 1 space for 1 & 2 bed units; 2 spaces for 3 bed units
- Hotel/Guesthouse/B&B/Hostel: 1 space/3 residential units; 1 space/room + 1 space/3 staff on duty
- Bicycle (residential): hotel/guesthouse/B&B 1 space/10 bedrooms; hostel 1 space/5 bed spaces

**Non-residential (selected, car ; bicycle)**
- Caravan/Camping site: 1 space/pitch ; 1 space/pitch + 1/3 staff
- Industrial/Manufacturing: 1 space/100 m² ; 2 spaces/100 m²
- Data Centre: 1 space/500 m² ; —
- Warehousing: 1 space/1000 m² ; 1 space/100 m²
- Offices: 24 spaces/100 m² (note: as printed) with bicycle 1/5 employees
- Bank/Financial/Insurance: 1 space/consulting/employee basis ; 1/5 employees
- Convenience retail / Shopping centre (>1000 m² gross): bicycle 1/8 employees + car spaces per 1000 m²
- Retail Warehousing: 10 spaces/1000 m² ; 1/8 employees
- Petrol Filling Station: 1 space/500 m²

(Table A3 car/bicycle columns are interleaved in the PDF; figures above are as printed. Refer to the source PDF Table A3 for the full per-use matrix.)

**Parking provision rules**
- Non-residential: **5% of car parking spaces** for disabled (more where demand likely).
- Non-residential: **10% of car parking spaces** for parent-and-child parking.
- Various non-residential uses: additional **10–15% of staff parking for visitors**.

## EV Charging (A1, per CDP11.10(c))
EV charging point infrastructure required within residential, commercial and mixed-use developments (per Appendix 1 standards), consistent with national requirements.

## SuDS
All new developments (including extensions) required to incorporate Sustainable Urban Drainage Systems and minimise soil sealing.

## Open Space — public
Each green space in residential developments must integrate at least one native oak (or equivalent large indigenous deciduous) tree in the planting/landscaping scheme. (Quantitative % open-space figures are governed by the national Sustainable Residential Development guidelines; no single local % table in Appendix 1.)
