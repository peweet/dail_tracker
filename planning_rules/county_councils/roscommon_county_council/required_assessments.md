# Required Assessments — Roscommon County Council

**Authority:** Roscommon County Council
**Plan:** Roscommon County Development Plan 2022-2028
**Chapter:** Chapter 12 — Development Management Standards (section 12.5 "Assessments Required" and 12.14 Flood Risk Protection)
**Source URL:** https://www.rosdevplan.ie/rccdevpdfs/final/RCC-Dev-Plan-2022-2028-Volume-I.pdf
**Fetch date:** 2026-06-13

Triggers enumerated verbatim in the chapter:

## Assessments Required (12.5, "include but are not limited to")
- Natura Impact Statement (in order to inform the Appropriate Assessment (AA) process)
- Environmental Impact Assessment Report (EIAR)
- Flood Risk Assessment
- Traffic and Transport Assessment
- Architectural Assessment
- Archaeological Assessment
- Landscape Appraisal and Visual Impact Assessment
- Ecological Assessment
- Design Statement

(Design Statement also required dependent on scale/siting of residential development — Design Principles section.)

## Appropriate Assessment (AA) / Natura 2000
- A Natura Impact Statement may be required to inform the Appropriate Assessment process where a proposal could affect a European Site.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
- EIAR among the assessments required; for extractive/larger development an EIAR "shall be submitted where the development" exceeds relevant thresholds (referenced in the extractive-development section of the chapter).

## Flood Risk Assessment (12.14 Flood Risk Protection)
- "Some lands are liable to flooding or development proposals may give rise to flooding in other areas. In such cases a Site Specific Flood Risk Assessment may, as appropriate be required," per The Planning System and Flood Risk Management Guidelines (DECLG, 2009) and the flood-zone framework.

## Traffic and Transport Assessment
- Listed among assessments required (12.5). Larger developments required to prepare a sustainable mobility plan (may lessen car parking requirement).

## Architectural Heritage / Protected Structures
- Architectural Assessment listed among required assessments; the chapter sets out controls for works to or change of use of protected structures (guidance per Architectural Heritage Protection — Guidelines for Planning Authorities) and Areas of Archaeological Potential.

## Site Suitability / Wastewater (rural — 12.12)
- For non-serviced lands, "Site specific analysis for new systems and operating capacity of existing" on-site effluent treatment systems is required (septic tank + percolation area or patent treatment system), i.e. site-suitability / percolation assessment.
