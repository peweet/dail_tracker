C/245/25 
ADJOURNMENT OF THE MEETING 
On the proposal of Cllr Kennedy, seconded by Cllr Mc Gowan it was 
resolved to adjourn the meeting for a short recess to facilitate online 
access to the meeting.  
 
C/246/25 
ELECTION OF THE CATHAOIRLEACH 
On re-commencement of the meeting, Cllr Kennedy as outgoing 
Cathaoirleach thanked the elected members, staff and management of 
Donegal County Council, together with the general public and members 
of the media for their assistance and support over the past year. 
 
She said that it had been a fantastic year and one in which she had got to 
visit most of the county while also having the privilege of making many 
new connections and friends both at home and abroad.  
 
Cllr Kennedy acknowledged the assistance provided by the Leas-
Chathaoirleach, Cllr Jimmy Kavanagh alluding to the excellent work that 
he had carried out over the past year. It had been a good partnership, 
she added, and had worked well in terms of covering and attending the 
numerous events and functions. 
 
Looking back over the last 12 months, she said that she had been 
privileged to nominate Jim Mc Guinness and Enya for Freedom of the 
County awards. Another highlight, she said, had been to name the 
“Donegal Camino Day”.  
 

4 
 
The past year, she said, had given her the opportunity to attend a vast 
range of events and launches associated with the various  council 
services. The holding of Special Housing meetings, she said, had been 
very worthwhile, and that efforts would continue to address the many 
associated issues including the provision of new homes.  
 
Alluding to the vast amount of work ongoing throughout the county 
particularly in relation to roads, piers and harbours, house retrofitting, 
tourism development, marketing etc, she said that it had been  particular 
highlight to see and be involved in the level of activity associated with 
these vital services. Tribute was paid to the Economic Development 
Team, the Climate Action Team, the Planning Regeneration Team, 
Community Development Team and the Emergency Services personnel 
for all their hard work and dedication.  
Thanks, was also extended to the staff in the following areas:  
 
• Age Friendly Team 
• Healthy Donegal 
• Library Services 
• County Museum 
• PEACEPLUS Initiative  
• Cross Border Working Groups 
• LCDC Team 
• Cultural Services Team 
 
Cllr Kennedy thanked the Cultural Service team for their assistance in 
making the recent Cathaoirleach’s Concert in An Grianan Theatre an 
outstanding success with the proceeds going to Donegal Domestic 
Violence. 
 
Concluding Cllr Kennedy thanked all staff members who had assisted her 
in any way through the year and said that it was with great pride that she 
now handed over the position of Cathaoirleach. 
 
Members from the various groupings congratulated Cllr Kennedy on what 
had been an excellent year in office and one in which she had excelled 
as Cathaoirleach. They acknowledged the fair and respectful manner in 
which she had conducted the meetings thus cultivating an atmosphere of 
mutual respect in the chamber. 
 
It was acknowledged that she had been an excellent representative for 
the county creating a positive impression wherever she went. There were 
calls also for more diversity within the Council given that the level of 
female representation was one of the lowest in the country.  
 
Members noted that Cllr Kennedy had taken part in a wide range of 
events, meetings and seminars over the past year all of which she had 
attended with enthusiasm, whilst representing the people of Donegal in a 
professional and competent manner. 
 

5 
 
Members then thanked Cllr Kennedy and wished her well for the future. 
The Chief Executive on behalf of the management and staff of Donegal 
County Council thanked the outgoing Cathaoirleach for her great work. 
One of her greatest attributes, he said, was the fact that she had been a 
good listener whilst holding her own counsel on the various issues that 
had arisen. These qualities had, he advised, made Cllr Kennedy an 
excellent ambassador for Donegal creating a modern and positive image 
of the county. He paid tribute to her skill in conducting meetings and the 
courtesy that she had extended to all. 
 
He proceeded to acknowledge the work ethic of the Leas-
Chathaoirleach, Cllr Jimmy Kavanagh who also played an important role 
in events over the past year.  
 
Members acknowledged the presence in the Chamber of Mr Charlie Mc 
Conlaogue, T.D, Minister of State at the Department of Culture, 
Communications and Sport and Senator Niall Blaney. 
 
Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig expressed concern that nine councillors were 
effectively excluded from access to the role of Cathaoirleach and said 
that this was not acceptable in the interests of democracy.  
 
Cllr Mc Dermott at this juncture proposed Cllr Paul Canning for the 
position of Cathaoirleach for the period 2025-2026.  
 
This, he said, was a proud day for Fianna Fáil in Inishowen and for the 
Canning family. He welcomed Cllr Canning’s wife, Stephanie, his 
daughter, Vanessa together with his parents Noel and Annie to the 
meeting.  
 
Cllr Canning, he contended, had an immense knowledge of the planning 
system and the Council in general and would be an exceptional 
representative for Donegal, having ably represented the people of 
Inishowen over the last 11-12 years. He would, he added, play a leading 
role in making Donegal better and said that he was honoured to propose 
Paul Canning for this role.  
 
Cllr Bradley seconded the proposal, noting that Cllr Canning would be an 
excellent representative and would fight hard for the people of Donegal. 
 
As there were no other nominations Cllr Canning was elected 
Cathaoirleach for the period 2025-2026. 
 
Taking the Chair, Cllr Canning thanked all who had supported him for the 
position and said that it was an honour to have been selected. He 
thanked all his political colleagues, his parents Noel and Annie, his wife 
Stephanie and daughter Vanessa together with his extended family circle 
for their ongoing support and co-operation .The Cathaoirleach welcomed 
his uncle Patsy and wife Margaret who had travelled from Scotland for 
the occasion. 

6 
 
He paid tribute to Cllr Kennedy who, he contended, would be a hard act 
to follow and thanked his proposer and seconder for their support.  
 
He said that he would aim to be fair and honest in his dealings with all 
and hoped to concentrate on the development of the county and in 
ensuring that the best use was made of all national allocations. There 
was much to do in terms of housing aspect, he added. Donegal, he 
advised, had a natural unspoilt landscape and it was a priority going 
forward that the county would become a prime tourism destination. He 
was, he said, looking forward to working with all concerned to make 
Donegal a better place to live and work in. 
 
Members congratulated him on his election acknowledging that his 
capability and considerable experience would be of great benefit in the 
year ahead. He was, they advised someone who had his finger on the 
pulse emanating from his chairmanship of the A5/N2 Cross Border 
Committee and the Donegal PEACEPLUS Partnership. 
 
This, they said was a proud day for his constituents and assured him of 
their support and co-operation in the year ahead.  
 
Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig asked that the Gaeltacht and the Islands be 
included as a priority for the Cathaoirleach during his year in office  
 
The Chief Executive on behalf of the management and staff of the 
Council assured him of his ongoing support and co-operation noting that 
the Cathaoirleach with his cross-border experience was well aware of the  
need to promote and enhance the shared vision for the Northwest. He 
welcomed Minister Mc Conalogue and Senator Blaney to the meeting.  
 
C/247/25 
ELECTION OF THE LEAS-CATHAOIRLEACH 
Cllr Canning proceeded to invite nominations for the office of Leas-
Cathaoirleach. 
 
Cllr Brogan proposed, seconded by Cllr Coyle that  the position of Leas-
Chathaoirleach be taken by Cllr Donal Mandy Kelly.  
 
He welcomed Cllr Kelly’s wife Aisling and son Donal to the chamber 
together with his father former Cllr Donal Kelly and his mother Jacquie.  
He said that Cllr Kelly would be an able assistant for the Cathaoirleach 
and an excellent ambassador for the county. At this juncture he paid 
tribute to the outgoing Leas -Cathaoirleach, Cllr Jimmy Kavanagh who, 
he contended had excelled in the role. 
 
Cllr Coyle said that Cllr Kelly’s hard work and determination had seen 
him top the poll in the Letterkenny Electoral Area at the 2024 Local 
Election and that this was a testament to his strong work ethic. These, he 
said, were excellent skills which he would bring to the position of Leas-
Chathaoirleach.  

7 
 
As there were no other nominations it was thus resolved to appoint Cllr 
Donal Mandy Kelly as Leas-Chathaoirleach for the period 2025-2026. 
 
Cllr Kelly paid tribute to the outgoing Cathaoirleach and the Leas 
Chathaoirleach. Continuing, he thanked his proposer and seconder and 
his fellow councillors for their support. It was, he advised a proud day for 
his family, and he thanked and acknowledged the support that they had 
shown him over the past number of years. He said that he would work 
hard and do his best to represent the Council to the best of his ability.  
 
Members congratulated him on his appointment and assured him of their 
support and assistance in the months ahead.  
 
The Chief Executive extended congratulations on behalf of the 
management and staff of Donegal County Council and said that he was 
looking forward to working with him during his term in office. 
 
This concluded the business of the meeting.
