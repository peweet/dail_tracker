# Required Assessments / Triggers — Cork City Council

Source: Cork City Development Plan 2022–2028, Chapter 11 — Placemaking and Managing Development. Fetched 2026-06-13.
URL: https://consult.corkcity.ie/en/consultation/draft-cork-city-development-plan-2022-2028/chapter/11-placemaking-and-managing-development

The chapter references the following assessment triggers:

- **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR):** all applications screened; may be required below thresholds where significant environmental effects are likely.
- **Appropriate Assessment (AA):** all applications screened in respect of European Sites (Natura 2000).
- **Ecological reports:** may be required for environmentally sensitive areas.
- **Traffic & Transport Assessment:** required where excessive impact on the road/transport network is likely; mandatory for access to the national road network.
- **Visual Impact Assessment:** for developments affecting protected views or breaking the city skyline.
- **Daylight / Sunlight analysis:** required for schemes exceeding 50 units.
- **Retail Impact Assessment:** for significant retail over 1,000 sq.m net floor area.
- **Design Statements:** required for significant proposals or developments in sensitive areas.
- **Flood Risk:** referenced (e.g. Traveller accommodation criteria exclude Flood Zones A/B); detailed flood-risk policy sits elsewhere in the plan.
- **Built Heritage Assessment:** built-heritage statements may be required (protected structures / ACAs).
