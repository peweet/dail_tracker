Pleanail - Planning 
 
(i) 
Tuarascáil an Phríomhfheidhmeannaigh ar Chur i bhFeidhm NPF 
Chief Executive’s Report on NPF Implementation 
Tim Buter, Director of Services informed the Members that this report is in 
response to the Ministerial Guidelines on NPF Implementation and advised 
that the Council must ensure that housing needs are met in Kilkenny up to 2034 
and then onto 2040.  The requirements for housing in Kilkenny City and County 
over the next 9 years is 948 units per annum. 
 
Mr. Denis Malone provided an update and gave an overview on the following: 
 
▪ 
2018 NPF planned for total population of 5.85 million by 2040. 
▪ 
Updated projection will increase by 5.7 million by 2030 and 6.1 million 2040 
▪ 
Ministerial Guidelines revised housing growth figures for each Local 
Authority - Kilkenny’s allocation is revised upward from Development 
Plan 2021. 
▪ 
Assess zoned and available land. 
▪ 
Housing Growth Requirement: - 173 units per year to 2034. 
▪ 
Capacity of zoned, undeveloped land is 4,484 units. 
▪ 
Target of 948 homes per year up to 2034 
 

3 
 
 
 
▪ 
Rezone Strategic Reserve land in Kilkenny City [Loughmacask and Breagagh Valley] 
▪ 
Incorporate Tier 1 Strategic Reserve land in Ferrybank. 
▪ 
Include new unit requirements in Variation 5: Castlecomer Settlement Plan. 
▪ 
Include Settlement Plans for District Towns (Callan, Thomastown, 
Graiguenamanagh) 
▪ 
Finalise Variation 5 (Castlecomer) – December 
▪ 
Finalise Variation 6 (Ferrybank) – December Meeting. 
▪ 
Commence Variation 8 (Kilkenny City, Callan, Rosbercon, Thomastown, 
Graiguenamanagh). 
▪ 
Environmental Screening (AA/SEA) 
▪ 
Consultations with EPA, DECC and Department of Housing. 
▪ 
Publication scheduled for 26th  January.

Tim Butler and Denis Malone responded to queries as follows 
 
• 
The National Planning Framework outlines a structured approach - 50% of 
growth allocated to Cities, 30% to CSO towns and the remainder to smaller 
rural towns and villages. 
• 
Uisce Éireann has a programme of work in progress. 
• 
Infrastructure will be delivered by developers and subsequently taken over 
by Uisce Eireann. 
• 
Council’s housing policies support the continued population of rural areas. 
• 
A Masterplan was initiated with the HSE prior to COVID but was 
suspended during the pandemic.  Resourcing issues delayed progress; 
engagement resumed earlier this year.  The draft Masterplan has been 
submitted to the HSE for consultation, with feedback expected in the 
coming weeks.  Publication is anticipated in early New Year. 

4 
 
 
• 
Three Water Schemes identified - Castlecomer, Graignamanagh, and 
Bennettsbridge.  Castlecomer and Graignamanagh are approximately 3–4 
years from completion.  Uisce Eireann has provided timelines for resolving 
water capacity issues in these areas over the next 3 to 4 years. 
• 
Wastewater projects are dependent on Uisce Eireann funding, which is 
sourced from Government allocations. 
• 
Abbey Quarter - Hotel site is at an advanced stage of development and 
work is ongoing on two plots designated for residential use. 
 
It was proposed by Cllr. Michael McCarthy, seconded by Cllr. Tomás 
Breathnach and agreed that: 
 
“We, the Members of Kilkenny County Council, having considered the Report to 
Elected Members on NPF Implementation: Housing Growth Requirements, do hereby 
resolve to commence the process to vary the Kilkenny City & County Development 
Plan 2021 as recommended in that Report.”
