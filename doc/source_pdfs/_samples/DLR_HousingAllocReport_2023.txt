D佖 Laoghaire-Rathdown County Council
    Housing Allocations Report for 2023

D佖 Laoghaire-Rathdown County Council's current Allocation Scheme was
adopted by the Elected Members of the Council in April 2021 following extensive
consultation with the Housing Strategic Policy Committee (SPC) and is in
accordance with Section 22 of the Housing (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2009
and the Social Housing Regulations 2011.

The Scheme provides the framework through which all available social housing
properties are allocated to households that have been deemed eligible for social
housing support in accordance with Section 20 of the Housing (Miscellaneous
Provisions) Act 2009 and for existing tenants of the Council who have applied for
a transfer to alternative accommodation.

The Housing Department has upgraded Choice Based Letting (CBL) as part of
the move towards providing improved access for housing applicants through the
provision of a Housing Online system. CBL is the system that is used to advertise
and allocate properties to housing applicants and tenants on our transfer lists.
Those registered for CBL can also check their waiting list position on their
respective waiting list.

D佖 Laoghaire-Rathdown County Council allocated a total of 508 properties in
2023 in accordance with the Allocation Scheme. This is the highest number of
allocations made by the Council to date.

We continue to support disabled people to access housing. 21% of all allocations
in 2023 were to households with a member who had a disability.

In 2023 we continued to promote "Downsizing" or Rightsizing for existing
Council tenants as a way to facilitate the needs of those who find their current
accommodation too big or unsuitable to their need. Included in the 191 transfer
allocations listed above were 6 allocations to households that were approved to
downsize. We also prioritised all applications to downsize and will continue to
make every effort to match them to a more suitable property.
