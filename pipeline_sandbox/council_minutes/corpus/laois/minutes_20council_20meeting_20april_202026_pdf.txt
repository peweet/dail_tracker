54. 
CONSIDERATION OF THE DRAFT ANNUAL FINANCIAL STATEMENT FOR 2025 
UNDER SECTION 108 OF THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT ACT, 2001 
 
Ms. Julie Bergin, Director of Finance and Culture introduced the draft Annual Financial 
Statement for 2025 and confirmed that it had been prepared in accordance with the Local 
Government Accounting Code of Practice. She summarised the main areas and outlined 
details of the surplus arising at the end of 2025. 
On the proposition of Cllr. Catherine Fitzgerald, seconded by Councillor John Joe Fennelly, 
the draft Annual Financial Statement for 2025 was agreed.  
In relation to the over-expenditure for 2025, Ms. Bergin advised that the over-
expenditure arose as a result of additional funding received by the Council. On the 
proposition of Councillor John King, seconded by Councillor James Kelly, the revised 
expenditure for the financial year ended 31st December 2025 was agreed. 
With regard to the transfers to and from Capital, Ms. Bergin outlined the details regarding 
same. On the proposition of Councillor Paschal McEvoy, seconded by Councillor Ollie 
Clooney the transfers to and from capital were agreed.  
The Chief Executive thanked Ms. Julie Bergin, Director of Finance & Culture, Ms. Sharon 
Murray, Financial Management Accountant and all the team for their assistance in the 
preparation of the A.F.S. for 2025.

NOTICE OF MOTIONS  
Notice of Motion No: 25/2026 
 
Councillor Barry Walsh proposed the following Notice of Motion:- 
 
"That Laois County Council requests from Ms. Norma Foley, T.D and Minister for 
Children, Disability and Equality dedicated funding for the appointment of a dedicated, 
full-time and fully resourced Access and Inclusion Officer for Laois County Council.  This 
motion formally supports the Irish Wheelchair Association's Access ‘It's No Joke 
Campaign’, which highlights the critical need for a dedicated professional in every local 
authority to improve disability access to public services and amenities in our 
communities.  This motion also calls on Laois County Council to prepare a report on 
additional measures it has taken to enact the provisions of the Disability Act 2005, in the 
last 5 years, and this report to be presented at CPG level, shared with Councillors and 
disability groups in the County” 
 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor John King.  The Members were advised 
that correspondence would issue to the Minister for Children, Disability and Equality in this 
regard.  It was also agreed that the Notice of Motion be referred to the AILG for circulation to 
the other Local Authorities. 
 
Notice of Motion No: 26/2026 
 
Councillor Aisling Moran proposed the following Notice of Motion:- 
 
“That Laois County Council call on the Government to act on their election promise and 
enact the Occupied Territories Bill to immediately stop importing all products from Israel 
and to refuse all US Military planes stopping in Ireland and using Irish Airspace like other 
members states in the EU have done” 
 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor Tommy Mulligan.  It was agreed that 
correspondence issue to the Government in relation to this.
