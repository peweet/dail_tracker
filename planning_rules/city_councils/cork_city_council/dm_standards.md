## Building heights
- Heights vary by location (lower/upper ranges). City Centre target 4–6 storeys; South Docks permits up to **10 storeys** for exceptional tall buildings.

## Dwelling size mix (targets by location)
- City Centre: 20% studios, 25% one-bed, 35% two-bed, 20% three-bed.
- City Docks: 10% studios, 30% one-bed, 45% two-bed, 20% three-bed.
- City Suburbs: 10% studios (at transit/HEI locations only), 20% one-bed, 34% two-bed, 28% three-bed, 8% four-bed+.

## Open space
- Public open space: **15%** for greenfield sites; **10%** general provision.

## Private amenity space (houses)
- At least **48 sq.m** (smaller acceptable with quality justification).

## Apartments
- National guidelines (2018) apply; minimum **20%** glazing to habitable rooms.

## Separation / privacy
- Traditional minimum of **22 m** between rear elevations acknowledged; lesser distances often appropriate in an urban context.

## Rural / ribbon development
- Rural one-off-house minimum **site area is not explicitly quantified** in the chapter (Cork City is predominantly urban).
- Ribbon development: **five or more houses** on one side of any **250 m** of road frontage is treated as problematic ribbon development.

## Other thresholds stated
- Daylight/sunlight analysis required for schemes exceeding **50 units**.
- Retail Impact Assessment for significant retail over **1,000 sq.m** net floor area.

## Car parking / cycle parking / plot ratio / site coverage / EV / bin storage
- Not enumerated as numeric tables within the captured Chapter 11 text; the chapter references national standards. No specific Cork numeric parking ratios, plot ratio, site coverage, EV or bin-storage figures captured.
