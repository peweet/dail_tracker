# Louth County Council — Development Management Standards

**Authority:** Louth County Council
**Plan:** Louth County Development Plan 2021-2027 (adopted 30 Sept 2021; effective 11 Nov 2021)
**Chapter:** Chapter 13 — Development Management Guidelines

## Residential Density & Plot Ratio (Table 13.3)
- Regional Growth Centres (Dundalk, Drogheda): 50 per ha (town/village centre), 35 (edge of settlement); plot ratio 2 (centre) / 1 (edge)
- Self-Sustaining Growth Towns (Ardee, Dunleer): 35 (centre), 25 (edge); plot ratio 1 / 0.5

## Building Heights
- Considered case-by-case; design statement required; informed by Urban Development and Building Height Guidelines (2018).

## Plot Ratio & Site Coverage — Employment Buildings (Table 13.8)
- Offices: site coverage 60%, max plot ratio 2:1
- Manufacturing: 60%, 1.5:1
- Distribution / Warehousing: 60%, 1.5:1

## Privacy / Separation (13.8.9.1)
- Minimum 22 metres between directly opposing first-floor habitable room windows (not required for non-habitable rooms — bathrooms, stairwells, landings).

## Private Open Space (Table 13.4)
- 1-2 bedroom: 50 m² (town centre/infill/brownfield) / 60 m² (greenfield/suburban)
- 3 or more bedroom: 60 m² / 80 m²
- Apartments & duplexes: see Table 13.5 (apartment guidelines)

## Public Open Space (13.8.15)
- Normally 15% of total site area (reductions case-by-case for high-quality space).
- Developments up to 5 units with high-quality private open space above minimum: no public open space requirement.

## Boundary Treatment (13.8.11)
- Rear boundary: 2 m high block wall
- Side boundaries between properties: 2 m high (timber must be bonded, concrete posts)

## Landscaping (13.8.12)
- Tree replacement: 5:1 in Drogheda & Dundalk; 10:1 in the County area.

## Single-Storey / Unit Mix (13.8.13)
- Developments >50 units encouraged to provide single-storey units; >100 units require at least one single-storey unit per 100 residential units (unless demonstrated no demand).

## Bin Storage (13.8.19)
- Communal bin facilities: conveniently located, screened, well ventilated.

## EV Charging (13.16.9 / 13.8.18)
- Charging points (wiring & ducting) in all car parking areas; aim for minimum 20% of total spaces.
- Communal parking areas: ducting/wiring for EV charging at 20% of total spaces.

## Parking Space Dimensions (Table 13.9)
- Perpendicular to kerb: 5.0 m × 2.5 m
- Adjacent to wall/obstruction: 5.0 m × 2.75 m
- Parallel to kerb: 6.0 m × 2.5 m
- Accessible parking bay: 5.0 m × 2.5 m plus 1.2 m to side and rear
- Loading bay: 6.0 m × 3.0 m
- Circulation areas: 6.0 m in width

## Car Parking Standards (Table 13.11) — Areas 1 (town/settlement centres) / 2 (high public transport) / 3 (all other areas)
- Residential dwelling: 1 / 1 / 2 per unit
- Apartment: 1 / 1 / 2 per apartment
- Retail / Food Retail: 1 per 20 m² / 1 per 10 m² / 1 per 5 m² (food retail 1 per 30/50/50 m²)
- Offices: 1 per 50 m² / 1 per 20 m² (varies)
- Schools: 1 per classroom
- Hotel: 1 per 2 bedrooms
- Clinics/surgeries: 2 spaces per consulting room
- Nursing homes: 1 per employee and 0.5 per bed
- Stadium / large uses: to be determined by the Planning Authority
(Three-Area demand-based standards; full table in source.)

## Entrances and Sightlines (Table 13.13 — Minimum visibility standards)
- National & Protected Routes: Y (sight distance) 215 m; X 3.0 m (1-6 houses), 4.5 m (7+/non-domestic); ground 0.6-1.05 m
- Regional Routes: Y 125 m; Regional Road / 75 m variants; X 3.0 m / 4.5 m
- Local Road / Cul-de-sac: X 4.5 m
- In certain circumstances X setback of 2.4 m allowed; Y setback of 3.0 m allowed (DMURS may modify).

## Road Gradients (13.16.19)
- Flat gradient no more than 1:50 (2%) at new junctions/entrances for first 5 m from edge of public road.

## Rural One-Off Housing
- Chapter 13 sets qualifying criteria by Rural Policy Zone (criteria in Chapter 3 'Housing', §3.17.4) rather than a single fixed minimum site area.
- Ribbon development defined as five or more houses on one side of any given 250 m of road frontage.
- Backland development only in Rural Policy Zones 1 & 2 where site family-owned ≥15 years and landholding ≥1.5 hectares; only one dwelling per landowner.
- On-site wastewater systems must meet EPA Code of Practice (p.e. 10).
