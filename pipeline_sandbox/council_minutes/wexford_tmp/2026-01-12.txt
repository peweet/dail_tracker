Notices of Motion
10.1 Cllr. T. Forde, Cllr. A.R. O'Brien and Cllr. C. Doyle
"This council recognises the plight of the lrish Fisheries and the injustice at the heart of the
Common Fisheries Policy.
We ask the Irish government and all representatives to adopt a wholly different approach in
Brussels and to move from a soft touch diplomatic stance to hardball negotiations and request;
Efforts to make access to EU waters and markets conditional on respecting shared stock
management and to penalise non-EU states like Norway and lceland that overfish shared
stocks.
The full application of the Hague Preferences to allow Ireland a higher proportional quota for
certain species when stocks fall below critical levels, and the condemnation of other states
who have blocked this.
A substantial emergency financial rescue package from the Irish government and the EU to
mitigate the immediate impact of severe quota cuts and job losses, including the utilisation of
the remaining Brexit Adjustment Reserve (BAR) funds and using available EU fuel subsidies.
A fair allocation of quotas that reflects Ireland's significant marine territory (12% of EU waters,
but less than 6% of the quotas), rather than historical catch levels (relative stability) which
disadvantage thecountry."
10.2 Cllr. M. Sheehan
"That this Councils calls on the Government to fast-track and publish the Legislation outlawing
the use of anonymous social media accounts, profiles and user/chat groups on all social
media platforms.
Upon adoption, the motion is forwarded to all local authorities for consideration. "
