## Residential Density
- Rural areas: developments with densities of **up to 10 dwellings per hectare** in defined rural settlements.
- Density otherwise responds to the character, scale and density of the settlement (low-density residential addressed at §13.30.8). No single urban uph table; guideline-referenced.

## Separation / Privacy (§13.9)
- **"22 m rule"** — traditionally about **22 m between two-storey dwellings** / between opposing upper-floor windows.
- Setback of **at least 7.5 m** from the rear of any structure in backland situations.
- Minimum **25 m² of consolidated usable private open space** per relevant unit.

## Plot Ratio & Site Coverage (§13.21)
- **Maximum plot ratio: 2.0 in urban areas, 1.0 in all other areas.**
- **Maximum normal site coverage: 65%**, allowed to increase **up to 85% in urban areas** (or up to existing).

## Public Open Space (§13.20.2)
- Not below **10% of the site area**.
- Schemes of **25 or more units: a minimum of 0.25 hectares** of public open space must be provided.
- **Minimum 10 m² of dedicated playable space per residential unit.**

## Sightlines & Access (§13.22.1)
- Visibility assessed from the proposed entrance and for impact on road traffic, in accordance with **DMRB** requirements.
- Excessive hedgerow removal resisted; hedgerows removed to achieve sightlines must be reinstated behind the sightlines and maintained.
- Vehicular entrance not wider than **3 m or 50% of the front boundary**; hardstanding parking space **2.5 m × 5 m**.

## Car Parking
- Detailed car-parking standards are in **Chapter 12 (§12.12)**, not Chapter 13 (not transcribed here — see `raw/`).

## Rural / One-off Housing (§13.22)
- Rural settlement policy and one-off-house siting criteria are set in **Chapter 7** (County Kilkenny Rural Design Guide applies). No single fixed minimum-site-area figure in Chapter 13.
- Ribbon-development guard: **4 houses on any one side of a given 250 m of road frontage** indicates likely ribbon development.

## Other
- Drainage/reclamation of a **wetland greater than 0.1 ha** needs planning permission.
