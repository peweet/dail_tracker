Cllr. McClearn referenced speed ramps he recently observed on the N55 in the village of
Ballynagh, Co. Cavan and queried how such measures were permitted in Cavan while similar
installations are not approved in Galway. In response, Mr. Haugh advised that, although
there is no specific policy prohibiting traffic calming measures, any such interventions must
be considered on a case-by-case basis. He noted that traffic calming ramps are not
necessarily the starting point, and that it is generally preferable to implement horizontal
realignment or other traffic calming measures at the entrance to a village or town, through
landscaping or other visual measures so that drivers clearly recognise the change in
environment. He further noted that the installation of speed ramps carries a significant
financial cost,and anysuchinterventionsmust beconsideredonacase-by-casebasis.

Cllr. Canning queried who is responsible for removing the reeds at Portumna Bathing Area,
and Ms.Ruane confirmed that the Environment Department, Galway County Council, are
taking the lead on this project as there is a risk to Blue Flag status.
Cllr. Canning highlighted the ongoing issue with the illegal encampment in Portumna and
that the regular campers are now avoiding the area.
Cllr. McClearn noted that faults reported to Electric Skyline three months ago remain
unresolved and suggested that Galway County Council consider revoking the contract, citing
the 10-working-day guarantee. Cllr. Kelly seconded the proposal, referencing Abbey Street.
Mr. Troy advised that he will review the existing contract and contact Electric Skyline
regarding the highlighted complaints, and report back at the next MD Meeting. He noted
that delays may be possibly due to infrastructural improvement needed rather than direct
replacement of bulbs, which was outside the control of Electric Skyline.
Cllr. Kinane thanked the Gort Area Office for the repair work carried out on the Boardwalk in
the Cowpark Clarinbridge and enquired whether bollards will be installed indicating
willingness to cover any associated costs through notice of motion monies.

additional costs required to deliver the planned Wastewater Treatment Plant for
Clarinbridge and Craughwell. Cllr. Killilea advised that the motion should be brought to the
Plenary Meeting and Cllr. Kinane stated that support from Councillors at the Plenary Meeting
wouldbe appreciated.

be contacted regarding Notice of Motion funding.
Cllr. Canning raised concerns about lack of reflection on M18 motorway signage when
