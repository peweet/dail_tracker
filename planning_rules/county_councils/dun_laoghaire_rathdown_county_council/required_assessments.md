Assessment triggers referenced in the Written Statement (quoted, no inference). The two-column layout makes precise per-trigger paragraph extraction difficult, but the following assessment types are explicitly named:

## Appropriate Assessment (AA)
- Appropriate Assessment / Habitats Directive screening referenced throughout the plan (31 occurrences), with Natura/European-site screening and Stage 2 AA referenced.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
- Environmental Impact Assessment referenced (10 occurrences) for projects meeting Planning and Development Regulations thresholds.

## Flood Risk Assessment
- Flood Risk Assessment referenced (20 occurrences); plan supported by Strategic Flood Risk Assessment and sequential approach.

## Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment
- Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment, photographic survey and condition report required for works to protected structures / within ACAs (retention of features sought).

## Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA)
- Traffic and Transport Assessment required (per the 2014 TTA Guidelines and Active Travel Routes / School Traffic Zones guidelines) to assess traffic impacts on the surrounding road network.

## Site suitability
- Site suitability referenced (3 occurrences). Justification Test for flood risk is not explicitly named as a separate term in the captured text (handled via the SFRA / sequential approach).

Note: Exact trigger thresholds for each assessment are dispersed across Chapter 12 and supporting appendices; the assessment types above are confirmed present in the plan.
