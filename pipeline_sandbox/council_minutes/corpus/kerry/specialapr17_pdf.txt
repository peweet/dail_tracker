The meeting commenced at 10.30a.m. 
 
Cllr. M. O’Shea, Cathaoirleach, commenced the meeting.  It was noted that the four Schedule of 
Municipal District Works Plans have already been extensively debated and adopted.  It was agreed 
that the Service Delivery Plan is the Elected Member’s Plan and should be conveyed as such.  It 
was further agreed that a press release would issue on the achievements met in each Municipal 
District. in terms of the Service Delivery Plan for the past 12 months. 
 
Item 1. 
Consideration of the Service Delivery Plan 2017

O’Sullivan, Director of Services, Operations and Safety, for the preparation of the Service

Mr. Charlie O’Sullivan acknowledged the appreciation conveyed to the staff. He stated that he will 
be arranging a press release regarding the works carried out in the MD areas.  He then responded 
to broader issues raised as follows:- 
 
• The key challenges for the directorate are the level of state funding which the Department 
of Transport, Tourism & Sport has acknowledged is inadequate.  Funding is driven by 
Corporate Plan and LECP as prescribed in legislation.  
• A regular programme of footpath repairs to be put in place over the next year.  
• Wild Atlantic Way is identified as a priority in the Corporate Plan.  Conference to be 
arranged which will bring together all the stakeholders.  
• In terms of Greenways, the priorities are South Kerry and the Southern Trail.  In relation to 
the Southern Trail, the final legal agreement has been secured with CIE and this is very 
welcome. It is the intention of Kerry County Council to progress with the Tralee/Fenit 
Greenway later this year.  
• Public Lighting:- new procedures are being put in place to report the status of repairs to 
Elected Members.  
• Pavement Overlays:- no details received yet for 2017. 
• Listowel By-Pass:- sought pre-planning meeting with An Bord Pleanála and will be 
proceeding with an application and preparing an EIS. 
• Kilcummin Road deferred until the sewerage scheme is completed. Still on the programme 
but timeline depends on the completion of the sewerage scheme.  
• Not aware of the issue of the caretaker at Aglish Cemetery but will investigate it. 
• Confirmed that TDs do not have voting rights in relation to the development and adoption 
of the Service Delivery Plan.  The Plan is developed following the budget and grant 
allocation and it is not a matter for Oireachtas Members.  

7 
• Acknowledged impact of staffing and resources on the programme. Confirmed that panels 
are in place and there will be appointments but staffing levels will not be going back to pre-
recession levels, as the funding is not in place.  
• Plant:- reviewing operations of Machinery Yard.  Trialling a high velocity patching unit at 
present in the Killarney area.  Will be considering other areas following this.  Need to 
consider best value for money before purchasing.  
• Confirmed that Plunkett Street will be completed by the 14th April 2017. 
• In relation to the CPO at Knockeenduff, Killarney it is intended that the CPO will be 
published within the next fortnight. Negotiations are ongoing with the landowner in this 
regard. 
 
 
Cllr. Toiréasa Ferris and Cllr. Norma Foley expressed their appreciation to Mr. Eamon Scanlan, 
S.E.E. for all his work in the Tralee area over the past number of years.  The Elected Members 
concurred with this expression of appreciation.  
 
Cllr. Aoife Thornton requested that Minister Ross is invited to meet with the Elected Members 
when he is visiting Kerry to discuss the most serious issues in relation to our roads network.  This 
was unanimously agreed. 
 
On the proposal of Cllr. Robert Beasley and seconded by Cllr. Séamus Cosaí Fitzgerald, the 
Service Delivery Plan 2017 was adopted as presented.
