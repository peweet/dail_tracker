NOTICE OF MOTIONS  
Notice of Motion No: 19/2026 
 
Councillor Conor Bergin proposed the following Notice of Motion: 
 
“That Laois County Council calls on the Minister for Finance, Mr. Simon Harris, T.D., to 
review the current tax rules that apply to personal investment products to make it easier 
for ordinary people to save and invest for the future and to reform these rules so that 
responsible long-term saving is supported rather than discouraged” 
 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor John King.  It was agreed that 
correspondence issue to the Minister for Finance in relation to same. 
 
Notice of Motion No: 20/2026 
 
Councillor Marie Tuohy proposed the following Notice of Motion: - 
 
"That this Council request the Minister for Tourism, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sports and Media, Mr. 
Patrick O' Donovan T.D. to consider nominating Abbeyleix as the designated venue to host 
the Irish Famine Commemoration Day (IFCD) in May 2027 and that this Council support 
this effort in whatever way possible.” 
This Notice of Motin was seconded by Councillor Tommy Mulligan.  The Members were 
advised that Abbeyleix has a strong historical association with the period of the Great 
Famine and is well placed to host a commemoration of national significance. The Irish 
Famine Commemoration Day is organised nationally by the Department of Tourism, 
Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media, with the venue designated by the Minister. The 
Council will write to the Minister requesting consideration of Abbeyleix as the host 

location for the 2027 commemoration and will confirm the Council’s willingness to 
support such a proposal. 
  
The project can also be promoted by presentations at the following forums: 
 
• 
Laois Heritage Society's monthly meeting 
• 
Laois Heritage Forum. The next meeting date to be confirmed in April. 
• 
An open event at Portlaoise Library (and possibly other library branches) where 
members of the public and Local Representatives would be invited to attend and 
find out more." 
 
It was agreed that correspondence issue to the Minister for Tourism, Arts, Gaeltacht, 
Sports and Media in this regard. 
Notice of Motion No: 21/2026 
Councillor Tommy Mulligan proposed the following Notice of Motion: - 
“That Laois County calls on the Office of Public Works to facilitate the illumination of the 
Rock of Dunamaise on special occasions, in order to enhance the County’s tourism 
offering and promote this heritage site (e.g. opening of car park in August, public 
holidays)”  
 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor Catherine Fitzgerald.  It was agreed 
that correspondence issue to the OPW in this regard. 
 
Notice of Motion No: 22/2026  
 
Councillor Tommy Mulligan proposed the following Notice of Motion: - 
 
“That Laois County Council request a review of existing signage around the County where 
inaccuracies in the Irish language have been identified, with a view to correcting errors 
and promoting accurate and consistent use of Irish” 
 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor Caroline Dwane Stanely. Mr. Simon 
Walton, Director of Services advised the meeting that any issues of concern can be 
brought to the Council for attention as appropriate.  
 
Notice of Motion No: 23/2026 
 
Councillor Tommy Mulligan proposed the following Notice of Motion: - 
 
“That Laois County Council write to the Minister for Housing, Local Government and 
Heritage, Mr. James Browne, T.D. opposing private housing on the Bessborough Mother 
and Baby Home site in Cork and requesting state intervention to take control of that site”. 
This Notice of Motion was seconded by Councillor Caroline Dwane Stanley.  It was agreed 
that correspondence issue to the Minister for Housing, Local Government & Heritage in 
this regard. 
Notice of Motion No: 24/2026 
 
Councillor Caroline Dwane Stanley proposed the following Notice of Motion: - 
 

“That Laois County Council write to the Minister for Housing, Local Government and 
Heritage, Mr. James Browne, T.D. requesting him to review the Affordable Housing 
Scheme to address the needs of those whose income limit exceeds the threshold for Social 
Housing and who do not qualify for a private mortgage”. 
 
This Noice of Motion was seconded by Councillor Be Brennan.  The Members, in 
considering the Motion requested that measures to reduce costs, for example, the 
inclusion of second-hand homes, or new homes with a lower specification be considered 
for the Scheme. It was agreed that correspondence issue to the Minister for Housing, Local 
Government and Heritage in this regard.  
 
45. 
RETIREMENT OF COUNCILLOR PADRAIG FLEMING FROM LAOIS COUNTY 
COUNCIL  
Councillor Padraig Fleming addressed the Members of Laois County Council announcing 
his retirement from Laois County Council with effect from Tuesday, March 31st, 2026. Cllr. 
Fleming thanked his fellow Members and Management and staff of Laois County Council 
for their assistance to him during his time with the Council.  The Cathaoirleach and 
Members wished Councillor Fleming every good wish in his forthcoming retirement, and 
this was followed by a number of presentations to Councillor Fleming.  Mr. Michael 
Rainey, Chief Executive of Laois County Council on behalf of the Management & Staff 
thanked Councillor Fleming for his contribution to Laois County Council over the last 
seventeen years and wished him well in his retirement.  
 
This concluded the business of the meeting.
