# Commercial Licensing — Dáil Tracker

Dáil Tracker is **dual-licensed**. You can use it under either:

1. the **open-source licence** — [GNU AGPL-3.0](LICENSE), free of charge; or
2. a **commercial licence** from the author, for a fee — for cases where AGPL's
   terms don't suit your business.

This page explains which you need and how to get a commercial licence.
*Nothing here is legal advice.*

---

## Do I need a commercial licence?

**You do NOT need one** (the free AGPL licence is enough) if you:

- run Dáil Tracker unmodified, for yourself or inside your organisation;
- modify it for **internal** use that you never expose to outside users; or
- redistribute or host it **and are happy to release your modified source code
  under the AGPL**, including to anyone who uses it over a network.

**You probably DO need a commercial licence** if you want to:

- offer Dáil Tracker — or a product built on it — as a **hosted / SaaS service**
  to others, **without** publishing your modified source code;
- **bundle or embed** it inside a **closed-source / proprietary** product you
  distribute; or
- otherwise use it commercially in a way that AGPL-3.0's copyleft and §13
  network-source-disclosure requirements would prevent.

In short: **the AGPL requires that network-deployed, modified versions make their
complete corresponding source available under the AGPL.** A commercial licence
waives that obligation for you, in exchange for a fee.

## What the commercial licence grants

- The right to **use, modify, and deploy** Dáil Tracker's source code **without**
  the AGPL's source-disclosure / copyleft obligations (no requirement to publish
  your modifications).
- The right to **distribute or host** it as part of your own product or service.
- Standard commercial terms: defined support/warranty position, a liability
  framework, and clear governing law (Ireland).

It is a licence to **the software** (the ETL pipeline, parsers, SQL views, core
library, API, and Streamlit app). See the full template at
[`legal/COMMERCIAL_LICENCE_AGREEMENT_TEMPLATE.md`](legal/COMMERCIAL_LICENCE_AGREEMENT_TEMPLATE.md).

## What it does NOT grant (important)

The commercial licence covers **our code only**. It does **not** grant any rights
over the **data** Dáil Tracker ingests. Those datasets come from public bodies
(the Houses of the Oireachtas, Iris Oifigiúil, the CRO, the Charities Regulator,
SIPO, the CSO, and others) and remain under **their own licences** — see
[`NOTICE.md`](NOTICE.md). As a commercial licensee you are still responsible for
complying with each data source's licence and attribution terms independently. In
particular, **Iris Oifigiúil** material is Government of Ireland copyright and is
**not** open — we cannot sub-licence it to you.

The commercial licence also does not grant rights to the **"Dáil Tracker" name or
brand** — see [`legal/TRADEMARK_POLICY.md`](legal/TRADEMARK_POLICY.md) for brand
use, which can be included in a commercial agreement on request.

## Pricing

Commercial licences are priced per engagement. Typical structures (we'll agree
one with you):

- **Annual subscription** — a flat yearly fee per product or deployment, with
  updates and an agreed support level.
- **Revenue-share royalty** — a percentage of revenue you derive from the
  product that embeds Dáil Tracker (with audit and reporting terms).
- **Per-deployment / per-seat** — a one-off or recurring fee scaled to scale of
  use.

Pricing scales with the size and nature of your use. Non-profits, journalists,
researchers, and civic-tech projects: talk to us — the AGPL already covers most
of you for free, and we're flexible on the rest.

## Why dual licensing?

It lets the project stay **genuinely open source** — anyone can read, run, learn
from, and improve it under the AGPL — while funding continued development through
businesses that want to use it on closed/commercial terms. The open project and
the commercial offer support each other.
