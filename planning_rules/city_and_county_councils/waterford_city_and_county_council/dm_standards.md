## Plot ratio / site coverage
- General: site coverage shall not normally exceed **75%**; plot ratio shall not generally exceed **1:2** (2.0).
- Higher plot ratios (incl. ~2.0) accepted for General Business / enterprise / employment uses and Opportunity-zoned lands in urban areas.

## Separation / privacy
- Between directly opposing above-ground-floor windows: **22 m** separation generally observed for reciprocal-overlooking housing (may be reduced with design justification).
- Side garden walls generally **1.8 m**, presented behind the front building line.

## Private open space (houses)
- Minimum per Table 3.2 (by dwelling type); may be reduced for smaller houses but **not less than 50 sq.m**.

## Public open space
- Minimum **15%** of total site area.
- Institutional lands: minimum **25%** of total site area for residential development (Policy H21).

## Building lines / setbacks
- Principal road frontages (business parks etc.): building line generally **not less than 15 m** from the road, with a minimum planted strip of **5 m**.
- New development along specified protected routes: preferably not less than **25 m** from the public-road boundary fence, and in any event not less than **18 m**; along the Tramore/Waterford Road not less than **30 m**.
- On all County roads: general set-back **18 m** from the road fence.

## Sightline requirements (Table 8.1 — Minimum Sight (Y) Distance)

| Category | Speed limit | Min sight (Y) distance |
|---|---|---|
| A | 100 km/h | 215 m |
| B | 80 km/h | 160 m |
| C | 60 km/h (built-up) | 90 m |
| D | 50 km/h (built-up) | 70 m |
| E | 80 km/h (local roads) | 55 m |

- Cul-de-sac (≤3 dwellings): **30 m** sightlines.
- Set-back (X) point from nearside edge of roadway: **4.5 m** (housing estates/commercial/industrial) or **2.4 m** (single dwellings).
- Eye heights 1.05 m and 2.0 m; object heights 0.26 m and 2.0 m.

## Car parking — dimensions
- Parallel parking to kerb: 6.0 m × 2.5 m.
- Perpendicular (low-traffic roads only): 5.0 m × 2.5 m.
- Disabled parking: minimum 5% of spaces; bays minimum 6.0 m × 3.6 m (incl. 1.2 m transfer hatching).
- Loading space: min 3.7 m wide × 6 m long × 4.3 m high; max driveway opening at street boundary 6 m (min 3.6 m).
- Non-residential car-parking ratios given per use in a table (e.g. manufacturing/light industry 1 per 200 m² GFA; offices 1 per 200 m² GFA; general industrial 1 per 500 m² GFA; sports clubs 2 per court / 5 per 100 m²; stadia 1 per 15 seats + 1 coach per 500 spectators).

## EV charging
- Residential multi-unit (new & major renovation): **minimum 1 EV charge-point space per 5 car-parking spaces** (ducting for every space).
- New dwellings with in-curtilage parking: infrastructure to enable future recharging point.
- Non-residential (>10 spaces) and publicly accessible car parks: at least 1 recharging point + 1 per 5 spaces equipped.

## Waste / refuse storage
- Screened compound on site for short-term waste and segregated storage (bio-waste / dry recyclables / residual) pending collection.

## Rural one-off housing
- Rural-housing-need test per Chapter 7 / Volume 1 (Sustainable Rural Housing Guidelines 2005). A specific **minimum site area is not stated as a single figure** in Volume 2; siting is governed by the building-line setbacks and sightline table above and wastewater/percolation suitability.

## Residential density
- Volume 2 (Section 3.2) sets out general density advice (criteria-based, location-dependent) rather than fixed dwellings/ha figures; density targets are carried in Volume 1 Core Strategy / Table 3.2.
