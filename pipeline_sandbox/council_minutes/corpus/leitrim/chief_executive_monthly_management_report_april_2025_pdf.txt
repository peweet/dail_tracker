All In A Day’s Work Programmes & Associated Financial Supports 
The “All in A Day’s Work” campaign continues to be rolled out on the areas of Lean, Green and Digital supports.  
There is significant growth in demand for all three supports, in particular the Energy Efficiency Grant (EEG) and Grow 
Digital (GD) Voucher.   The EEG, with up to 75% of eligible costs up to a maximum grant of €10,000 can be supported 
following the completion of a Green for Business, Green Start or a SEAI Energy Audit.   The GD voucher is a grant 
covering 50% of eligible costs (minimum grant of €500 up to a maximum grant of €5,000) limited to new software 
subscriptions for a period of 12 and training and configuration of the software.

COMMUNITY 
 
Age Friendly Programme   
Nursing Home Community Linkage Project Roscommon & Leitrim – Pilot programme - Women's Health Funded 
project which will run for 18 months in both counties. The aim of the project is to reduce loneliness of residents in 
public & private nursing homes. The project coordinator will recruit volunteers from community groups to partner 
with residents. Information briefing sessions with community groups will take place in the Chamber on the 
20/5/2025. The coordinator will develop individual action plans with residents, introduce group activities such as 
physical and cultural programmes and improve digital skills.  
 
National Healthy Ageing Course – Deirdre Lang of HSE in contact with OPC to arrange evaluation of course content 
for a new nationwide programme the HSE will roll out to Older People. Leitrim OPC provisionally booked to meet 
Deirdre on 13th of June in Drumshanbo to review course content and incorporate OPC feedback before the 
programme is launched.   
 
Healthy Ireland 
39 volunteers have now attended training for Dementia Friendly Mohill with the HSE. Healthy Ireland along with 
Sharon Richardson (HSE) attended the Carrick Town Team meeting and have agreed that Carrick will be the next 
Dementia Friendly town. The committee are forming a subcommittee to start the process. The next town team to 
be approached will be Manorhamilton. 
 
Bressies ‘Where is my Mind’ ran in The Landmark on May 3rd in collaboration with Leitrim’s Health is Wealth. 
Cathaoirleach Cllr Paddy O'Rourke, Cllr Paddy Farrell, Deputy Frank Feighan, and Deputy Martin Kenny were in 
attendance. Stands were on site for the event from The Samaritans.  
 
Three more community events are planned for May. On May 15th Mental Health Irelands Nationwide ‘Hello! How are 
You?’ awareness day takes place and Healthy Ireland are organising ‘Tea and Tunes’ Connect Cafés. The events are 
running in The Bee Park from 1030-11.30am, and in Breffni FRC from 2.30p-3.30pm. The music circle will be 
facilitated in Breffni FRC by Kristina Kenny and in Bee Park by Hugh Feely, with the public encouraged to bring their 
own instruments or come to listen and chat. 
 
On May 23rd Mike Hanrahan from Stocktons Wing will be performing in The Mayflower Drumshanbo for our 
‘Mayflower Memories’ Age Friendly Ireland event. Mike will perform and explain the research project for his PHd – 
the topic of which is the relationship between music and memories for people with dementia. The event targets 
people living with dementia, their carers, family and friends. Sharon Richardson from the HSE will also present on 
Dementia and support available, and Philip Darcy will have his Showband Memorabilia Exhibition there on the 
evening. The event runs from 6-9pm. 
 
Co-design Hub consultation meetings ran across the county with 24 community groups and active age groups in 
attendance. The remaining series of community events and workshops will be designed based on feedback from 
the groups in attendance and an online survey for those who weren’t in attendance.  
 
Love Where You Live 
Entries for the Love Where You Live Schools Competition closed on 7th April. There was a wonderful response to this 
year’s Climate Action themed competition. Independent judge Oisín McGann along with Leitrim County Council 
staff will visit the participating primary schools in May for judging.  
 
The 2025 Love Where You Live competition for community and voluntary groups opened for entries on 9th April, 
deadline for entries is 26th May. This is a joint initiative of Leitrim Public Participation Network and Leitrim County 
Council. All community and voluntary in Leitrim are encouraged to enter. This years’ categories are  
Green Community, Green Estate, Age Friendly Community, Social Inclusion and Community Achievement. Judging 
of entries will take place in late June and the Love Where You Live Awards night will take place on 2nd October.

LIBRARY SERVICES 
 
 
 
 
There were 181 new library memberships recorded for April.  There were almost 9,500 visits to libraries across the 
county. 
Over the month all libraries were busy with the usual events such as class visits, reading groups, , art & craft sessions, 
games afternoons/evenings, bridge club etc 
Many school classes attended ‘Spring into Storytime’ Events held across the branch network.  Some outreach 
Storytime sessions were also facilitated by staff. The ‘Spring into Storytime’ initiative was run across the county 
with fun and engaging story-times being delivered to young children as part of the national Right to Read 
Programme.  
The third event in the ‘Conversations on Books’ series took place on 12th April in the Dock Arts Centre. and featured 
the book Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury. An afternoon of lively chat was facilitated by library staff. 
Cathaoirleach of Leitrim County Council, Cllr. Paddy O’Rourke launched the ‘Leitrim Gaelic Games – Reflections and 
Memories’ exhibition in Ballinamore Library on 12th April. This special event celebrated the rich history and impact 
of Gaelic Games on our communities here in Leitrim. Through the exhibition boards, various elements of Gaelic 
Games across the county were highlighted, including the Club scene, LGFA, Hurling, Handball, and Scór. Among the 
many fascinating photographs were images of the Leitrim team who won the Connacht Championship in 1927, the
