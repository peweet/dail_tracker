# Required Assessments / Triggers — Galway City Council

Source: Galway City Development Plan 2023–2029, Chapter 11 / Part B — Development Standards. Fetched 2026-06-13.
URL: https://consult.galwaycity.ie/en/consultation/draft-galway-city-development-plan-2023-2029/chapter/part-b-development-standards

The chapter enumerates the following assessment triggers:

- **Appropriate Assessment (AA) / Natura Impact Statement:** required where a plan/project may have significant adverse effects on European Sites (Natura 2000); screening determines whether Stage 2 AA is needed.
- **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR):** mandatory thresholds per Planning and Development Regulations 2001–2015; sub-threshold discretionary EIA where significant effect likely; required for major quarry/extractive operations.
- **Flood Risk Assessment (FRA):** site-specific FRA required for development in Flood Zones A or B; all development must consider surface-water flood risk; must demonstrate no increased risk to site/downstream; climate-change and residual risk incorporated.
- **Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA):** required for significant trip-generation; threshold = applications potentially employing >100 people, all schools, and residential of 100+ dwellings; modelled 5 and 15 years post-opening (per TII 2014).
- **Built Heritage Impact Assessment:** required for Protected Structures and for development in Architectural Conservation Areas (ACAs) (per DAHG Architectural Heritage Protection Guidelines 2011).
- **Ecological Impact Assessment (EcIA):** for developments in areas supporting protected species/biodiversity features.
- **Noise Assessment:** where potential for environmental noise exists.
- **Scheme Sustainability Statement:** large-scale developments (25+ homes OR >500 m² retail/commercial/office OR >1,000 m² enterprise/employment).
- **Design Statement:** residential sites over 1 hectare; sensitive sites (Protected Structures, ACAs, protected views).
- **Invasive Species Control Programme:** where invasive species present.
- **Construction & Demolition Waste Management Plan:** medium and large-scale developments.
- **Daylight / Sunlight Assessment:** may be required for impact on adjoining properties (per BS 8206-2 and BRE guide).
