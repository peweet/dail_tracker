01/171125 
Declaration of Interests 
The Cathaoirleach sought from the members any declarations of conflicts of interest 
or pecuniary or beneficial interests under Section 177 of the Local Government Act 
2001 as amended. There were no declarations. 
 
02/171125 
Draft Revenue Budget  
Members considered the Draft Revenue Budget as prepared by Chief Executive for 
the financial year ending 31 December 2026 (as circulated). 
 
Ms. Kavanagh welcomed everyone and thanked the Cathaoirleach and elected 
members for their ongoing collaboration as part of the budget process. She advised 
that members today were being asked to approve an expenditure budget of 
€284,925,772 and to note the Capital Programme 2026- 2028 (€879.920.811)  
 
A copy of the Draft Budget in the prescribed form, together with explanatory and 
comparative statements, had been circulated to the members. Section 103 of the 
Local Government Act, 2001, as amended by the Local Government Reform Act 
2014, provides that the Budget must be adopted within a period of 14 days from the 
date of the statutory Budget meeting.  
 
Ms. Hanlon informed the members that the process of preparing the Budget involved 
a number of stages including the consideration by the Finance Committee and the 
Corporate Policy Group of the broad parameters for Budget 2026 at several 
meetings from July to October.  It also involved a detailed review of income and 
expenditure headings by the Management Team. The draft budgetary plans were 
agreed at the Municipal District meetings in October.   
Ms. Hanlon provided specific details of the proposed income and expenditure across 
all the Council’s eight divisions (A-H).  
 
The total estimated expenditure for 2026 is €284,925,771 up from €260,271,147 in 
2024, an increase of €24,654,624.

03/171125 
Motion - Local Property Tax/ Pay Parking Transfer 
Motion in the name of Councillor T. Durkan was considered: 
That all unspent money generated from Local Property Tax (LPT) and paid parking 
within each of the Municipal Districts is retained for use within that Municipal District 
for future years. 
 
In the absence of Councillor T. Durkan, the motion was proposed by Councillor W. 
Durkan (as authorised in writing to Meetings Administrator in advance of the 
meeting) and seconded by Councillor T. O’Dwyer. 
 
Resolved on proposal of Councillor W. Durkan, seconded by Councillor T. O’Dwyer 
and agreement of all members present, that all unspent money generated from Local 
Property Tax and paid parking within each of the Municipal Districts is retained for 
use within that Municipal District for future years.

06/171125 
Adoption of the Annual Rate 
Resolved on the proposal of Councillor P. McEvoy, seconded by Councillor T. 
O’Dwyer and agreed by all members present that: Subject to Section 6 of the Local 
Government Rates and Other Matters Act 2019 as amended and in accordance with

07/171125 
Rates Abatement Scheme 2026 – Vacant Properties 
Members noted and considered the Chief Executive’s report on the proposed Rates 
Abatement Scheme for Vacant Properties for the financial year ending 31 December 
2026 (as circulated). 
 
Resolved on the proposal of Councillor S. Doyle, seconded by Councillor K. Duffy 
and agreed by all members present, That Kildare County Council makes a scheme 
for the abatement of rates due to it by liable persons, or classes of liable persons, in 
respect of vacant properties (as presented) in accordance with Section 9 of the Local 
Government Rates and Other Matters Act 2019, as amended. Such a scheme in 
respect of vacant property will provide for a 100% abatement of rates on vacant 
properties (with a limit of five consecutive years) as provided for in Section 9 (1) of 
the local government rates and other matters act 2019 as amended for the financial 
year ending 31 December 2026.
