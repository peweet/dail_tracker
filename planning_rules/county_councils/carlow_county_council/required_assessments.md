# Required Assessments — Carlow County Council

**Authority:** Carlow County Council
**Plan:** Carlow County Development Plan 2022-2028
**Chapter:** Chapter 16 — Development Management Standards
**Source URL:** https://consult.carlow.ie/en/consultation/carlow-county-development-plan-2022-2028/chapter/chapter-16-development-management-standards
**Fetch date:** 2026-06-13

## Appropriate Assessment (AA)
Required: All projects/plans arising from the Plan are screened for the need for Appropriate Assessment under Article 6 of the Habitats Directive. AA itself required where screening determines significant adverse effects on European sites (SACs/SPAs) cannot be ruled out.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
Required for projects listed in Part 1 of the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) where the stated threshold is met/exceeded (or where no threshold is set), and Part 2 projects exceeding specified thresholds. Sub-threshold developments require EIA screening.

## Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment
NOT explicitly enumerated as a trigger in Chapter 16. Protected structures and ACAs referenced in design/landscape context (16.5.2, 16.9.7) but no formal assessment-trigger threshold stated in this chapter.

## Flood Risk Assessment (FRA)
Required where a risk of flooding may be an issue for a development; the Planning Authority will require submission of a Site-Specific Flood Risk Assessment. (Justification Test / sequential approach not detailed numerically in this chapter.)

## Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA)
Required where a new development has significant car-trip generation potential; assessed via submission of a Traffic and Transport Assessment, with reference to TII Guidelines 2014.

## Site Suitability / Percolation (one-off rural houses)
Required for all domestic dwellings: a detailed site investigation report including percolation and water-table tests certified by a qualified person. Site assessment and wastewater treatment/percolation design must conform with the EPA Code of Practice for Domestic Waste Water Treatment (2021).
