# Required Assessments — Sligo County Council

Triggers found in this chapter. Chapter 33 is a development-management-standards chapter and is not the council's principal location for Appropriate Assessment / EIA screening policy (those triggers sit in the plan's environmental/natural-heritage chapters and the statutory SEA/AA of the plan, not extracted here). Items enumerated in Chapter 33:

## Flood Risk Assessment (33.2.6)
- Sequential approach to be followed when considering location of development in areas at risk of flooding.
- "Applicants may be required to submit a detailed, site-specific flood risk assessment." Pre-planning consultation is critical.
- Flood risk assessments to be proportionate, undertaken by a competent person (e.g. qualified hydrologist/flood-risk consultant), supported by data, and to state risk and management over the development lifetime.
- **Justification Test:** "Where required, flood risk assessments shall specify, in exceptional circumstances and where the criteria of the Justification Test have been met, the design of structural or non-structural flood risk management measures as prerequisites to development in specific areas."
- Floor-level raising specifications for areas of limited flood depth.

## Traffic & Transport Assessment / Road Safety Audit (33.9.3)
- TTA in accordance with Traffic Management Guidelines and TTA Guidelines (May 2014), plus Road Safety Audit per TII Publication Standards, where proposals generate significant additional trips. Need assessed case-by-case, ideally at pre-planning.
- TTA may be required for retail developments over 1,000 sq.m.

## Mobility Management Plans (33.9.4)
- Encouraged for employee-intensive developments likely to generate significant travel demand.

## On-Site Wastewater Site Assessment (33.2.7)
- A site assessment (site-suitability / percolation) must be carried out for all planning applications involving provision of a new (or retention of an existing) on-site wastewater treatment system, per the EPA 2021 Code of Practice (PE ≤10) and S.R. 66:2015, by a suitably qualified professional.

## Daylight / Sunlight / Overshadowing Assessment (33.2.2)
- A daylight, sunlight and overshadowing assessment may be required, in accordance with the BRE Guide 209, 2022 Edition (June 2022).

## Design Statement
- Required for all single rural house applications (except outline permission) (33.4.3).
- Required for multi-unit housing developments (contents set out in Table 33.1) (33.3).

## Biodiversity / Ecological
- Planning applications on sites of 0.5 ha and over shall demonstrate Biodiversity Net Gain, including ecological surveys (Section on BNG, [PA-190]).

## Architectural / Built Heritage
- Chapter includes a guide to new development in historic streetscapes (Table 33.6) and references to protected structures, Architectural Conservation Areas; detailed Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment / protected-structure trigger policy is located in the plan's built-heritage chapter, not within Chapter 33.

## Appropriate Assessment / EIA
- Not enumerated as explicit project-level triggers within Chapter 33. These are addressed in the plan's environmental chapters and the statutory AA/SEA accompanying the Development Plan (not part of this captured chapter).
