## Appropriate Assessment / Natura (SAC / SPA)
- Development proposals that may impact on European Sites must be screened for Appropriate Assessment and accompanied by a Natura Impact Statement (if required) and referred to NPWS (Section 12.3.1 / 12.3.2). The Council, as competent authority, carries out AA per Article 6(3) of the Habitats Directive.
- Ecological Impact Assessment required for proposals with potential to impact environmentally sensitive sites (EU/National protected sites, protected species/habitats, or proximity to significant watercourses).
- Proposals affecting a known/likely breeding or resting site for an EU Habitats Regulations species require an NPWS derogation licence (Article 16).

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
- EIAR required for classes of development above the mandatory thresholds in the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) (EIA Directive 2011/92/EU as amended by 2014/52/EU).
- Sub-threshold / discretionary EIAR may be requested where the Planning Authority considers significant environmental effects likely. ALL planning applications undergo EIA screening / preliminary assessment.

## Flood Risk Assessment / Justification Test
- Flood risk management per The Planning System and Flood Risk Management Guidelines (2009, as amended). South Dublin Strategic Flood Risk Assessment (2021), Dodder/Eastern CFRAMS inform known flood risk (Map 14).
- Development on lands that may be at flood risk: site-specific flood risk assessment by an appropriately qualified Chartered Engineer required (Section 12.x (i) Flood Risk Assessment).

## Archaeological & Architectural Heritage / Protected Structures
- Works that would materially affect the character of a Protected Structure require planning permission (Section 12.3.7 Protected Structures).
- Archaeological Heritage section (12.3.4) sets out requirements for sites of archaeological interest.
- (Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment / ACA-specific assessment requirements are handled under the heritage policy framework of Chapter 3 and these Ch12 standards.)

## Traffic & Transport Assessment (Section 12.7.2)
- Traffic Impact Statements required for all major traffic-generating development as defined by the Traffic and Transport Assessment Guidelines (2014, NRA/TII); Planning Authority may also require where significant impact on County road network.
- Traffic surveys must be within six months of application date; school-impact assessments done during term time.

## Travel Plans (Table 12.24, Section 12.7.3)
- Workplace Travel Plan thresholds: Offices/Financial >500 sq m (statement) / >2,000 sq m (standardised); Retail >600 / >2,500 sq m; Industrial >2,500 / >6,000 sq m; Warehousing >2,500 sq m; Leisure/Hospitals 25-100 jobs or >100,000 visitors/annum.
- Mobility Management Plan required for all new schools or existing schools with **25%+** classroom expansion.

## Design Statement
- Required for medium/large/complex applications: **30+ residential units**, commercial development over **1,000 sq m**, or as required by the Planning Authority (QDP8 Objective 1, Ch5) — must address the Building Height and Density Guide criteria and flag any departures.

## Site suitability / wastewater
- Not a major feature of this urban plan (predominantly mains-serviced). On-site wastewater would follow EPA Code of Practice where relevant; not enumerated as a standalone trigger table. A site suitability report in relation to waste treatment is required for rural-housing applications (Ch12 §12.6.9).
