## Residential Density (Table 13.4 — Units per Hectare)
- Key Town: Town Centre/Infill/Brownfield 35 or site-specific; Institutional Lands 35; Outer Suburban/Greenfield 25-30
- Self-Sustaining Towns / Growth Towns: Centre/Infill/Brownfield 30 or site-specific; Institutional 30; Outer Suburban/Greenfield 15-25
- Towns: Centre/Infill/Brownfield 25 or site-specific; Edge of Centre/Greenfield 25
- Small Towns & Villages: Centre/Infill/Brownfield 15 or site-specific; Edge of Centre/Greenfield 10-12
- Rural Settlements: individual housing schemes generally not larger than ~10-12 units/ha
(Source: Sustainable Residential Development in Urban Areas Guidelines, DEHLG 2009.)

## Public Open Space (DM HS 4)
- Large infill / brownfield: minimum 10% of total site area
- Greenfield: minimum 10% of total site area
- All other cases: 10% of total site area
- SuDS may take up max 10% of open-space provision

## Public Open Space provision by scale (DM HS 5)
- <25 dwellings: landscaped passive recreational area
- 26-99: active amenity open space (ball games) + passive
- 100+: passive + active + younger children's play area (min. 100 m²) OR MUGA; plus play area/ground (min. 200 m²), grass sports pitch or MUGA

## Private Open Space — Houses (DM HS 6)
- 1 and 2 bedroom: 60 sq m
- 3, 4, 5 bedroom: 75 sq m
- Located behind the building line, to rear, maximising sunlight/privacy/shelter

## Privacy / Separation (DM HS 7)
- Minimum 22 m between opposing first-floor windows at the rear of dwellings (exceptions only in exceptional high-quality cases).

## Private Amenity Space — Apartments (DM HS 7)
- Minimum depth at least 1.5 m for balconies/patios
- Studio: 4 sq m; One bedroom: 5 sq m (table continues in source)

## Car Parking (Table 10.3 — Land Use Parking Space Requirements)
- Houses: 2 per dwelling (encouraged on-site)
- Apartments/Flats: 1.25-2 per unit
- Retail: 1 per 25 m² GFA
- Offices: 1 per 20 m² GFA
- Financial Institutions: 1 per 20 m² GFA
- Retail warehousing: 1 per 35 m² GFA
- Cash & Carry: 2 per 90 m² + loading
- Industry: 1 per 60 m² or 1 per 4 employees (greater)
- Warehousing: 1 per 100 m² or 1 per 4 employees (greater)
- Hotels/B&B/Guesthouses: 1 per bedroom
- Bars & Lounges: 1 per 7 m² within speed limit / 2 per 7 m² outside
- Restaurants: 1 per table; Take away: 6 per unit
- Commercial Garage: 1 per 30 m²; Service Station: 1 per 300 m² + shop
- Hospitals: 1 per bed; Nursing homes: 1 per 2 bedrooms; Surgeries/Clinics: 2 per consulting room
- Childcare/crèche/montessori: 1 per employee + 0.25 per child
- Schools: 2 per classroom + bus circulation
(Full table in source; uses not listed determined by Planning Authority.)

## Rural One-Off Housing (DM RH 1)
- **Minimum 0.202 hectares (0.5 acres) of site area**
- **30 metres of road frontage** (unless considerable set-back exists)
- Sightlines at entrance must comply with Laois County Council Parking and Roads Standards (Chapter 10) and be achieved within the site boundary only; excessive hedgerow removal unacceptable
- On-site wastewater per EPA Code of Practice for single houses (2009) and County Laois Groundwater Protection Scheme
- Surface water per Greater Dublin Strategic Drainage Study (2005)
- Compliance with Planning System and Flood Risk Management Guidelines (DEHLG 2009)
- Ribbon development: defined as 5 or more houses on any one side of a given 250 m of road frontage (§5037 context)

## Density determination (DN 1)
- Determined with reference to SRDUA Guidelines (2009)/Urban Design Manual, Apartment Design Standards 2020, Building Heights Guidelines 2018, Core Strategy Table 2.16 & Table 13.4, prevailing scale/pattern.
