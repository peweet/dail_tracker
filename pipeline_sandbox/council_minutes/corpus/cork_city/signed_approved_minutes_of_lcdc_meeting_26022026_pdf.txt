1. Welcome and Apologies. 
Denis Leamy (Chair) welcomed the committee members.  
Apologies were noted. 
 
2. Declaration/conflict of interest. 
The chair reminded the Committee of the need to declare any conflicts of interest. Niall 
O’Keeffe and Cllr. Kenneth Collins declared conflict of interest regarding the SICAP Annual 
Plan 2026 and End of Year 2025 review (item 10 on the agenda).  
 
Presentation: 
The chairperson welcomed Denise Cahill, (Healthy Cities Coordinator), Sandrine Bertin, 
(Food Policy Development Worker), and Janas Harrington (UCC) to present Cork City’s Food 
Strategy to the committee members. 
Denise Cahill led the presentation and then opened it to Sandrine Bertin and Janas Harrington 
and they both presented their work on Cork Food Strategy: “From Consultation to Action. 
Building a Thriving Food System for Cork City”.  
The LCDC members acknowledged the presentation, congratulated Denise C, Sandrine, and 
Janas on their presentation. 
The chair gave the committee an opportunity to ask questions after the presentation.

3. Draft Minutes of LCDC Meeting on 29th January 2026. 
Minutes of the LCDC Meeting on 29th January 2026 were approved pending the agreed 
amendments. 
Proposed by: Denis J. Fitzpatrick. 
Seconded by: Martin Davoren. 
 
4. Matters Arising. 
KH raised a query regarding the LCDC’s role and function in relation to Sláitecare Healthy 
Communities, and Healthy Ireland Fund. MD will furnish an overview report to LCDC 
members detailing service level agreement and funding models of Sláintecare and Healthy 
Ireland.  
 
5. LCDC Membership. 
Denis Leamy, Chairperson has written to Chief Superintendent Colm Noonan to join the 
LCDC. The Cork City Public Participation Network plenary will take place in June; nominations 
will be sought to select another PPN representative for the LCDC.   
 
6. For Information. 
a) Cork City Council Community Development Grants. 
MD gave an update to the committee. The Community Development Grants applications 
are now closed. The Community Team are working on processing the applications. 
b) LCDC Local Enhancement Programme Grants. 
MD updated the committee that the LCDC LEP Grant 2026 is a Capital Fund. The deadline 
for applications has been extended to 05/03/2026.  
c) LCDC Communications Campaign – Ministerial Launch on 1st May 2026. 
MD updated the committee that the national campaign to promote the work of the LCDC 
will launch on the 1st May 2026.  
Members were asked to keep this date free on their diaries. 
7. PPN Report. 
KI presented the PPN Report to the LCDC members and the current update of the work of the 
PPN to date.
