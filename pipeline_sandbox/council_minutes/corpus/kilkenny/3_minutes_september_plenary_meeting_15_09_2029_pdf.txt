As Cathaoirleach of Kilkenny County Council, representing all the Elected Members, 
Kilkenny County Council unequivocally condemns the recent racist attacks targeting 
members of the Indian community, across our Country.  These acts of hatred and 
intolerance have no place in our society and stand in direct opposition to the values we 
hold as a community and as a Local Authority. 
 
We extend our solidarity and support to all those affected, and we reaffirm our 
commitment to fostering a safe, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone 
regardless of ethnicity, nationality, or background. 
 
Racism, in any form, whether directed at individuals or communities, is unacceptable 
and must be challenged wherever it arises. 
 
Kilkenny is a County enriched by the diversity of its people.  We proudly welcome 
individuals and families from all over the world who choose to make Kilkenny their 
home. Their contributions, social, cultural, and economic have enriched the fabric of 
our community and our economy and help build a more vibrant and compassionate 
society. 
 
As a Local Authority, we are guided by a values system rooted in inclusion, dignity, 
and mutual respect. We remain committed to working with residents, community 
groups, and organisations to promote understanding, celebrate diversity, and ensure 
that Kilkenny remains a place where everyone feels safe, valued, and empowered to 
thrive. 
 
Together, we stand against racism and bigotry to build a better Kilkenny.”

Tuarascáil Iniúchta Rialtais Áitiúil agus Ráiteas Airgeadais Bliantúil 
Local Government Audit Report and Annual Financial Statement 
Mr. Martin Prendiville advised the meeting that the Audit for the Year 2024 had 
now been completed by the Local Government Auditor and was circulated to 
the Members.  There was no change to the Annual Financial Statement [AFS] 
following the audit. 
 
Martin Prendiville confirmed that the expenditure for the year was €312.4m 
which was €21.3m higher than the adopted budget.  The surplus for the year 
was €31,775.  Mr. Prendiville referred Members to Page 24 of the AFS which 
outlined details of the expenditure variances for each Service Area along with 
the relevant income streams.

Mr. Prendiville also presented the Local Government Auditors Report to the 
Members.  He outlined the topics reported on by the Auditor and the replies 
provided by Management on those topics. 
 
Contributions received from Cllrs. Maria Dollard, Eugene McGuinness, Seán Ó 
hArgáin, Pat Fitzpatrick & John Brennan in relation to the following: 
 
▪ 
Loan Financing. 
▪ 
Funding of the Capital Account. 
▪ 
Delays on the PLEEP Project and implications of same.

to be identified in advance of committing to a Project.  He advised that some 
loan finance would be drawn down before the year end to fund the PLEEP 
expenditure and for match funding for other capital projects.  The Members

authority on this project for a total of nine counties.  A Section 85 Agreement 
is in place with the other eight Local Authorities. 
 
Delays have been encountered and Kilkenny County Council is working 
towards completion of the project in a timely manner.
