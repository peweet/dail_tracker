## Residential Density
- "the number of units to be provided on a site should be determined with reference to the Guidelines for Planning Authorities on 'Sustainable Residential Development in Urban Areas' or any subsequent update." The Planning Authority "may use its discretion in varying these maximum density standards to take account of the character and context of respective settlements." (No fixed dwellings-per-hectare table in chapter.)

## Public / Communal Open Space
- "Communal (public or shared) open space should... comprise of 15% of gross site area for green field sites and 10% for brown field sites." Narrow/incidental strips excluded from the calculation.

## Private Open Space / Separation Distances
- Chapter 12 does not state numeric private-open-space areas or building-to-building separation/privacy distances; apartments designed per Sustainable Urban Housing: Design Standards for New Apartments Guidelines; residential per Sustainable Residential Development in Urban Areas Guidelines. (No numeric m² / metre values in chapter.)

## Childcare
- "Child care facilities shall be provided at the rate of one facility per 75 residential units" (per Childcare Facilities Guidelines 2001).

## Site Coverage / Plot Ratio / Building Height
- Site coverage "should not exceed **75%** for industrial or retail developments. **80%** site coverage may be permissible in town centre locations."
- (No numeric plot ratio or building-height table in chapter.)

## Rural One-Off Housing (12.7 etc.)
- Rural house design per the County Roscommon Rural Housing Design guidance and rural clusters configured to reflect traditional patterns of rural development. **No numeric minimum rural site area stated in this chapter** — rural site viability governed by wastewater site assessment (below).

## Wastewater (12.12) — rural site suitability
- Serviced lands: connect to mains foul sewer where available; CCTV analysis of new sewer lines before taking in charge.
- Non-serviced lands: individual systems (septic tank + percolation area, or patent effluent treatment system); "Site specific analysis for new systems and operating capacity of existing" systems required (i.e. percolation / site-suitability testing).
- Granny flats: required separation distances from wastewater treatment systems must be achieved.

## Car Parking Standards (Table 12.1, minimum spaces) — selected
- Residential (1-3 bedrooms): 1.5 per unit.
- Residential (4+ bedrooms): 2 per unit.
- Visitor parking (residential): 1 for every three dwelling units.
- Guest House/B&B: 1 per bedroom; Hotel: 1 per bedroom; Hostel: 1 per bedroom or 1 per 10-bed dormitory.
- Shops <250 m² gross: 1 per 20 sq.m; 250-1000 m²: 1 per 14 sq.m; >1000 m²: 1 per 10 sq.m.
- Offices (Town Centre / Office Park): 1 per 50 sq.m.
- Public Houses/Restaurants/Lounge Bars: 1 per 10 sq.m net floor area.
- Hot Food Take Aways: minimum 5 spaces per unit or 1 per 10 sq.m retail space.
- Hospitals/Nursing homes: 1.5 per bed; Surgeries: 2 per consultant's room.
- Manufacturing/Light Industrial: 1 per 50 sq.m; Warehouses: 1 per 100 sq.m.
- Petrol Filling Station: 2 per pump (plus other uses).
- Childcare: 1 per staff member + 1 per 4 children.
- Primary/Secondary school: 1.5 per staff member (secondary +1 per 10 students over 17).
- Reduction in car parking provision considered in town-centre areas / with achievable smarter-travel plan.

## Bicycle Parking Standards (Table 12.2, minimum) — selected
- Dwelling houses and flats: 1 unit per dwelling.
- Shops / Supermarkets & large stores: 1 unit per 200 sq.m GFA.
- Offices / Industry: 1 stand per 500 sq.m GFA.
- Warehousing: 1 stand per 1000 sq.m GFA.
- Hotels/guest houses: 1 stand per 50 bedrooms.
- Playing fields: 4 stands per pitch; Schools: 1 stand per 50 pupils.

## EV Charging Point Standards (Table 12.3)
- Residential multi-unit (new + major renovations) with private/visitor spaces: minimum **1 EV charge point space per five car parking spaces**; ducting for every parking space.
- New dwellings with in-curtilage parking: install infrastructure to enable an EV recharging point.
- Non-residential with >10 private spaces (e.g. office): at least 1 recharging point; min 1 space per five equipped with a functional EV charging point.
- Publicly accessible spaces (e.g. supermarket, cinema): at least 1 recharging point; min 1 per five equipped.
- Rapid charging points should be in proximity to building entrance points.
