## Appropriate Assessment (AA)
Required under the EU Habitats Directive: any plan or project with potential to impact the integrity of a European site (SAC/SPA) must be Screened for Appropriate Assessment. If screening indicates a likely significant effect (alone or in-combination), a Natura Impact Statement (NIS) / Natura Impact Report (NIR) must be prepared. Priority habitat/species adverse impacts can only proceed under IROPI (Article 6(4)) with compensatory measures. Early consultation with the Council / NPWS advised where a site is on or near a European site.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
An EIAR is required with a planning application where the thresholds in Schedule 5 of the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) are exceeded. For sub-threshold development, an EIAR must be submitted where the Planning Authority considers (having regard to Schedule 7) significant environmental effects are likely. Screening Determination and Scoping Opinion may be requested. EIA report must be prepared by competent experts. (Derives from EIA Directive 2014/52/EU amending 2011/92/EU.)

## Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment
Not set out as a discrete numeric trigger in Appendix 1; protected structures, ACAs and archaeological heritage are addressed in Chapter 16 (Architectural and Cultural Heritage). Assessment of impact on protected structures/ACAs is required as a qualitative consideration within development management.

## Flood Risk Assessment (FRA) / Justification Test
- The plan is informed by a Strategic Flood Risk Assessment (Volume 10c).
- Development proposals must have regard to the SFRA and the Planning System and Flood Risk Management Guidelines (DoEHLG/OPW 2009) and Circular PL2/2014; where required, supported by an appropriately detailed hydrological / flood risk assessment.
- FRAs must consider climate-change impacts (sea-level rise, coastal erosion).
- The sequential approach and Justification Test apply per the Guidelines for development in flood-risk zones.

## Traffic / Transport Assessment & Road Safety
- Housing developments of 10 units or more require a Transport and Mobility Statement (A1.6.1).
- Traffic Impact Assessments (TIA), Road Safety Audits and Road Safety Impact Assessments (RSIA) referenced (A1.6.2) per TII publications; required for relevant developments.

## Site Suitability / Percolation (one-off rural houses)
On-site effluent treatment assessed per EPA Code of Practice: Domestic Waste Water Treatment Systems (p.e. <10) 2021. Minimum rural dwelling site area 0.2 ha (0.5 acres). Site suitability/percolation assessment required for unsewered single-house proposals.

## Other
- Tourism infrastructure outside settlements: must include assessment of environmental sensitivities — EIA, AA and SFRA if required.
- Petrol filling station associated shops >100 m² net: sequential approach applies.
