Assessment triggers enumerated in the chapter (quoted, no inference):

## Appropriate Assessment (AA)
- Appropriate Assessment Screening Report and, where applicable, a **Natura Impact Statement** required.
- For relevant developments, additional supporting surveys cited: ornithological (bird) survey (winter and breeding) for at least 2 years prior to application; assessment of carbon balance of the proposed development.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
- An **Environmental Impact Assessment Report (EIAR)** (including any remedial EIAR required by statute) should ensure all impacts on heritage, environment, biodiversity, groundwater protection etc. are clearly addressed with appropriate mitigation.

## Architectural Heritage Assessment
- Where deemed necessary, the Planning Authority may require an **Architectural Heritage Assessment report**, prepared by a qualified and experienced conservation architect as described in Appendix B of the DEHLG Architectural Heritage Protection Guidelines for Planning Authorities.

## Flood Risk and other case-by-case assessments
- The chapter lists assessments decided on a case-by-case basis with the Planning Authority: **Flood Risk Assessment, Landscape Impact Assessment, Archaeological Assessment, Architectural Assessment, Ecological Impact Assessment** (Ecology/Biodiversity — including e.g. bat surveys).

## Site suitability / wastewater (rural)
- For non-serviced rural sites, the site must accommodate a wastewater treatment system with designed minimum separation distance in accordance with the EPA Code of Practice: Domestic Waste Water Treatment Systems (2021) — implying site-suitability/percolation assessment, though the words "percolation test" / "Justification Test" are not used verbatim in the captured text.
