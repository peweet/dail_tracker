Cllr Harley the outgoing Cathaoirleach thanked the Chief 
Executive, the Council Executive and staff for their assistance 
and co-operation over the past year.  
 
 
He said that it had been an honour and privilege to serve the 
people of Donegal. He alluded to a number of particular 
highlights including his attendance at the ceremony in London to 
accept the Lonely Planet award for Donegal. This, he 
contended, was testament to the hard work and efforts of the 
tourism team. The fact that Donegal had been chosen as the 
 
host destination for TBEX (Travel Blog Exchange) 2025, the world’s 
largest conference and community of travel creators and 
influencers reflected the level of commitment that had been 
directed towards the growth of the tourism industry in the county.  

 
 He acknowledged also the level of investment in Roads and 
Greenways over the past number of years. 
 
 
He said that it had been a particular privilege to attend the 50th 
anniversary celebrations for the 28th Infantry Battalion in Finner. 
 
Other notable events attended included the, 
 
• 
Meet the Maker and the Disrupt by the Sea Events. 
• 
Tip O’ Neill Awards 
• 
London Irish Vintage Day Diaspora Event 
• 
25th Anniversary of the Island of Ireland Peace Park and its 
designation as a world heritage site. 
 
He said that he had been especially honoured to host the Freedom 
of the County Award Ceremonies for Clannad and Tim O’ Kelly. 
 
Concluding he thanked his wife Denise and family circle for their 
support and co-operation over the past year. It had been, he said, a 
very special year and he thanked members, and staff for their 
assistance. 
 
Cllr C Brogan thanked him for his dedication and service and paid 
tribute to the level of co-operation that had been shown to all the 
political groupings.  
 
Cllr M Farren said that he had done an excellent job and should be 
proud of his achievements. 
 
Cllr G Doherty said that he had been an excellent ambassador for 
Donegal and had done the county proud. He also thanked the Leas 
Cathaoirleach for his great work in 2023/2024 and acknowledged 
the good working relationship that had existed for both the 
Cathaoirleach and the Leas Cathaoirleach. 
 
Continuing he congratulated all the new members and those that 
had been re-elected. All, he said, had been given a clear mandate 
and the onus now was on each member to do their very best. He 
acknowledged all the staff in Donegal County Council and the 
excellent working relationship that existed. 
 
At this juncture, he extended congratulations to Seán O’ Daimhin, 
Rannog na Gaeltachta on his retirement and his immense 
contribution to both Donegal County Council and to the Irish 
language. 
 
 
Cllr Kavanagh said that the Cathaoirleach had represented the 
county with honour both at home and abroad. He also 
acknowledged the contribution of the Leas Cathaoirleach.  
 
 
Cllr Mc Garvey said that the Cathaoirleach had served both with 
dignity and pride and had done the role of Cathaoirleach proud. 
 
 
Cllr Mc Bride said that it had been a job well done and that the 
Cathaoirleach had represented the county in an excellent manner. 

He was, he said, looking forward to working with all members in a 
fair and equitable manner. 
 
The Chief Executive welcomed the new members and those 
returning to the chamber. On behalf of the staff and the senior 
management team he assured members that they would have all 
relevant support and assistance over the next five years. 
 
Addressing the Cathaoirleach, he said, that it had been a privilege 
to work with him and thanked him for his courtesy and 
professionalism, noting that he had carried out the role with great 
dignity and respect. His integrity and good humour had been a 
valuable asset, he noted. He paid tribute also to the significant 
contribution made by the leas Cathaoirleach. 
 
Cllr Harley then invited nominations for the office of Cathaoirleach. 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty on a point of order said that he was objecting to 
Standing Orders as in his opinion they were unconstitutional. 
 
Cllr Mc Garvey proposed, seconded by Cllr M Farren that the 
position be filled by Cllr Niamh Kennedy. She had, he contended, 
been successfully elected for a third term and would be the first 
female independent councillor to take the chair of Donegal County 
Council. She was, he contended, an experienced local 
representative, hard- working and would represent the people of 
Donegal in an exemplary manner. 
 
 
The Cathoirleach asked if there were any other nominations.  
 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty, seconded by Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig proposed Cllr

Cllr Kennedy said that she was delighted to accept the 
nomination and would strive to maintain a good working 
relationship with all members, so that the best possible outcome 
could be achieved for the people of Donegal.  

 
She thanked her family, friends and supporters who had helped 
and assisted her through what had been a tough election 
process.  
 
She extended congratulations to all the elected members on 
their success, noting that all had done their respective 
communities proud and thanked those who had not returned to 
the chamber for their service and dedication.  
 
It was and honour, she said, to be the first female independent 
Cathaoirleach and the 4th woman in 125 years to serve as 
Cathaoirleach of Donegal County Council. 
 
There were many priorities, she advised, going forward with 
tourism a key player in the economic development of the county. 
Times had changed, she added, and Donegal was now home to 
many different communities, all of whom required 
representation. 
 
She thanked Cllr Harley for his tremendous work and the dignity 
in which he had he had carried out the role of Cathaoirleach.

C/156/24 
ELECTION OF LEAS CATHAOIRLEACH   
 
Cllr Harley proposed, seconded by Cllr Boyle that Cllr Jimmy 
Kavanagh be appointed to the position. He said that Cllr 
Kavanagh had been a fine ambassador for Letterkenny, and a 
capable and dedicated councillor who would represent the 
Council in a competent and dignified manner.  
 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty proposed Cllr Joy Beard for the position but did 
not have a seconder for his proposal. 
 
 
As there were no other proposals Cllr Jimmy Kavanagh was 
elected Leas Cathaoirleach for 2024-2025. 
 
 
Cllr Mc Bride congratulated Cllrs Kennedy and Kavanagh on 
their appointments. He said that the Cathaoirleach had alluded 
to the fact that this was an all-inclusive Council, yet the number 
of positions allocated to a number of the independent members 
had not been dealt with in a fair and equitable manner. He said 
that at no time had he or a number of others been allowed to 
negotiate or take part in the selection process. This, he 
contended, was not a good start and cited the need to sit and 
renegotiate same. This, he contended, was a slight to the 
electorate who had voted for members concerned. 
 

 
Cllr Brogan congratulated the Cathaoirleach and said that she 
had the full support of the Fianna Fáil grouping and cited the 
need to move forward in harmony as a team effort was required 
to met the challenges that lay ahead.  
 
 
Cllr G Doherty congratulated both on behalf of the Sinn Fein 
grouping and said that his party were willing to work with all. He 
said that the positions had been allocated based on the D’Hondt 
principles and that the independent block had received their fair 
share. He said that he could not comment on any internal 
wrangling withing the independent grouping. 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty contended that the 100% Redress Party and a 
number of other independents had been advised not to do 
business with himself or Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig on the basis 
that they had both made allegations of corruption during the 
previous council term. He alluded to the fact that two thirds of 
the electorate in Donegal had voted for the independent 
grouping and that this was a disservice to the electorate who 
had clearly given them a huge mandate. 
 
Cllr Mc Bride proposed, seconded by Cllr Mc Brearty that the 
meeting be adjourned in light of same. Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig 
endorsed these comments and said that it was imperative that 
the mandate given by the electorate was endorsed. 
 
Cllr Farren on behalf of the Labour Party, Cllr Jordan on behalf 
of the Donegal MD and Cllr Harley on behalf of Fine Gael 
together with Cllr Mc Clafferty congratulated and the 
Cathaoirleach and the Leas Cathaoirleach and wished them well 
over the coming year.  
 
Cllr J Brogan offered his congratulations but stated that he was 
not aware of being represented in talks by anybody and 
supported Cllr Mc Bride’s called for an adjournment as the filling 
of the positions had not been fairly negotiated.  
Cllr Kavanagh thanked members for their support and said that 
the selection of Cllr Kennedy as Cathaoirleach had balanced a 
little the gender bias on the Council. He said he was honoured 
and proud to accept the position and would strive to carry out the 
role to the best of his ability. 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty asked for a copy of the power sharing 
agreement. 
 
The Chief Executive congratulated Cllrs Kennedy and Cllr 
Kavanagh, noting that they were both well experienced 
councillors and assured them that they would have the support 
of the executive who would work with them on their respective 
priorities.  
 
The Meetings Administrator reminded members that Paragraph 
18, Schedule 10 of the Local Government Act, 2001 outlined the 
right to form groupings for certain appointments and outlined 

details in relation to same. He said that there was now a 
proposal to adjourn, proposed by Cllr Mc Bride and seconded by 
Cllr Mc Brearty. Cllr Mac Giolla Easbuig further endorsed this 
proposal. 
 
A show of hands was taken by the Meetings Administrator with 
members unanimously agreeing to continue the meeting.

Cllr Meehan said that he was of the belief that the Council was

under the D,Hondt system discounting those members that were 
not involved in the negotiations. He asked that the names of

Cllr G Doherty indicated that this was a matter for the

said that in doing so he had isolated the five others who joined 

the negotiations and worked with the various other political 
groupings. 
 
A heated exchange then occurred and the Cathaoirleach 
adjourned the meeting until 2pm.

C/214/24 
ADVISORY NOTE ON SPECIFIC OBLIGATIONS ON 
ELECTED MEMBERS   
 
The Meetings Administrator drew Members attention to their 
obligations under the Local Elections (Disclosure of Donations 
and expenditure) Act, 1999, as amended and the necessity on 
members and unsuccessful candidates to furnish returns of 
election expenditure incurred and donations received , where 
appropriate by the 5th September, 2024. 
 
 
He further advised in relation to the obligation on new Members 
to furnish an Ethics Declaration of Interest by the 26th July, 
2024. 
 
 
Members were informed also of their obligations under Section 
141 of the Local Government Act, 2001(as amended) and the 
duty of Members appointed to other bodies to present a report 
on the activities and operation of that body.  
 
The Meetings Administrator noted that there was an onus on 
Members to comply with the requirements of Section 141(a) of 
the Local Government Act, 2001 and Section 53(1) of the Local 
Government Reform Act, 2014. This it was noted placed an 
onus on Members to notify the Authority of their attendance at 
meetings of all bodies to which they had been nominated by the 
Authority and to submit within 15 working days a written report 
on all authorised attendance at conferences and events. 
 
 
Members were informed that the details of the new guidelines in 
relation to the establishment of Strategic Policy Committees had 
just issued and that proposals would be brought to the Corporate 
Policy Group and members in due course.

housing crisis.

Meetings continued.
