## Residential Density (11.5.x)
- Density set by reference to the Sustainable Residential Development and Compact Settlements Guidelines 2024: densities at/above the mid-density range at central/accessible locations, closer to mid-range at intermediate, below mid-range at peripheral locations. SPPR 1 of the Urban Development and Building Heights Guidelines (2018) also applies. (No fixed uph table in chapter.)

## Plot Ratio (11.5.4, DM OBJ 15)
- "the indicative maximum plot ratio standard shall be 1.0 for housing at edge of town locations with an indicative maximum plot ratio of 2.0 in town centre/core locations."
- Max 50% of any development on C1-zoned lands can be residential; max 30% on B1-zoned lands.

## Site Coverage (11.5.5, DM OBJ 16)
- "Site coverage shall generally not exceed 80%." Higher permissible in limited circumstances (public transport corridors, regeneration areas, mixed residential/commercial).

## Building Setbacks (11.5.6, DM OBJ 17)
- Setbacks sought along Motorways, National Primary, National Secondary, Regional and Local Roads to allow for future road improvements (no fixed metric).

## Separation / Privacy Distances (11.5.7)
- DM OBJ 18: "A minimum of 16 metres separation between directly opposing rear or side windows above ground floor level" (detached, semi-detached, terraced units).
- DM OBJ 19: "A minimum of 16 metres separation distance between opposing rear or side windows" (apartments/duplex up to three storeys).
- DM OBJ 21: "A minimum distance of 2.3 metres shall be provided between dwellings for the full length of the flanks" (detached, semi-detached, end-of-terrace).
- Proposals exceeding three storeys must demonstrate adequate separation (DM OBJ 20).

## Building Height (11.5.9)
- Increased building height locations identified: Dunboyne Central & Pace rail stations, Maynooth Environs, Drogheda Environs, Navan, proposed Navan rail station.
- "all proposals for buildings in excess of 6 stories at these locations shall be accompanied by a statement demonstrating compliance with the Urban Development and Building Heights Guidelines (2018)."

## Public Open Space (11.5.11)
- DM OBJ 26: "Public open space shall be provided for residential development at a minimum rate of 15% of total site area" (F1, G1, H1 zoned lands excluded from the 15%).
- DM OBJ 27: Standalone developments of 9 residential units or less exempt from the 15% requirement (private amenity space must then exceed minimum).
- Reduction permitted to "not less than a minimum of 10% of net site area" in exceptional circumstances (via Social Infrastructure Assessment), or financial contribution in lieu (s.48).

## Private Open Space — Houses (Table 11.1)
| House | Minimum private open space | Max semi-private (in lieu) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 bed house | 20 sq.m | 10 sq.m |
| 2 bed house | 30 sq.m | 15 sq.m |
| 3 bed house | 40 sq.m | 20 sq.m |
| 4 bed house | 50 sq.m | 25 sq.m |
- Further reduction acceptable where high-quality semi-private space provided in lieu, subject to ≥50% of area being private open space.

## Car Parking (Section 9, Table 11.2, DM OBJ 89) — selected
- Dwellings/Apartments: accessible locations max 1.5 per dwelling/unit; intermediate & peripheral locations max 2 per dwelling/unit.
- Hotel Accommodation: 1 per bedroom; B&B/Guesthouse: 1 per bedroom; Hostel: 1 per bedroom or 1 per 10 beds.
- Manufacturing Industry: 1 per 50 sq.m GFA; Warehousing: 1 per 100 sq.m GFA; Offices: 1 per 25 sq.m GFA (1 per 50 sq.m above 1,500 sq.m).
- Food Retail: 1 per 20 sq.m GFA (1 per 14 sq.m above 1,000 sq.m); Non-Food Retail: 1 per 20 sq.m; Retail Warehousing: 1 per 50 sq.m; Banks: 1 per 20 sq.m.
- Restaurants: 1 per 5 sq.m dining area; Bars/Night Clubs/Lounges/Function Rooms: 1 per 4 sq.m public area.
- Retail outlets within vehicle service garages: 1 HGV space per 30 sq.m net floor area where food is served.
- Hospitals: 1 per bed; Surgeries: 2 per consulting room; Nursing Homes: 1 per 3 beds + 1 per employee.
- Schools: 1 per employee + set-down + 1 per 4 children (set-down).
- Cinemas/Theatre: 1 per 3 seats (1 per 5 seats above 1,000 sq.m); Funeral Homes: 1 space per plot.
- Sport Clubs (pools/courts/gyms): 2 per court, 5 per 100 sq.m; Playing pitches: 15 spaces per pitch.
- Accessible parking: minimum 5% of total spaces for developments requiring >10 spaces (min 1 space).
- Non-residential standards are "maxima"; reductions for living-over-the-shop, town-centre, good-transport locations.

## Cycle Parking (Table 11.4, DM OBJ 96/98) — selected
- Residential: minimum 1 private secure bicycle space per bed space; 1 visitor space per two housing units.
- Retail/shops: 1 space per 10 car spaces or 1 per till/checkout (whichever greater).
- Offices: 10% of employee numbers (min 10), or 1 per car space (whichever greater).
- Schools: 25% of pupil registration / min 10 (plus separate teacher parking).
- Other developments: 1 bike space per car space, or 10% of employee numbers.
- Park & Ride: 10% of total car spaces / min 50 spaces (sheltered).

## EV Charging (11.9.2)
- DM OBJ 94: All car parks shall include wiring and ducting capable of accommodating future EV charging points "at a rate of 20% of total space numbers."
- DM OBJ 95: In any publicly accessible car park in excess of 20 spaces, four fully functional EV charging points shall be provided (per IEC 61851).
- DM OBJ 24: EV charging points required to serve residential development.
- EV charging points required with every new/extended service station.

## Sightlines / Rural One-Off House Site Area
- **Not in Chapter 11.** Sight-distance/visibility splay 'x'/'y' values and rural one-off-house minimum site area are addressed in Chapter 9 (Rural Development Strategy) and via TII publications — not captured in this chapter. (Rural development standards explicitly cross-referenced to Chapter 9.)
