1. Land Disposals 
 
P. Johnston, A/DoS outlined details of the disposal of the former Bank of Ireland, Lismore to 
Lismore Town Association.  
 
Proposed by Cllr. Doocey and seconded by Cllr. Pratt and agreed by all.  
 
Cllr. McGuinness said he supported the disposal saying there is a need for accommodation of 
this type in the area. He said initially there was discussions about social housing for this site 
and said there is a real need for social housing in Lismore and surrounding areas and asked for 
a commitment from the executive to provide social units in the area.  
 
 

P. Johnston, A/DoS then outlined details of the disposal of the old Railway Station, 
Kilmacthomas to Kilmacthomas Social Enterprise CLG.  
 
Proposed by Cllr. S. Power  and seconded by Cllr. Brazil and agreed by all. 
 
Cllrs. Phelan, O’Leary and Geoghegan all spoke in support of the disposal. 
 
2. Finance (i) Annual Financial Statement AFS (ii) Section 104 
 
B. Pollard, Head of Finance presented the 2023 Annual Financial Statement. He noted the 
increased activity in 2023, the continued improvement in rate collection and the continued 
improvement in the area of loans. He noted the Revenue surplus on the Income and Expenditure 
Account for the year  compared to a debit balance in 2022. The credit balance on the Revenue 
Account  as at 31/12/2023 is €159,080 compared to a debit balance of €1,271,105 as at 
31/12/2022. The Capital Account shows a credit balance of €14,590,489 at the year end. He 
requested the Members approval to the Section 104 Local Government Act 2001 -  Statement 
of Additional Expenditure re 2023 Annual Financial Statement by Division. 
 
Proposed by Cllr. Barron and seconded by Cllr. Geoghegan and agreed by all. 
 
Cllrs. Ryan, S. Power, Phelan, Nugent, Murphy, Kiely, Kelly, Geoghegan, Doocey, Bailey, 
Pratt, Hearne, Daniels, Cronin, Fitzgerald, Keating and Barry all spoke to welcome the surplus. 
Suggestions as to how that surplus might best be spent included spend on roads and footpaths, 
replacement windows and doors, a dehumidifier pilot scheme, funding grants for those 
residents’ associations who missed the application deadline in 2023 and reducing social rents. 
 
B. Pollard, Head of Finance noted the Revenue surplus of €1, 430,185 cleared the 2022 deficit 
of €1,271,105 leaving a surplus of €159,080 which he said  although not insignificant was not 
huge. He said primarily it is good for our reputation particularly at Government level  that we 
are no longer in deficit.  
 
The question of allocating monies to those residents’ associations who missed out on funding 
this year because of missing the application deadline was raised again with I. Grimes, DoS  
saying there is no surplus in the Community Budget to make additional payments. 
 
M. Walsh, CE said the surplus relates to expenditure in 2023 noting that the Community 
funding relates to expenditure in  2024. The Revenue surplus is not money available to spend 
in accounting terms but it eliminates our deficit.
