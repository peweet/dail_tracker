C/434/24 
HOUSING CAPITAL UPDATE  
Mr John Gallagher, Senior Engineer, provided an update on the 
progress made from the 11th November to the 9th December 2024 on 

4 
 
the various Housing Capital projects county-wide. This included an 
update on turnkey acquisitions, developments in in-house design and 
construction including design and build options and the Letterkenny 
Flagship Projects and an overview of the land acquisition programme. 
 
Members welcomed the presentation and a lengthy debate ensued 
with the following issues highlighted.  
 
Issues  
• Update requested in relation to the Kiltoy site, and an indication 
provided as to when the houses will be allocated. Issues need to 
be sorted as a matter of urgency. 
• Ballymacool residents need to be kept in the loop regarding 
developments on the site and consulted on a regular basis going 
forward. Commitment needed that water upgrade works will be 
progressed.  
• Concern that it is only when a development goes forward for 
planning that the need for water/sewerage upgrade is flagged. 
• Full update requested on the road plan for Meadowhill, Raphoe 
so that the existing residents can be updated. 
• Members emails reading defective concrete blocks issues 
needed to be responded to and feedback provided in relation to 
the associated technical issues.  
• Archaeological Assessment an issue as there was a belief that 
these were only required in respect of the Historical Towns 
identified in the County Development plan.  
• Concern with regard to the Air to Water heating systems when 
the electricity supply is off for a prolonged period. Has 
implications for the elderly and young families. 
• Query regarding the 56 units in Carndonagh and whether Uisce 
Éireann are still of the opinion that there are capacity isues here.  
• Noted that the Approved Housing Bodies had not been listed as 
showing any interest in providing accommodation in Inishowen.  
• High level of need in the Milford Electoral Area and proposed 
projects will only meet 18% of the overall housing need. 
• Lack of water/sewerage facilities hindering social housing 
development in the Milford Electoral Area which is presently 
lagging in terms of housing provision.  
• Need to upgrade Treatment Plants in the Milford area as there 
are problems when power outages occur.  
• Increased emphasis on turnkey provision required. 
• Consideration warranted in terms of lands within the control 
points at Churchill which could be utilised for housing delivery. 
• Sites in smaller towns and villages need to be looked at in terms 
of turnkey provision. 
• Need for swift engagement with contactors in terms of suitable 
sites. 
• S.I policy needs a revamp and the overall process speeded up.  

5 
 
• Concern that Uisce Éireann are hampering development in 
Carndonagh through lack of service provision and that water is 
being pumped out of Inishowen thus reducing capacity and 
hindering development in the Peninsula.  
• Issue identified with the new Housing Adaptation Grant form in 
that there is no provision for fossil fuel options. 
• Issues with water supply in Gort na Greine and Glentain must be 
sorted urgently. Dedicated contact number needed for Uisce 
Éireann so that when problems arise, they can be sorted in a 
speedy manner. 
• Development being held up in Ramelton where specific sites 
have been identified but water and sewerage capacity the 
stumbling block. 
• Issues with the pump at Coolcholly Pumping Station – query as 
to whether the issue is with the pump or are the problems 
related to the existing mains connection. 
• Greater momentum required in terms of buying houses in 
Housing Estates. 
• Additional piece of land could be bought at Kilylastin to increase 
the  number of units from 9 to 11. 
• Identified the need to form a working group to look at the issues 
surrounding water services infrastructure and the utilisation of a 
traffic light system which would streamline development and 
explore options for improving communication in terms of the 
current infrastructural deficits. 
• Workshop needed to explore water and sewerage issues and to 
explore all options going forward. 
 
Questions  
➢ Who pays for utility upgrades, and can Donegal County Council 
apply for funding to cover same in respect of works at 
Ballymacool, Oldtown etc? 
➢ What geotechnical examinations have been carried out on 
housing developments currently under construction? 
➢ Is it proposed to do a geotechnical examination on the ground 
area for any new developments so that it can be established 
whether the ground is contaminated and ensure that all 
concerned are fully aware of the type of concrete that needs to 
be put in? 
➢ What companies have been approved to oversee the engineers 
plan of work? 
➢ What laboratories have been used to test the concrete blocks 
and the concrete used in the foundations for new 
developments? 
➢ How many Clerks of Works are on each site on behalf of 
Donegal County Council and the Department of Housing? 
➢ Will the Contractors appoint Clerks of Work to oversee the 
projects on behalf of the Council? 
➢ What sump tests have been done on lorries coming into these 
sites to pour the foundations? 

6 
 
➢ What level of testing on blocks and the foundations has been 
carried out to date on all of these new developments? 
➢ Has there been any interest confirmed regarding the Cloondara 
development in Carndonagh? 
➢ Is the Council interested in the lands opposite the football pitch 
in Milford? 
➢ What surveys/studies are being carried out prior to the purchase 
of sites and what level of liaison takes place with the various 
utility companies? 
➢ What is being done to progress the buying of houses in 
Ballymacool  by Donegal County Council and what discussions 
have taken place to date?  
➢ Issues with the pump at Glenswilly Chapel and what is being 
done to get this moved on to the mains power supply? 
➢ Will the Part 8s for Oldtown and Milford go live this side of 
Christmas? 
➢ Concern regarding the water supply at Lough Colmcille and 
query as to whether the required upgrades will proceed and who 
is ultimately responsible? 
➢ What is the situation with the Cluid houses in Letterkenny? 
➢ When will the houses at Crana Crescent be allocated? 
➢ Has the route for the slip road at Drumacrin in Bundoran been 
identified as there is a need to ensure that traffic congestion 
does not become a problem in this area? 
 
Mr Patsy Lafferty, Director of Housing, Corporate & Cultural Services 
said that it was intended to organise a workshop with the 
Letterkenny/Milford MD in the next week to go through the detail in 
relation to Ballymacool. 
 
Mr John Gallagher, Senior Engineer responding to a number of queries 
advised that a meeting would be arranged with the residents in 
Ballymacool and members would receive an update in early 2025 
regarding the design of the scheme. He said that Uisce Éireann were 
well aware that upgrades were needed at Ballymacool and were 
working on the detail. When this becomes available, he said, that an 
application would be made to the Department to fund the 
recommended upgrades and allow development to proceed. It was 
noted that there were regular meetings with Uisce Éireann regarding 
Ballymacool and other projects. Every effort was made, he advised, to 
flag projects with Uisce Éireann as early as possible before acquiring 
land and same was purchased only where there were adequate mains 
water and wastewater connections. Members would, he added, be kept 
up to date with the progress on same.  
 
He noted that there had been a number of questions in relation to 
pumping stations and water and sewerage issues which were within 
the remit of Uisce Éireann only. Contact details for the relevant 
personnel in Uisce Éireann should, he said, be available to members 
so that they are aware of who to contact when a situation arises.  

7 
 
In relation to the queries on the geo technical examinations, he said 
that site investigations and geological investigations of ground 
conditions were carried and fed into the design for all new projects.  
 
Alluding to queries raised in relation to archaeological assessments in 
the Newtowncunningham and Tamney areas, he said that that there 
was acknowledgement that a measured response was needed but 
these had to be carried out where requested by the Department. Every 
effort wads made, he said, to do these in parallel with the site 
assessment process.  
 
Cognisance was taken of the concerns raised by a number of members 
in relation to the air to water heating systems in social housing and the 
fact that these households were totally dependent on electricity. This 
was something, he advised, which could be looked at in the context of 
overall design issues.  
 
It was noted that there were concerns in relation to the provision of 
social housing in the Milford Electoral Area and that it was intended to 
meet with the MD members early in the New Year to keep a focus on 
that area and to look also at the smaller rural areas in terms of land 
acquisition.  
 
It was confirmed that capacity issues in Carndonagh had been raised 
at the recent meeting with Uisce Éireann and that these needed to be 
sorted before development could move forward. 
 
Mr Gallagher said that studies and procedures were carried out with 
due diligence and that the aim was to frontload these at an early stage 
so that that a number of sites could be assessed in one lot prior to 
acquisition.  
 
He advised that the Part 8 for Kilmacrennan would issue on the 12th 
December and that it was hoped to get the detail for Oldtown and 
Milford out before Christmas. Crana Crescent in Buncrana would, he 
confirmed, be complete in the next week to ten days.  
 
Donegal County Council, he advised, were not responsible for the 
water issues in Gort na Greine but that contact details for the relevant 
personnel in Uisce Éireann would be circulated to members.  
 
With regard to the slip road in Drumacrin, Bundoran, he noted that an 
indicative road alignment had been shown in the Development Plan 
and that it was also planned to look at land acquisition in that area also. 
 
The issue with the pump at Coolcholly was a matter for Uisce Éireann, 
he confirmed. 
 

8 
 
Housing Capital, it was noted, were looking actively at houses in 
existing estates and the Regeneration Team were looking at derelict 
properties with the aim of moving these forward.  
 
Issues at Killylastin, it was confirmed were in the process of being 
resolved. 
 
Mr Gallagher informed the meeting that the Meadowhill, Raphoe  
project was onsite at the minute and was progressing well with the 
expected completion date being May 2025 and would be kept under  
ongoing review.  
 
C/435/24 
UPDATE IN RELATION TO TURNKEY ACQUISITION AND 
APPROVED HOUSING BODIES 
 
Mr Donal Walker, Senior Engineer updated members in relation to the 
turnkey process and the status of the acquisition programme to date. 
An analysis of the proposals received since the initiation of the public 
procurement process in 2023 was presented and confirmation that this 
process would remain open until 2026. 
 
 
Mr Eamonn Brown, Divisional manager, Housing & Corporate gave an 
overview of the role of the Approved Housing Bodies, Donegal County 
Council interaction with same, and the application of the Capital 
Advance Leasing Facility and the Capital Assistance Scheme. 
Members were then given details on the updated housing grant 
schemes, the key updates involved together with changes to the 
associated occupational therapist services assessments. 
 
 
 
Concluding, he outlined, the fact that applications under the old 
scheme which had not yet been approved could be rescinded and a 
new application made. Applications approved but where the works had 
yet to commence could be rescinded also, he added, and a new 
application made. There was no provision however, he noted for 
applications that had been approved and works commenced.  
 
 
Cllr Mc Brearty welcomed the updates on turnkeys, AHBs, Housing 
Grant Schemes and on a point of clarification said that there needed to 
be proper debate on the final two presentations as members had 
specific issues which warranted further debate. He alluded to the fact 
that the three grants were now included on the one application form 
and that the €75k ceiling was restrictive in specific circumstances. 
Inflation, the cost of living, the defective concrete block issue were all 
having an impact, he added. Extensions to Council Houses were now 
defective, he added, given the absence of proper building control.  
 
 
The Leas-Cathaoirleach noting the time asked that questions in relation 
to the last two presentations be forwarded by email. 
 
 

9 
 
 
Mr Liam Ward, Deputy Chief Executive, said that the Director of 
Housing, Corporate & Cultural Services had indicated that he would be 
happy to discuss the issues outlined at a future special meeting. 
 
 
This concluded the business of the meeting.
