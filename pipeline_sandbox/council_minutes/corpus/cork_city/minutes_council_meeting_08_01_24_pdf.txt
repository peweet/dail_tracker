To ask the Chief Executive for an update on the Leghenamore Road Improvement 
Project? 
 
 
REPLY 
 
 
Part 8 Planning for the proposed Lehenaghmore Road Improvement Scheme was 
approved by Council in 2021. The scheme requires the acquisition of over eighty plots 
from twenty five land owners. In order to secure same, a Compulsory Purchase Order 
(CPO) for the scheme was published and submitted to An Bord Pleanal in August 
2022. An Bord Pleanála is currently assessing the objections received to the CPO and 
its determination on the land acquisition order is expected shortly.  
 
In the interim, the project team has been finalising the detailed designs for the works. 
It has also entered into negotiations with land owners aimed at securing the required 
lands through agreement where possible. Agreements have now been reached with the 
majority of land owners and negotiations at an advanced stage in respect of the 
remaining plots.

shortly (whether by approval to the CPO or direct agreement with landowners) thereby 
enabling the Council to proceed with the publication of tenders for the works subject 
to continued grant funding for the scheme. It is estimated that the construction phase 
will be of approx. twelve months duration.

NORTH WEST REGIONAL PARK 
 
 
In response to the following question submitted by Comhairleoir M. Nugent, a written 
reply was circulated as outlined below:- 
 
 
Can the CE report on progress re the North West Regional Park, is there a project team 
with a lead in place? Can re-zonings through the City Development Plan be 
accommodated to still deliver this priority Council objective? 
 
 
REPLY 
 
 
The development of a new North-West Regional Park is an objective of the City 
Council as set out in the City Development Plan 2022-2028 (objective 10.91). In 
addition, an appropriate area of land has been zoned as open space within the Plan to 
support this intention. 
 
The Development Plan proposes a facility that includes both active and passive 
recreational areas with interconnected spaces including parks, natural green spaces, 
ecosystems, greenways and blueways. The new park is intended to create green 
linkages between the City and its hinterland, complimenting biodiversity and proving 

important economic, leisure, health and environmental benefits to the north west 
quadrant of the City.  
 
The Development Plan envisages the preparation of an overall masterplan to guide the 
development of the proposed park.  Tenders for the appointment of a multidisciplinary 
design team to progress the development of the masterplan were issued in November 
2023. It is expected that the successful tenderer will be appointed in February and that 
work on the preparation of the masterplan will be progressed thereafter. The proposals 
for the new park will be presented to Members in advance of public consultation and 
Members will be kept appraised of progress on the development of same. 
 
Gerry O’Beirne, 
Director of Services, 
Infrastructure Development. 
 
6.8 
NORTHERN DISTRIBUTOR ROAD 
 
 
In response to the following question submitted by Comhairleoir J. Maher, a written 
reply was circulated as outlined below:- 
 
 
Can the Chief Executive provide an update on when the proposed route for the 
Northern Distributor Road for Cork City will be decided and when work will 
commence and what pressures / lobbying have been made by Cork City Council to 
ensure this project is delivered? 
 
 
REPLY 
 
 
The Cork Metropolitan Area Transport Strategy (2020) provides for the delivery of a 
new Cork Northern Distributor Multi-Modal Route as one of a number of capital 
projects which are required to support the development of Cork City over the period 
to 2040. The Strategy envisages scheme appraisal over the period to 2026 with scheme 
delivery progressing over the period to 2031.  
 
It is intended that the Cork Northern Distributor Multi-Modal Route provide for 
orbital movement for bus, pedestrian, cycle and some strategic and/or general traffic 
thereby reducing reliance on radial routes through Cork City Centre. 
 
In 2021 Council secured initial grant funding for the project from the National 
Transport Authority. This enabled the appointment of a multi disciplinary design team 
led by RPS Consulting Engineers to progress the assessment and preliminary design 
for the scheme. 
 
In accordance with the requirements of the Public Spending Code, the Strategic 
Appraisal Report for the scheme was prepared and submitted for approval in 2023. 
This was subsequently updated and approved in December 2023. This approval 
enabled the Design Team to progress with the concept development and options 
selection phase of the design process. This work is now underway and it is anticipated 
that Council will be undertaking non statutory public consultation on the preferred 
scheme routing in the coming months. This will be followed by the preliminary design 

and planning approval phases for the scheme. Members will be kept appraised of 
progress on the scheme over the coming months. 
 
Gerry O’Beirne, 
Director of Services, 
Infrastructure Development.

10.2.2 
PARK AND RIDE FOR THE NORTHSIDE 
 
 
An Chomhairle considered and approved the report of the Director of Services, 
Infrastructure Development, dated 11th December 2023 on the following motion:- 
 
‘That Cork City Council makes contact with relevant departments, NTA and other 
stakeholders necessary to ensure a park and ride is delivered for the Northside of Cork 
City.’ 
 
(Proposer: Cllr. J. Maher 23/468) 
 
The report of the Director of Services stated that the Cork Metropolitan Area 
Transport Strategy (2020) details the infrastructure required to support the 
development of Cork over the period to 2040. It envisages the development of a 
network of strategic Park and Rides at designated public transport interchanges so as 
to provide onward access to the City Centre and other key destinations via high 
frequency public transport, walking or cycling. 
  
The Strategy identifies six indicative locations for such facilities which will be the 
subject of further investigation (Blarney/ Stoneview, Dunkettle, Carrr’s Hill, Bandon 
Rd and Ballincollig). In the majority of cases the Park and Rides are related to the 
delivery of the new Bus Connects network with the necessary Bus Corridor upgrades 
to be delivered in advance of the opening of new Park and Ride facilities. This is 
because Park and Ride facilities are not attractive to potential users if the related bus 
service is hampered by general traffic congestion. Significant work has been 
undertaken by NTA in relation to the Bus Network Review and more recently in 
relation to the proposed Strategic Corridor Upgrades. Progress on the other elements 
of the Bus Connects Programme will similarly be advanced in the period ahead and 
these include Park and Rides, ticketing, cashless payment systems, bus fleet/zero 
emissions, new fare structures and new stops/shelters. Members will continue to be 
updated on progress in the planning and delivery of the Bus Connects programme and 
related element.

11.3.2 
REVISE POLICY REGARDING AFFORDABLE HOUSING SCHEMES 

 
 
An Chomhairle considered and approved the report of the Director of Services, 
Housing Delivery & Regeneration, dated 14th December 2023 on the following 
motion:- 
 
 
‘That Cork City Council agrees to revise policy to allow successful applicants for 
affordable housing who face a delay of over 4 weeks from original closure date, 
through no fault of their own, to apply for other affordable housing schemes as these 
may come available.’ 
 
(Proposer: Cllr. K. Collins 23/345) 
 
The report of the Director of Services stated that Cork City Council ‘Local Authority 
Affordable Purchase’ schemes are offered in accordance with the Affordable Housing 
Act 2021 enacted on 21st July 2021 and the Affordable Housing Regulations 2022 
which came into effect on 13th April 2022. The legislation does not contain any 
impediment to applicants applying for multiple affordable schemes, with their 
eligibility independently assessed for each scheme. 
 
However, successful applicants, who are under offer for purchase of a home in one 
scheme, in advance of advertisement of a subsequent scheme, reach a point where 
they need to demonstrate commitment and property-specific mortgage approval. 
Therefore, it is not feasible to suspend their offer for purchase of a home indefinitely, 
while subsequent schemes are advertised, and applicants qualified.

11.3.4 
CONVERSION OF COMMERCIAL PROPERTIES INTO HOUSING 
 
 
An Chomhairle considered and approved the report of the Director of Services, 
Housing Delivery & Regeneration, dated 14th December 2023 on the following 
motion:- 
 
 
‘That a report be prepared on the potential conversion of commercial city centre 
properties into housing.’ 
 
(Proposer: Cllr. D. Boyle 23/401) 
 
The report of the Director of Services stated that Cork City Council, Housing Delivery 
& Regeneration Directorate has, in recent years, as part of its housing delivery 
programme, actively sought to implement initiatives to incentivise the potential 
conversion and/or repurposing of commercial properties for the provision of social 
housing. Such initiatives include, but are not limited to, ‘Competitive Dialogue’, 
‘Repair & Lease’ and ‘Turnkey Acquisition’ all of which have the capacity to deliver 
in a variety of scale. 

 
The initial success of such initiatives is evidenced in delivery of residential projects 
at Denroches Cross, Watercourse Road and Skehard Road, which saw the repurposing 
of industrial buildings and their yards through the ‘Competitive Dialogue’ 
procurement process. 
 
In addition, the successful procurement of a development proposal which resulted in 
the conversion of ‘Springville House’, former commercial offices, (Blackrock Road) 
to provide for right sizing housing, is delivered in partnership with Tuath. The 
innovative approach by Cork City Council was recognized by Chambers Ireland, 
Excellence in Local Government Awards 2023, as winner of the ‘Urban Revival’ 
category. 
 
While such properties are generally in the realm of private ownership, which can be 
challenging to realise such conversion/repurposing, Housing Delivery & 
Regeneration is aware of the potential and continues to develop our initiatives to 
further incentivise the conversion of commercial properties to residential 
developments. 
 
11.3.5 
INCLUDE MOBILITY SCOOTERS UNDER GRANTS FOR OLDER PEOPLE 
AND PEOPLE WITH A DISABILITY PROGRAMME 
 
 
An Chomhairle considered and approved the report of the Director of Services, 
Housing Operations, dated 14th December 2023 on the following motion:- 
 
‘That Cork City Council includes mobility scooters under the Grants for Older People 
and People with a Disability programme. 
 
Mobility scooters have become an essential mode of transportation for individuals 
with mobility challenges, enabling them to maintain their independence and 
participate fully in community life. These scooters play a crucial role in enhancing the 
quality of life for older people and those living with disabilities, providing them with 
the freedom to navigate their surroundings. 
 
By incorporating mobility scooters into the existing Grants for Older People and 
People with a Disability programme, we can directly assist individuals in obtaining 
these devices. This support will alleviate the financial burden that often accompanies 
the purchase of a mobility scooter, making it more accessible to those who require it. 
 
Additionally, by including mobility scooters in the grant program, we send a clear 
message of inclusivity and support to our older residents and fellow citizens with 
disabilities. It is vital that we empower all members of our community to live their 
lives to the fullest and participate in society without limitations.’ 
 
(Proposer: Cllr. K. O’Flynn 23/322) 
 
The report of the Director of Services stated that Cork City Council along with all 
local authorities determine whether a grant is payable in the first instance in 
accordance with the Statutory Regulations, Housing (Adaptation Grants for Older 

People and People with a Disability), Regulations 2007 [S.I. No. 670 of 2007], 
Amendment Regulations 2014 [S.I. No. 104 of 2014] and the criteria outlined therein, 
and if so, the works which are necessary to make the accommodation more suitable 
for the applicant. 
 
The scheme comprises a range of grants for necessary improvement works or 
adaptations to houses, in order to facilitate the continued independent occupancy of 
their own homes by older people and people with a disability. These schemes cover 
works reasonably necessary for the purposes of rendering a house more suitable for 
the accommodation of applicants, which does not include the provision of grant 
funding towards mobility scooters. 
 
An Chomhairle further agreed to refer the motion back to the Housing Strategic Policy 
Committee for further discussion.

out the water pipe system throughout the City, but this also resulted in hundreds of 
leaks in the piping system.

which had been used to patch previous leaks, to detach and enter the drinking water.

Murphy Construction, I am now proposing that Cork City Council calls on the 
coalition Government to, as a matter of urgency, abolish Uisce Éireann, and to transfer 
all of the water services, sewage services and water treatment plants back to the local 
authorities such as Cork City Council. 
 
I envision this process being completed using existing infrastructure within the local 
authorities, and drawing on the experience and care of the water workers, to return our 
water treatment and management systems to full public ownership. 
 
Only when these essential water treatment services are returned to democratic, public 
control, can the residents of Cork City and beyond have faith in the quality and safety 
of our water supply.’ 
 
(Proposer: Cllr. T. Tynan 24/018)

19.4 
RECENT DELAYS ON KEY CITY BUS SERVICES 
 
 
An Chomhairle considered and approved the following motion:- 
 
‘That Cork City Council write to the management of Bus Éireann to request an urgent 
meeting with Councillors to address recent delays on key city bus services and to the 
Minister for Transport to ask for an explanation as to the National Transport 
Authority’s announcement that new services in Cork scheduled for implementation in 
2024 have been postponed until at least 2025 despite urgent community need.’ 
 

(Proposer: Cllr. L. Bogue 24/040) 
 
SUSPENSION OF STANDING ORDERS 
 
 
An tArdmhéara invited Comhairleoir O. Moran to address the meeting on the 
following motion, after which a discussion took place and An Chomhairle 
unanimously approved the motion:- 
 
 
‘That Cork City Council, noting the attacks by Hamas of 7 October and the death of 
over 1,100 people, including nearly 700 civilians and 36 children, Israel's ongoing 
military campaign in Gaza and the resulting humanitarian catastrophe there, with the 
death of over 22,000 people in Gaza, including 8,000 children, and further noting that 
the Republic of South Africa has launched a case at the International Court of Justice 
deeming this military campaign an act of genocide, will write to the Minister for 
Foreign Affairs to publicly push the EU to call for an immediate and permanent 
ceasefire, an appropriate humanitarian intervention and a political settlement based on 
equality and justice.’ 
 
 
 
 
 
This concluded the business of the meeting
