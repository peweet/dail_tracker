## What the SSHA is

The **Summary of Social Housing Assessments (SSHA)** is a statutory, point-in-time
count of all households that local authorities have assessed as **qualified for
social housing support but whose need is not currently being met** ("net need").
It is carried out annually by the 31 local authorities under **Section 21 of the
Housing (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2009** and the **Social Housing Assessment
(Summary) Regulations 2016**, compiled by the Local Government Management Agency
(LGMA) and analysed/published by the Housing Agency.

**Net need explicitly excludes:**
1. **Duplicate applications** — only the first application is counted where a
   household applied to more than one authority (2024: 436 duplicates removed;
   2025: 421).
2. **Households already in receipt of social housing support** — i.e. living in
   local authority or Approved Housing Body (AHB) accommodation, or supported via
   HAP, RAS or SHCEP.
3. **Households on a transfer list** — anyone applying to transfer from an
   existing support, including HAP.

SSHA homelessness figures **differ from** the DHLGH monthly homelessness reports
(different definitions, timing, household-vs-individual counts, IT anomalies).

Count history: annual since **2016** (first count Sept 2016; June 2017–2019;
November each year 2020 onward, moved to Nov due to Covid disruption and kept
since). SSHA 2024 = 9th annual; SSHA 2025 = 10th annual. Figures comparable back
to a 2013 assessment under the common legislative framework.

Policy anchor: 2024 report references **Housing for All** (90,000+ social homes by
2030, avg 9,500/yr new build to 2026). 2025 report references the successor
**Delivering Homes, Building Communities 2025–2030 Action Plan** (72,000 social
homes over the plan, avg 12,000/yr new build).

Income thresholds (since 1 Jan 2023, +€5,000): Band 1 €40,000, Band 2 €35,000,
Band 3 €30,000 (net income; +5% per extra adult up to 10%, +2.5% per child).

## Geography (local authorities)

- **Dublin City** has by far the greatest need: 12,682 households (21.2%) in 2024;
  13,002 (21.1%) in 2025.
- **Four Dublin authorities** (Dublin City, Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown, Fingal, South
  Dublin): 24,598 (41.0% of national) in 2024 → 25,684 (41.6%) in 2025 (+1,086,
  +4.4%).
- **Mid-East** (Louth, Kildare, Meath, Wicklow): 8,665 (14.5%) in 2024 → 8,865
  (14.3%) in 2025.
- **Cork** (City + County): 5,055 (8.4%) in 2024 → 4,718 (7.7%) in 2025 (−337,
  −6.7%).
- **Galway** (City + County): 3,174 (5.3%) in 2024 → 3,463 (5.6%) in 2025 (+289,
  +9.1%).
- **Limerick City & County**: 2,423 (4.0%) 2024 → 2,419 (3.9%) 2025.
- **Waterford City & County**: 1,419 (2.4%) 2024 → 1,458 (2.4%) 2025.

**Largest movers:**
- 2024 biggest **decreases**: Monaghan −16.5%, Kilkenny −15.4%, Meath −7.5%.
- 2024 biggest **increases**: Leitrim +22.7%, Donegal +19.0%, Galway City +14.6%.
- 2025 biggest **decreases**: Cork City −10.4%, Louth −9.2%, Laois −7.0%.
- 2025 biggest **increases**: Longford +39.7%, Leitrim +16.9%, Monaghan +15.5%.

(Note: 21 May 2019 Cork City/County boundary change means per-authority Cork
figures aren't comparable before 2019, though national aggregates are.)

## Year-over-year story (2024 → 2025)

- Net need up +3.0% (1,778 households) to 61,719 — second consecutive annual rise.
- Dublin's share edged up to 41.6%; Longford (+39.7%), Leitrim, and Monaghan saw
  the sharpest local increases; Cork City the sharpest drop (−10.4%).
- Homelessness rose on both measures (basis of need 16.1%; tenure 16.1%).
- Continued shift away from private renting toward living with family.
- Employment among applicants continued rising; long-term (7+ yr) waiters' share
  fell slightly.
- Sharp rise in refugee basis-of-stay within the non-EEA cohort.

## Data structure (for enrichment)

Each annual report = one national snapshot plus an **appendix breaking every
profile table down by the 31 local authorities** (Tables A1.1–A1.9), with
2-year (prior/current) comparison columns. The dimensions available per local
authority:

- Age profile of main applicant (A1.1)
- Employment status (A1.2)
- Source of household income (A1.3)
- Household size/composition (A1.4)
- Main need for social housing support (A1.5)
- Specific accommodation requirements (A1.6) + Traveller identifier (A1.6a)
- Current tenure (A1.7)
- Length of time on waiting list (A1.8)
- Citizenship (A1.9)

Machine-readable versions of the main + appendix tables for **all years** are on
the Housing Agency **Data Hub** (link above) — preferable to PDF scraping.

## Enrichment note (Dáil Tracker)

> **Flagged for enrichment — not yet built.**

Why it's relevant: SSHA is **local-authority-level civic-need data** with a clean
annual time series back to 2016. Dáil Tracker is organised around TDs and
constituencies; social-housing waiting-list need is a strong constituency-context
signal (housing is consistently the top national political issue).

Possible direction (subject to the usual pipeline rules):
- Pull the **Data Hub** machine-readable LA tables (avoid PDF scraping) into
  `pipeline_sandbox/` first (Polars), per [[project_pipeline_sandbox_rule]].
- Map **31 local authorities → Dáil constituencies** (many-to-many; needs a
  crosswalk — LAs and constituencies don't align 1:1).
- Surface as constituency/member context: waiting-list size, homelessness share,
  trend vs prior year, tenure mix.
- Note the **SHCEP / HAP / RAS exclusion** when describing "net need" so the
  figure isn't misread as total housing need.
- Privacy: SSHA is fully aggregated (no individuals) — no conflict with
  [[feedback_personal_insolvency_privacy]].

Open questions: LA→constituency crosswalk source; whether the Data Hub exposes a
stable download/API; how to present a non-1:1 geographic join honestly.

## Related programme references — SHCEP & capital schemes

> Naming caveat: the SSHA body text says "Social Housing **Capital** Expenditure
> Programme (SHCEP)" but its own glossary (and the Department) define SHCEP as the
> Social Housing **Current** Expenditure Programme — recurrent **leasing**
> payments, not capital build. There is **no programme literally named "Capital
> Expenditure Programme"**; the capital (new-build) side runs under **SHIP**
> (Social Housing Investment Programme), **CALF** (Capital Advance Leasing
> Facility) and **CAS** (Capital Assistance Scheme). Treat the SSHA wording as a
> report error.

**Census (CSO Census 2022) — population denominator / civic context**

The CSO Census is the strongest geographic fit for a constituency-organised app:
results are published down to **Small Area** level *and* aggregated by **Dáil
constituency**, so census provides the *population denominator* (and the same
tenure/Traveller/nationality dimensions as SSHA) — letting you express need
*per head of population* by constituency. Small Areas nest within constituencies,
so they can be aggregated up (solves the SSHA LA→constituency crosswalk problem).

- Census 2022 **Small Area Population Statistics (SAPS)** — 48 tables incl.
  housing/tenure, household composition, Travellers, nationality, age; **open CSV
