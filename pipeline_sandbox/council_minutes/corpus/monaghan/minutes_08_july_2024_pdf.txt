3. To receive a presentation on Section 179A and the implications for Housing  
 
Mr. L. MacCinna, Senior Executive Engineer, Housing, provided members with an update on 
new legislation which had been enacted on 08 March 2023 by Minister Darragh O’Brien 
under Section 179A of the Planning and Development Regulations 2023, to accelerate the 
construction of social housing. He stated that Section 179A allows an exemption to the Part 
8 planning process for local authorities once the following conditions are met: 
 
• 
The housing development must fall within the definition as set out in the legislation; 
• 
The housing development must be carried out by, on behalf or, or jointly or in partnership 
with a local authority; 
• 
The housing development must not materially contravene the development plan or local 
area plan for that development; 
• 
The housing development is in accordance with the strategy included in the development 
plan for the area; 
• 
No assessment is required under the Environmental Impact Assessment Directive or the 
Habitats Directive; 
• 
The land must be owned by the local authority or a state authority, zoned for residential 
use and has access or can be connected to necessary facilities and infrastructure; and 
• 
the work must commence on or before 31 December 2024. 
 
Mr. MaCinna advised the members that Monaghan County Council have two social housing 
developments with funding approval that fall under the Section 179A legislation - Slí Loráin, 
Latlorcan, Monaghan which consists of 51 social houses and Slí NaCoille, Mullaghmatt, 
Monaghan which consists of 21 social houses. He highlighted that the key difference 
between Section 179A and Part VIII legislation would be that these types of social housing 
developments would not be brought before the elected members for approval, provided 
that the relevant conditions had been met. Mr. MacCinna emphasised the strong working 
relationship between the elected members and the executive and highlighted that the main 
benefit of this new legislation would be a reduction in the amount of time required to 
complete the planning process.  
 
Mr. MacCinna, Mr. R. Burns, Chief Executive and Ms. O. McConnon, A/Director of Services, 
responded to members queries.  
 
Ms. O. McConnon advised the members that a detailed presentation on housing delivery 
schemes would be scheduled for the September County Council meeting.  
 
The Cathaoirleach and members thanked Mr. MacCinna for his presentation.

11. To receive update on N2 National Primary Road Schemes 
 
Mr. G. McMahon, A/Director of Services provided the members with background information 
in relation to the N2 National Primary Road Schemes:  
 
Clontibret – Border Road Scheme 
• 
The Clontibret to the border road scheme had been first developed in the 1990s but 
was suspended in the mid-2000s to allow for a larger project to tie in with the NI 
border.  
• 
The scheme recommenced in 2008, however, was suspended again in 2012 due to 
the economic downturn.  
• 
The project will see the upgrade of 28km of the N2 Dublin to Derry Road to a dual 
carriageway. 
• 
The objectives of the scheme are to improve road safety, enhance north south 
regional connectivity and provide journey time certainty for cross-border traffic.  
• 
The project will bypass Emyvale, urban areas of Monaghan Town and Corracrin, 
allowing for public realm and active travel within the urban areas.   
• 
The project is listed in the National Development Plan and also an objective within 
the Northern and Western Regional Assembly’s Spatial & Economic Strategy for 
planning and construction by 2032.  
• 
It will provide investment of over €300m in road transport in the border region.  
• 
The scheme will complement the proposed upgrade of 85km of the road network in 
Northern Ireland – The A5 Western Transport Corridor from Aughnacloy to Derry.  
• 
N2 Clontibret – Border Road Scheme commenced in 2018 and the new preferred 
route corridor was published in early 2021.  
• 
In 2021, the project entered phase 3 of the design process, however as no funding 
allocation was received in 2022 the design process was suspended.  
• 
In 2023, an application for EU Funding through the Connecting Europe Facility (CEF) 
was successful and this allowed design works to recommence.  
• 
The current phase of the design seeks to provide the detailed road alignment and 
junction layouts which will allow the Environmental Impact Assessment Report 
(which will be the formal planning approval document) to be developed for 
submission to an Bórd Pleanálla. 
 
Ardee – Castleblayney Road Scheme 
• 
Originally the Ardee - Castleblayney Road Scheme was developed in parallel with the 
Clontibret to the Border Road Scheme with the same design consultants.  
• 
In 2022, the detailed road alignment design had been completed and consultation 
with the relevant landowners had taken place, however, in 2023 the project was 
suspended as funding was restricted to provide only for expenditure that was 
‘contract committed’.  
• 
The Ardee – Castleblayney Road Scheme did not receive any funding allocation in 
2024 and remains suspended.  
 
 
12. To receive update on North/South Interconnector project 
 

 
8 
 
Mr. A. Hughes, Senior Planner joined the meeting to provide an update from the Planning 
Authority in respect of two specific queries relating to the North South Interconnector in 
which legal opinion had been sought: 
  
1. The expiry of the planning permission 
2. The Authority of the Electricity Supply Board (ESB) to engage in compliance matters. 
  
Mr. Hughes provided the members with the following synopsis of the legal opinion from 
Senior Counsel in relation to both queries: 
  
• 
Expiry of Planning Permission 
The life of a ‘normal’ planning permission is 5 years. This period applies to planning 
permissions granted under Part III or Part XXI of the Planning and Development Act 2000 
(as amended). The ‘approval’ that was granted for the North South interconnector was 
granted under s.182A of the Act, which does not come under Part III or Part XXI. 
Consequently, the period of 5 years does not apply to the North South interconnector 
approval, and it is Counsel opinion that there is no limitation (in terms of an appropriate 
period) in respect of this ‘approval’ - therefore in effect the approval has an indefinite 
duration. 
  
• 
The authority of the Electricity Supply Board (ESB) to engage in compliance 
matters. 
Conditions numbers 1,4,5,6 and 7 of the Boards decision refers to the ‘undertaker’ being 
required to do certain things – in terms of compliance. It is Counsel opinion that the role of 
ESB Networks would fall within the definition (i.e. ‘undertaker’), and that the Council does 
have jurisdiction to engage with ESB Networks in terms of considering and determining 
compliance submissions under the conditions attached to the Board Order. The Bord had 
considered and determined the application for the interconnector, as was their statutory 
function.  Conditions attached to the approval required certain matters to be agreed with 
the Planning Authority and there is an obligation on The Planning Authority to participate 
in the process of compliance and agree matters. Mr. A. Hughes assured members that this 
would be carried out in a diligent manner.

8. What is the update on the Water Quality in Lough Muckno? 
Reply: The monitoring phase of the Lough Muckno Road to Recovery Project ended in 
December 2023. The consultant is currently finalising the report and we expect to receive it 
at the end of July. Staff from Environment Section and the consultant working on the project 
presented the initial findings to the Climate & Environment SPC on the 29th of April 2024.  
 
The initial findings from the lake coring and lake monitoring suggest an internal loading of 
phosphorus from the lake sediment which suggests that the recovery of Lough Muckno will 
take decades even if all current inputs of phosphorus from the catchment area were ceased. 
 
The data from the assessment of the inputting tributaries to Lough Muckno gathered as part 
of the Road to Recovery project is currently being used to inform and focus the Environment 
Section work plans. The inputting load assessment from the project indicates that the 
County Water and Gentle Owens River Catchment areas are contributing the highest loads 
to the Lough. Investigative assessment and farm inspections having been ongoing in the 
Gentle Owens River since 2023 and work is due to commence in the County Water River 
catchment in the coming weeks.  
 
Although Lough Muckno is not a designated bathing water and Monaghan County Council is 
therefore not obliged to monitor such waters, over the summer months samples will be taken 
at 3 locations on Lough Muckno and analysed for the bathing water parameters. These 
samples will be taken on a monthly basis from June to September and analysed for the 
bathing water parameters.  
 
Samples will be taken at the following locations:  
1) The Lough Muckno bathing area  
2)The Castleblayney OEC/water sports area and  
3) Black Island Point.  
 
If sample results are not within the permissible limits for the bacteriological parameters, an 
advice not to swim notice will be erected on site and posted on the councils’ social media.  
 
Samples were taken on the 27th of June 2024, and results show that the water was compliant 
with bathing water standards, with water quality deemed to be in the ‘excellent’ category in 
each of the three sample locations.
