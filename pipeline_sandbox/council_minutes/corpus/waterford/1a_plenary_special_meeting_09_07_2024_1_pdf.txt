1. Section 153 (2) of the Local Government Act 2001 (as amended)  
 
I.Grimes, Interim Chief Executive, outlined that in light of the recent ruling in the High Court 
(Oceanscape Unlimited Company -V- Dun Laoghaire Rathdown County Council – 2022 339 
JR) that our legal representatives have advised that the following resolution is approved by the 
members; 
 
In accordance with the provisions of Section 153(2) of the Local Government Act, 2001 (as 
amended), we the elected members of Waterford City and County Council hereby expressly 
authorise the Chief Executive of the local authority, or persons acting under his direction and 
instruction, to act for and on behalf of the local authority in the High Court Judicial Review 
proceedings instituted by Ruden Homes Limited and John Deane against Waterford City and 
County Council [High Court Record No. 2022/651 JR]  and to do any act, matter or thing, to 

conduct, defend or compromise the aforesaid proceedings and further agree that to the extent 
necessary this authorisation shall apply to all actions taken to date by the chief executive, or 
persons acting under his direction and instruction, in the proceedings. 
 
Cllr. Kelly enquired if resolution wasn’t approved if the case would fail and asked about the 
associated. 
Cllr. Fitzgerald is approved was this approval for all cases in the future. 
 
I.Grimes, Interim Chief Executive outlined that it is prudent to have the resolution in place for 
the case, difficult to determine the costs at this time and will be a decision for the courts. Also 
confirmed that today’s approval is not setting a precedence for future cases. 
 
Cllr. Geoghegan noted the position of the meeting is to support the submission of the resolution 
to the court. 
Cllr. D. McGuinness outlined that it is important to empower the Chief Executive to take 
whatever legal measures deemed prudent to represent the council against the court action being 
taken and asked that the members are kept up to date with all proceedings.  
Cllr. Phelan noted it is incumbent on the members to have the Chief Executive act on behalf of 
the members and enquired if there are any potential personal risks that could result from the 
council being unsuccessful in its case.  
 
I.Grimes, Interim Chief Executive noted that the council is essentially defending its decision 
in relation to the Development Plan, the documents circulated to the members demonstrate the 
full extent of the case.  He assured the members that he will keep them updated on proceedings 
and confirmed that there are no personal risks to the elected members.  
 
Cllr. Conway outlined his concerns in relation to the contents of the plaintiff’s affidavit which 
leaves the council with no option but to defend.  If the plaintiff is successful it will result in 
further action against the council.  
Cllr. Nugent stated that the council must defend the process undertaken in relation to the 
Development Plan. 
Cllr. Donal Barry enquired about the affect on the Development Plan and for other parties to 
take a case if the plaintiff’s case is successful. 
Cllr. Griffin enquired if there is any impact on the new councillors who were not involved in 
the process for the Development Plan. 
 
I.Grimes, Interim Chief Executive outlined that it will be a matter for judge to decide on the 
evidence as presented and cannot expand on the possible implications on the Development 
Plan.  He reiterated that there is no personal liability to any of the elected members.  
 
The resolution as presented was proposed by Cllr. O’Sullivan and seconded by Cllr. D. 
McGuinness.  A roll call vote was requested and agreed on. Roll call vote undertaken by H. 
Dunphy, Meetings Administrator.
