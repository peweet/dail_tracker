Outgoing Mayor Cllr. Joe Conway brought the meeting to order. Ms. Honor Dunphy, Meetings 
Administrator, read the names of the persons duly elected to Waterford City & County Council in 
the local elections of 7th June 2024. 
 
Cllr. Conway welcomed all members to the AGM and thanked the press, P. Johnston, Returning 
Officer and all involved in the successful running of the recent Local Elections 2024. He welcomed 
all those present and thanked his wife Sandra and family for their support during his term as Mayor.  
 
The members congratulated the outgoing Mayor and his family and in particular his wife Sandra. 
They thanked him for his courtesy, impartiality, dignity, wit and the cúpla focal in the Chamber and 
acknowledged his excellent year as Mayor of Waterford City & County.  
 
I. Grimes, A/CEO also spoke to congratulate the outgoing Mayor, his wife Sandra and family.

Cllr. Murphy received majority of votes and was deemed elected to the position of Mayor of 
Waterford City and County.  
 
Cllr. Murphy took the Mayors seat and addressed the meeting. He said he was privileged and 
honoured to be elected Mayor. He thanked his proposer Cllr. Wyse and his seconder Cllr. 
O’Sullivan. He thanked his Council colleagues for their support, thanked Cllrs. Griffin and Daniels 
and also his family, friends and supporters and paid tribute to his late mother Annette. He said as 
the new Mayor he promised to lead with integrity, honesty and dedication. He said he looked 
forward to working with the Executive and the members in progressing the North Quays 
development, in attracting continued investment for our University and in ensuring Government 
funding is available for Waterford Airport. He said he will prioritise initiatives that will enhance the 
quality of life for all and will continue to lobby for better public transport and continued delivery of 
housing.  He said the Office of Mayor is dear to the people of Waterford City and County and he 
will continue to make the office accessible to all communities. He said he hoped to be Mayor for all 
and praised our democratic process at both local and national level.

3. ADOPTION OF STANDING ORDERS 
 
It was agreed that the ordinary meetings of Waterford City & County Council be held on the second 
Thursday of each month in City Hall Waterford and Civic Offices Dungarvan on an alternating 
basis with no meeting scheduled for the month of August.
