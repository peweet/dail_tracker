ENVIRONMENT 
Anti-Dumping Initiative 
The Anti-Dumping Initiative (ADI) is a scheme whereby the Department of Environment, Climate and 
communications provide funding to local authorities with the aim of reducing the number of incidents of 
illegal dumping nationally. The initiative is co-ordinated through the WERLAs (Waste Enforcement 
Regional Local Authorities). 
Illegal dumping is particularly problematic for LCC.  In September 2025 ADI funding enabled LCC to carry 
out a number of clean ups at sites which were the focus of ongoing dumping at: 
• Tarmon Bring Banks, Drumkeeran 
• on the Miners Trail at Greaghnaslieve, Drumkeeran 
 
In addition, projects which aim to eliminate the potential for dumping, such as the   bulky waste and 
mattress amnesties were also held. The events were advertised on the local paper, local radio stations, 
LCC website and on social media.  Visual display signs were strategically placed for maximum exposure.  
On both entrance roads in Manorhamilton [Sligo Rd and Carrick Rd] and at Paric Sean MacDiarmada, 
Carrick-on-Shannon.   The involvement and co-operation of the area staff was key to the smooth running 
of both events. 
• Two Bulky Waste & Mattress Collection Events. – One in North Leitrim and one in South Leitrim.  
250 mattresses were collected in Manorhamilton for recycling and 55.56 tonnes of Bulky Waste 
was collected by Donegal Waste for disposal. 497 mattresses were collected in Mohill for recycling 
and 86.48 tonnes of waste were Collected by Barna Waste for disposal.

Local Authority Integration Team (LAIT) 
In September, the Integration Team conducted six clinics aimed at identifying the needs of residents and 
families. Two of these clinics focused specifically on Beneficiaries of Temporary Protection, while the 
remaining four were held across the two International Protection Accommodation Centres in Leitrim. 
Two "Offer a Home" activations were successfully completed in September, providing accommodation 
for two families consisting of four adults and four children. 
In September, the Integration Team collaborated with a range of agencies across Leitrim to design and 
initiate the rollout of three targeted programmes, scheduled to commence in October. These 
programmes will place particular emphasis on supporting residents at the Abbey Manor Hotel. 
The programmes were developed to support various age cohorts within the community, informed by 
emerging needs and feedback from residents. 
• Children not currently availing of the Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) Scheme – This 
programme aims to engage children who are not participating in ECCE, providing opportunities 
for early learning and development. 
• Young People aged 12–17: “Finding the Balance” – This initiative will explore themes such as 
culture, diversity, and integration. It will support young people in navigating their new 
environment, flourishing within it, and actively engaging with their peers and the wider 
community. 
• Parents: “Parenting Then and Now” – This programme will provide a space for parents to reflect 
on their experiences of parenting prior to arriving in Ireland, discuss their current parenting 
journey, and identify any supports that may help ease their transition and strengthen their family 
life.

All In A Days Work Programmes & Associated Financial Supports 
The “All In A Days Work” campaign continues to be rolled out on the areas of Lean, Green and Digital 
supports.  There is significant growth in demand for all three supports, in particular the Energy Efficiency 
Grant (EEG) and Grow Digital (GD) Voucher.   The EEG, with up to 75% of eligible costs up to a maximum 
grant of €10,000 can be supported following the completion of a Green for Business, Green Start or a SEAI 
Energy Audit.   The GD voucher is a grant covering 50% of eligible costs (minimum grant of €500 up to a 
maximum grant of €5,000) limited to new software subscriptions for a period of 12 and training and 
configuration of the software.

Social Housing Applications and Assessments 
To date in 2025, the Housing Department has received a total of 136 new applications for social housing 
support.  Of these, 109 have been assessed – with 86 accepted and 23 refused.  3 applications are 
currently undergoing assessment, while 24 have been deemed invalid and require further information. 
There are currently 504 approved applicants on the housing waiting list. 
 
Traveller Accommodation Programme 2025–2029 
The Traveller Accommodation Programme 2025–2029, which commenced on 1st January 2025, sets out a 
target to deliver 20 accommodation units over a five-year period. 
As of 30th September 2025, a total of 6 units has been delivered through the Standard Local Authority 
Housing Scheme, representing 30% progress toward the overall target. Three of these units were 
completed during the month of September. 
The Housing Department remains focused on progressing delivery in line with the programme’s objectives 
for the remainder of the term. 
Housing Grants 
Housing Grant Approvals and Payments  
During 2025, activity under the Housing Adaptation Grant schemes has remained strong, with a steady 
flow of approvals, payments, and applications under assessment. The following data provides an 
overview of progress to date and illustrates how the programme is supporting people to live 
independently at home in line with the Housing Disability Strategy. 
 
Expenditure to Date 
To date in 2025, a total of €530,139 has been paid out under the Housing Grants Programme, covering 98 
individual grants. This expenditure reflects: 
• 
28 Housing Adaptation Grants for People with a Disability (HGD), 
• 
12 Mobility Aids Grants (MAG), and 
• 
58 Housing Aid for Older People (HOP). 
 
Committed Expenditure  
At present, a total of €845,048 has been approved under the Housing Grants Programme but has not yet 
been drawn down by applicants. This covers 141 individual grants, broken down as follows: 
 
• 
39 Housing Adaptation Grants for People with a Disability (HGD), 
• 
16 Mobility Aids Grants (MAG), and 
• 
86 Housing Aid for Older People (HOP). 
 
These approvals represent a significant pipeline of works that, once completed and claimed, will further 
advance the objectives of the Housing Strategy for Disabled People by enabling greater independence, 
accessibility, and quality of life for those supported through the scheme. 

23 
 
Housing Grant Applications Under Assessment 
In addition to the approvals already issued, we also have a pipeline of new applications currently under 
assessment. These include 23 Housing Aid for Older People applications, 8 Mobility Aids applications and 
9 Housing Adaptation Grants for People with a Disability.  
 
Housing Grants Workshop and Implementation of Revised Unit Rates 
During September, the Council held a workshop with Council Officials and Elected Members. During this 
session, Elected Members were informed of the revised unit rates for eligible works under the three grant 
schemes: the Mobility Aids Grant, the Housing Adaptation Grant for People with Disabilities, and the 
Housing Aid for Older People Grant. These revised rates came into effect on 23 September 2025. 
All eligible applications that were on hand but had not been issued an approval letter by 23 September 
will be assessed using the revised unit rates. However, applications that were assessed and received 
approval prior to this date under the previous unit rates will not be eligible to have the new rates applied 
retrospectively. 
Social Housing Rent Review 2025 
The rent review process for approximately 1,600 local authority and leasing tenancies remains ongoing. 
Further updates will be provided as the review advances toward completion.
