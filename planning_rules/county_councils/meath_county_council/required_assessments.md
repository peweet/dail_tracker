Triggers enumerated verbatim in the chapter:

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR) — 11.1.3
- An EIAR is required to accompany a planning application for development of a class set out in Schedule 5 of the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) which exceeds a limit, quantity or threshold for that class.
- EIA Screening referenced as a required assessment for certain proposals (e.g. extractive/quarry — DM standards reference Mandatory EIA Thresholds in Schedule 5 Part 2).

## Appropriate Assessment (AA) / Natura 2000 — 11.1.4
- AA is a focused, detailed assessment of a plan/project (alone and in combination) on the integrity of a Natura 2000 site.
- Four phases: Screening Report (Stage 1); Natura Impact Statement (Stage 2 Appropriate Assessment) if further assessment required; assessment of alternatives (Stage 3); assessment where no alternatives and adverse impacts remain (Stage 4). (Refer to Chapter 8 — Cultural & Natural Heritage Strategy, HER OBJ 33.)

## Other assessments (chapter, "may be deemed necessary... but not limited to")
- Flood Risk Assessment
- Landscape Impact Assessment
- Archaeological Assessment
- Architectural Assessment
- Ecology/Biodiversity Assessment (including e.g. bat surveys)
- Decided on a case-by-case basis at pre-application stage.

## Traffic & Transport Assessment (TTA) / Road Safety Audit
- TTA, Road Safety Audits (RSA) and Road Safety Impact Assessments required for major developments with significant potential to generate traffic or create a significant hazard, particularly on national roads.
- Indicative thresholds for requiring a TTA:
  - Traffic to/from the development exceeds 10% of the traffic flow on the adjoining road;
  - Traffic exceeds 5% of the traffic flow where congestion exists;
  - Residential development in excess of 100 dwellings;
  - Retail and leisure development in excess of 1,000 sq.m;
  - Industrial development in excess of 5,000 sq.m.

## Pre-Application Consultation (s.247) — 11.2
- At least one pre-application consultation required for residential development, or non-residential development of more than 1,000 square metres gross floor space. (SHD: 100+ dwellings / 200+ student bed spaces decided by An Bord Pleanála.)

## Flood Risk Assessment
- Hydrology Appraisal / Flood Risk Assessment listed among assessments for relevant proposals; proposals for surface water management and flood risk minimisation required for larger developments.

## Architectural Heritage / Protected Structures
- Architectural Assessment among required assessments; controls on development on/near Protected Structures and within Architectural Conservation Areas (Chapter 8 + Appendix 6 Record of Protected Structures cross-referenced). Relaxation of zoning objectives for Protected Structures (11.14.5) where restored to the highest standard.

## Site Suitability / Percolation (rural)
- Rural one-off house site-suitability and on-site wastewater (percolation) standards are addressed in Chapter 9 Rural Development Strategy (cross-referenced), not within Chapter 11.
