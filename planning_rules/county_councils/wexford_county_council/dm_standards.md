## Residential Density
- New residential schemes typically at **30–50 units per hectare** (also applied to higher/lower schemes per context).

## Separation / Private Open Space
- **Minimum 22 m** between opposing first-floor windows at the rear; **22 m** in general between dwellings and schools/childcare facilities.
- **Table 3-4 — Minimum Floor Area and Private Open Space:**

| Unit type | Min house floor area | Min private open space |
|---|---|---|
| One bedroom | 50 m² | 48 m² |
| Two bedroom | 80 m² | 55 m² |
| Three bedroom | 92 m² | 60 m² |
| Four bedroom or more | 110 m² | 70 m² |

- Minimum **20% of dwellings** in new schemes to be a defined unit type (mix requirement); apartments must comply with the **Sustainable Urban Housing: Design Standards for New Apartments (2020)** and its SPPRs (Tables 3-5 / 3-6).
- Dual-aspect: minimum **33%** of units (objective generally **50%**).

## Rural One-off Housing — Table 3-3 (Site Size / Floor Area / Biodiversity)

| Dwelling floor area | Minimum site size | Biodiversity requirement |
|---|---|---|
| < 100 m² | **0.2 ha** | Boundary reinforcement |
| 100–200 m² | **0.2 ha** | 20% of site set aside |
| > 200–300 m² | **0.4 ha** | 20% of site set aside |
| > 300 m² | **1.0 ha** | 50% of site set aside (tree planting / biodiversity) |

- Setback of the dwelling from the roadside boundary assessed case-by-case.
- Sightlines for the relevant road category must be achievable within the red-line site (§3.1.1 rural house design).

## Sightlines & Access (Section 6)
- Forward visibility splays required; sightlines measured per Section 6 (Figures 6-6 to 6-8).
- Removal of the full length of a roadside boundary to achieve sightlines should be avoided.

## Building Heights
- Building heights addressed via the Development Management process (§2.4), applying the national Building Heights Guidelines / SPPRs.
