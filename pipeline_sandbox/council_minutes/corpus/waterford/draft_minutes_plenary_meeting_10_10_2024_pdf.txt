6. Local Property Tax 
 
S. McKeown, CE said the Local Property Tax Local Adjustment Factor is currently set at 10%. 
Total revenue collected from LPT last year was just over €21m representing 11½% of the 
Council’s total revenue income, €10.3m collected locally and the balance of €9.9m was 
received from the Equalisation Fund. The required public consultation resulted in 9 
submissions, 2 advocated no change from 2024 LPT rate, 1 advocated an increase and 6 
advocated a decrease from 2024 LPT rate. He recommended that the Council increase the Local 
Adjustment Factor to 15% and fix this rate for 5 years saying the resulting additional revenue 
will help fund the rising cost of delivery of services and in fixing for 5 years gives greater 
financial certainty and provides certainty to homeowners and has a modest financial impact on 
the vast majority of homeowners. 
 
B. Pollard, HoF said the estimated budget for 2025 is showing a deficit of €2.4m. 2025 will see 
an increase in income from commercial rates but there will be additional costs in relation to 

payroll, the loan for the Public Lighting Energy Efficiency Project, metered electricity, 
cybersecurity and upgrade of the financial management system.  The financial impact of the 
change in rate of LPT for the majority of households in Waterford (52%) will be an increase 
of  €4.50 annually.  
 
The increase  in the Local Adjustment Factor to 15% fixed for 5 years was proposed by Cllr. 
Geoghegan and seconded by Cllr. Phelan.  
 
Cllrs. O’Leary, E. Quinlan, D’Arcy, Brazil, Clune, Kelly, Ryan and Nugent all spoke in support 
of the CE’s recommendation that the Council increase the Local Adjustment Factor to 15% 
fixed for 5 years. 
 
Cllrs. Donal Barry, Cllr. McGuinness, Daniels, Bailey, Hearne, O’Riordan, Burke, Fitzgerald, 
Griffin and Roche spoke against the recommendation to increase the Local Adjustment Factor 
to 15% fixed for 5 years. 
 
Cllr. Roche proposed that the current 10% rate be retained. This proposal was not seconded. 
 
Cllr. McGuinness proposed that the Local Property Tax return to base rate .This was seconded 
by Cllr. Fitzgerald. 
 
A roll call vote on Cllr. McGuinness’s proposal was requested.

She said today in Afghanistan and countries like Iran women have no rights and we must try 
and highlight their plight. She said women like Mahbooba are working in Ireland and all over

11. Monthly Management Report 
 
Cllr. Cronin raised the issue of the N25, sections of which he said are not fit for purpose and 
called on the Council to ensure it stays on the agenda. 
Cllr. Griffin commended the work of the Local Community Safety Partnership. He asked why 
there were difficulties arranging inspections as regards the Repair and Lease Scheme and asked 
if there are adequate staff resources in the Housing Department.    
Cllr. E. Quinlan noted that a housing application where a member of the household is autistic 
is classified as a Medical 2 priority and asked if this classification can be amended to a Medical 
1 priority. He said autism friendly accommodation should be offered to families and should be 
advertised as such on CBL and those families where a member of the household is autistic and 
find themselves living in areas which are no longer suitable because of increased noise and so 
on should be permitted to apply for a transfer on those grounds. He asked given the tendering 
process if there is a way to ensure that the public lighting contract is not awarded based on cost 
alone. 
 
C. Hartley, A/DoS said the Housing Department is a busy one and staff resources will be 
reviewed as part of the current Workforce Plan Review. Medical priority is based on medical 
opinion as to how the accommodation in which the person is residing is impacting upon the 
person’s medical condition. She said the concept of autism friendly housing can be looked at 
and said medical conditions are grounds for a transfer. 
P. Johnston, A/DoS agreed that the N25 needs urgent attention and needs upgrading and said 
it needs to be put back on the priority list of TFI. The workshop on the public lighting network 
will allow for more detailed discussions.  
 
Cllr. McGuinness agreed that the N25 needs to be brought back onto the agenda. It needs to be 
made an election issue saying road safety is primary. Referring to the recent news in relation 

to the National Children’s Hospital and  the contractor submitting  an invoice for €25M for a 
job that cost €200,000 he asked if the Council have processes in place to ensure this would not 
happen on any of our large infrastructure projects and asked,  particularly in relation to the 
North Quays, where there have been alleged breaches of employment law and  has the Council 
withheld payments. 
Cllr. Mulcahy asked if there were any plans to cull deer. He noted difficulties dealing with 
complaints to Uisce Eireann, noting they should be invited to the chamber to answer questions 
from the members. 
Cllr. Bailey asked for an update on the North Quays and asked if another briefing will be 
scheduled with BAM, Harcourt Developments etc. She asked what the plans for the existing 
train station were. She said the housing waiting list for 2 bedroomed accommodation is longer 
than that for 3 bedroomed accommodation and asked if this could be addressed. She asked 
what plans are in place for Halloween and asked to whom do people report hoarding of 
materials for bonfires, fire works and so on.   
 
M. Quinn, DoS noted that have site consultants who review all invoices, and they make 
recommendations to the project team who ensure the invoices are reasonable, in line with 
budget and in line with the overall work plan and payment schedule that we have with BAM 
and there has been no significant issues.  The overall schedule, budget of the North Quays are 
broadly on target.  In relation to the current train station the offices will continue to be used by 
CIE with no specific plan at present for the station area. 
P. Johnston, A/DoS said he would revert regarding the deer culling. As regards the Quay he 
said any aspects that can be freshened up will be looked at. 
I. Grimes, DoS said work between the Environment Department, Open Spaces, the Emergency 
Services and the Gardai takes place at this time every year. We engage with schools explaining 
the dangers of fireworks, bonfires on an ongoing basis and the Chief Fire Officer will be 
engaging with schools and the media over the coming weeks. 
C. Hartley, A/DoS said where there is an inequity between the waiting lists it is addressed. 
 
Cllr. Phelan referring to the Community Recognition Fund and the 3 applications which will 
be recommended to the Department for approval asked that the councillors have more input 
going forward and asked to see the three applications recommended. He said 90 of the 190 
successful applications under the Local Enhancement Programme 2024 had not submitted 
receipts to the Council and asked why this was the case and further asked what happens to 
funds not drawn down. 
Cllr. Fitzgerald called for a discussion in relation to the public lighting network noting the 
rising cost of the LED streetlight replacements and the cost of replacing underground cables 
noting that these underground cables which are not up to standard are the cause of faults in 
many estates across the city and county. 
Cllr. Burke asked about the retrofitting of houses and in particular about air to water heating 
systems. She raised the issue of coastal erosion and asked for a review of mitigation measures 
and asked whether mitigation measures have been put in place and if not are there plans to put 
such measures in place. In relation to Waterford Airport she asked what can be done to expedite 
the project. 
P. Johnston, A/DoS said all issues in relation to the public lighting network will be discussed 
at workshop. He said funding has been available in regard to climate adaptation and climate 
mitigation measures noting however that roads and public infrastructure would have to be 
prioritised. 
S. McKeown, CE said the assessment of the business case for Waterford Airport is with 
Minister Ryan and Minister Lawless for consideration and a positive outcome to the business 

case is required to secure the match funding that is required to carry out the upgrade works. 
There is a regional case for this project which we will continue to make.  
C. Hartley, A/DoS said a workshop has been committed to in relation to the Community 
Recognition Fund and said she will revert with an update on applications. She will speak to 
Cllr. Phelan in relation to Local Enhancement Programme and said we are working with the 
Climate Action Team to deliver user friendly information in relation to deep retrofitting.
