## Appropriate Assessment (AA)
Required under the EU Habitats Directive (92/43/EEC), Birds Directive (79/409/EEC) and s.177 of the Planning and Development (Amendment) Act 2010. Screening for Appropriate Assessment is undertaken for plans/projects affecting European sites (SAC/SPA). A Natura Impact Statement (NIS) is required where potential for significant negative effects on European sites cannot be screened out.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
An EIAR is required where EIA is determined necessary (per the Planning and Development Regulations thresholds and screening). For developments below threshold, EIA may still be required where significant environmental effects are likely. (Cork Volume 1 references the EIA framework; specific Schedule 5/7 thresholds are the statutory national thresholds.)

## Architectural / Archaeological Heritage Impact Assessment
- Archaeological assessment REQUIRED for any proposed development whose area is 0.5 hectare or more, OR whose length is 1 km or more, including large-scale infrastructure (electricity, sewerage, telecommunications, water supply, flood relief, roadworks/realignment). Assessment to follow pre-planning consultation with the County Archaeologist and may include licensed metal detection, geophysical survey, archaeological testing.
- Where archaeological materials are found, preservation in-situ is the presumed preferred option.
- (Protected structures / ACA impact addressed in Chapter 16 Built and Cultural Heritage.)

## Flood Risk Assessment (FRA) / Justification Test
- The Plan is informed by an updated Strategic Flood Risk Assessment (June 2022); the Plan-Making Justification Test was applied to zonings.
- A flood risk assessment / drainage impact assessment is required to support all planning applications (Section 11.11).
- For sites within Flood Zone A or B, a site-specific Flood Risk Assessment is required.
- The Development Management Justification Test applies to development in flood-risk zones (per the Ministerial Guidelines page 37 Justification Test).
- "Minor works" (extensions, renovations, rebuilding of existing development, small-scale infill, change of use) are exempt from the Justification Test (per Section 5.28 of the Flood Risk Management Guidelines), but an assessment of flood risk should still accompany such applications.
- FRAs must address residual risk and climate change; first-stage site-specific FRA recommended pre-application.

## Traffic & Transport Assessment / Mobility Management (Objective TM 12-8)
- Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA) and Road Safety Audit required where traffic movements may materially impact the safety/free flow of traffic on National, Regional or Local routes.
- Mobility Management Plans (travel plans) required for: developments of 50 employees or more; residential developments over 100 units; all education, community and health facilities; and major extensions to such uses.

## Site Suitability (one-off rural houses)
Site suitability and wastewater assessment per Chapter 5 §5.6 (Environmental and Site Suitability Requirements); rural housing also subject to NPF NPO19 need/siting/design tests. (No fixed numeric minimum site area in Volume 1.)
