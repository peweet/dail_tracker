                           D·n Laoghaire-Rathdown County Council

                               Housing Allocations Report for 2025

Social housing properties are allocated in line with D·n Laoghaire-Rathdown
County Council's Allocations Scheme, which was developed in accordance with
Section 22 of the Housing (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2009 and the Social
Housing Regulations 2011. Following extensive consultation with the Housing
Strategic Policy Committee (SPC), the Scheme was adopted by the Elected
Members of the Council in April 2021.

The Scheme provides a clear framework for the allocation of all available social
housing properties to eligible households. Eligibility is determined in accordance
with Section 20 of the Housing (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2009. The Scheme
also facilitates transfer applications from existing social housing support tenants
seeking alternative accommodation.

The primary mechanism used to advertise and allocate properties is the Choice
Based Letting (CBL) system. Applicants and tenants registered with CBL can also
view their position on the relevant housing waiting list. In 2025, D·n Laoghaire-
Rathdown County Council allocated a total of 618 properties in accordance with
the Allocations Scheme.

Throughout 2025, the Council continued to support vulnerable households to
secure appropriate housing outcomes:

    À 23% of all allocations were made to households that included a person
         with a disability.

    À 24% of households allocated a home had been awarded Overall Priority
         status, including medical and homeless priorities.

In 2025, we continued to promote rightsizing among existing Council tenants as
a means of addressing situations where current accommodation no longer met
household needs or was larger than required. Of the 284 transfer allocations
outlined above, 32 were made to households that had been approved to
rightsize or downsize.

All applications seeking to downsize were prioritised, and efforts will continue to
be made to match these households with more suitable accommodation. In
doing so, the Council can, in particular, free up larger properties that are more
appropriate for family households.
