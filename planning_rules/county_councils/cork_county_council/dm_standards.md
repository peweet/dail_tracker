# Development Management Standards — Cork County Council

**Authority:** Cork County Council
**Plan:** Cork County Development Plan 2022(-2028) (adopted 25 April 2022; in effect 6 June 2022)
**Chapter:** No single "Development Management" chapter. Standards are distributed: Chapter 4 Housing (density, height, open space), Chapter 12 Transport and Mobility (parking standards Section 12.24 / Table 12.6, EV, mobility/TTA), Chapter 3 Settlements and Placemaking (design), Chapter 5 Rural (rural housing/site suitability).
**Source:** Volume One Main Policy Material PDF — https://www.corkcoco.ie/sites/default/files/2022-06/volume-1-main-policy-material.pdf
**Fetch date:** 2026-06-13

## Residential Density — Net Density (Chapter 4, Objective HOU 4-7, Table 4.1) — units/ha

| Category | Min Net Density | Max Net Density | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| High | 50 | No upper limit | Town centres of larger towns (pop >1500), near high-quality public transport; min 10% open space |
| Medium A | 30 | 50 | Suburban/greenfield of larger settlements (>5,000); min 35 units/ha where high-quality PT; 20% public open space on former institutional lands |
| Medium B | 20 | 35 | Smaller towns (<5,000) and key villages |
| Medium C (Low Density) | 5 | 20 | Edge of smaller towns / key villages; alternative to one-off housing; cannot exceed 20% of new housing requirement |

Village infill/backland near core: higher densities up to 30 units/ha considered.

## Building Height (Chapter 4)
- Urban settlements generally 2-4 storeys.
- New buildings greater than 4 storeys must address the development management criteria in paragraph 3.2 of the Urban Development and Building Heights Guidelines (2018).
- Comprehensive redevelopment sites in excess of 2 ha (possible taller-building clusters) require master planning.

## Public Open Space (Chapter 4)
- High density category: minimum 10% open space.
- Former institutional lands (Medium A): minimum 20% public open space.
- Reduced provision may be accepted where larger private gardens provided (esp. Medium C / villages).

## Plot Ratio
A plot-ratio standard is applied as a density tool in specific mixed-use areas to facilitate increased density while ensuring adequate amenity space (no single county-wide numeric value tabulated in Volume 1; applied per mixed-use site/settlement).

**Parking provision rules (Table 12.6 notes):**
- 5% of car parking spaces for disabled (non-residential).
- 10% of car parking spaces for parent-and-child (non-residential).
- Motorcycle: 1 motorcycle bay per 10 car spaces (non-residential and apartments).
- Non-residential with >10 parking spaces (or substantial renovation with >10): at least one EV recharge point.
- All other spaces (incl. residential) constructed to be capable of future EV charging.
- Reduced provision acceptable with good public transport / Transport Mobility Plan.
- Standards do not apply to town-centre reuse/refurbishment/change-of-use/small infill.

## Bicycle Parking (Chapter 12, §12.12, Section 12.24)
Significantly increased relative to previous plan; quantum standards set in Section 12.24. Cycle parking to comply with the Standards for Cycle Parking and Associated Cycling Facilities for New Developments (DLR County Council, 2018) and NTA National Cycle Manual.

## EV Charging (Chapter 12, §12.15, Objective TM 12-12)
Per Climate Action Plan: new non-residential buildings with >10 parking spaces must have at least one recharging point (by 1 Jan 2025); existing non-residential buildings with >20 spaces require a minimum number of recharging points by 1 Jan 2025. All new development to comply with national EV requirements.

## Vehicular Access / Visibility (Chapter 12, §12.11)
New vehicular accesses must be designed to appropriate standards of visibility for safety. (Numeric sightline x/y values are referenced to national road design standards rather than tabulated in Volume 1.)

## Rural Housing — Site (Chapter 5)
Rural one-off housing follows NPF NPO19 (areas under urban influence: demonstrable economic/social need + siting & design; areas not under urban influence: siting & design + settlement viability) per the Sustainable Rural Housing Guidelines (2005). No fixed numeric minimum site area is set in Volume 1; site suitability (incl. wastewater) assessed per Chapter 5 §5.6 Environmental and Site Suitability Requirements.

## Wastewater Treatment Plant buffers
Development not generally permitted within 100 m of a wastewater treatment works or 25 m of a pumping station (may increase for environmental reasons; buffer may count toward open space).
