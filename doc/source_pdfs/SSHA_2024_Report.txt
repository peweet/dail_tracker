Key findings 2024

         In total, 59,941 households were assessed as qualified for social housing support as of 4th November 2024.

         The number of households qualified for social housing support increased by 1,117 households (1.9%) since the previous
         assessment on 1 November 2023 and decreased by 31,659 households (34.6%) compared to the first annual assessment
         conducted in September 2016. A total of 13 of the 31 local authorities recorded a decrease in numbers in 2024.

         The four Dublin Authorities (Dublin City, Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown, Fingal and South Dublin) account for a marginally
         lower proportion of the national total social housing need at 41.0% (compared to 41.6% in 2023).

         One-adult households remain the majority household grouping on the waiting list and grew proportionately from
         57.4% of the total in 2023 to 60.8% in 2024.

         One-parent family households continue to be the second highest household grouping, decreasing from a proportion
         of 23.3% to 21.2% between 2023 and 2024.

         The number and proportion of households whose basis of need for social housing is homelessness increased from
         13.5% in 2023 to 14.7% in 2024.

         The proportion of households living with parents, relatives and friends combined is now the most common tenure
         type for households, increasing from 24.5% in the 2023 count to 36.6% in 2024˛. Consequentially, the Private Rented
         Sector (with and without RS) no longer remains the largest tenure type for households on the waiting list and its
         proportional representation has roughly halved since 2016 (from 67.4% to 33.9%).

In 2022, the SSHA began reporting the number of households living in Direct Provision. In 2024, there were 1,799
households recorded in this current accommodation type.

A Traveller Identifier was also introduced from 2022, and in 2024, 1,044 households identified as Irish Travellers.

There were less households on the waiting list for a period of more than 7 years, with this proportion declining from
21.5% in 2023 to 19.2% in 2024. Conversely, more households were on the list for less than 2 years with these
proportions increasing from 40.1% in 2023 to 42.9% in 2024.

The proportion of households with a main applicant over 60 years of age has been growing year on year and now
makes up 12.0% of total households. This is a 4.8 percentage point increase on the proportion of households in this
age range in 2016 (7.2%).

          The employment status of most qualified applicants remains unemployed, however this continues to fall (from 49.4%
          in 2023 to 47.6% in 2024) and the largest proportion of households continue to have an income source from social
          welfare only, although this is also falling (from 53.3% in 2023 to 52.3% in 2024).

˛ One local authority inadvertently grouped all households as within the Private Rented Sector in 2023.
These updates are explained in the footnotes 13 and 25 (Table 2.7).

What is the SSHA?

total number of households qualified for social housing support across the country
whose social housing need is not being met, in order to better understand the level
of need for such support. It is a snapshot at a point in time of the housing lists of 31
local authorities. This year's count was carried out on 4 November 2024.
