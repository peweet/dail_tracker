Assessment triggers enumerated in Chapter 15 (quoted, no inference):

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR) — 15.1.5
- Under the EIA Directive (2011/92/EU as amended by 2014/52/EU): public and private projects likely to have significant effects on the environment are granted permission only after assessment.
- Environmental factors assessed (Article 3): Population and Human Health; Biodiversity; Land and Soil; Water; Air and Climate (and others per Directive).

## Appropriate Assessment (AA) — 15.1.6
- All plans/projects (including the Council's own proposals under Part 8 of the Planning and Development Regulations 2001 as amended), unless directly connected with the management of a Natura 2000 site, must be subject to **screening for Appropriate Assessment** to determine likely significant effect on a Natura 2000 site (individually or in combination). Full AA / Natura Impact Statement required where screening indicates.

## Architectural Heritage
- Development affecting protected structures / ACAs governed by the Architectural Heritage Protection Guidelines for Planning Authorities (2011); international guidance per ICOMOS.

## Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA) — 15.7
- Required for larger developments per **Table 15.7**, in accordance with TII publication PE-PDV-02045 Traffic and Transport Assessment Guidelines (2014).
- Trigger example quoted: "Traffic to and from the development exceeds 10% of the traffic flow on the adjoining road." Applicants should consult the Transportation Department before submission.

## Flood risk / surface water
- Surface Water Management / SuDS measures required (15.8.1). Flood-risk handled via the Plan's Strategic Flood Risk Assessment and sequential approach (referenced in plan-level chapters rather than enumerated as a separate Justification Test trigger in this chapter's captured text).

## Rural wastewater / site suitability
- On-site wastewater treatment addressed; rural dwelling layout per Kildare Rural House Design Guide (Appendix 4). Explicit "percolation/site-suitability test" wording not located in the captured chapter text.

Note: Justification Test and percolation/site-suitability are not explicitly enumerated under those exact terms in the captured Chapter 15 text.
