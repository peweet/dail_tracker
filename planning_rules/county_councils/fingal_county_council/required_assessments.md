Assessment triggers enumerated in Chapter 14 (quoted, no inference):

## Appropriate Assessment (AA) — Objective DMSO1
- Under Article 6 of the Habitats Directive: establish whether AA is required for plans/projects. If screening finds AA required, the proponent must prepare a **Natura Impact Statement (NIS)**.
- DMSO1: ensure all plans and projects which could (individually or in combination) have a significant effect on a European site are subject to Screening for Appropriate Assessment.

## Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA / EIAR)
- EIA considers whether projects (alone or in combination) are likely to have significant effects on the environment.
- The Planning and Development Regulations 2001 (as amended) set out **mandatory thresholds** for specified classes of development. Where required, the applicant prepares an **EIAR**, examined by the Planning Authority leading to a reasoned conclusion.

## Architectural Heritage Impact Assessment (AHIA)
- Required for any development relating to a **protected structure** or within the curtilage & attendant grounds of a protected structure.
- Also for development within an **ACA** boundary where it impacts a building contributing to the ACA character, or for large-scale development within an ACA, or for protected structures/ACA of a complex nature.

## Flood Risk Assessment / Strategic Flood Risk Assessment (SFRA)
- The SFRA (2021) maps flood risk in the County and supports a sequential approach to planning. To be read in parallel with the Plan.
- Site-specific Flood Risk Assessment listed among assessments that may accompany applications.

## Traffic and Transport Assessment (TTA) — Objective DMSO116
- Applications for new residential, commercial, mixed-use, industrial and educational developments shall be accompanied by a TTA as required by the Council, having regard to the TII Traffic and Transport Assessment Guidelines 2014 (and updates).

## Other assessments referenced (non-exhaustive list in chapter)
- Landscape Appraisal, Archaeological Assessment, Architectural Assessment.
- On-site wastewater: percolation areas must comply with the EPA Code of Practice (Wastewater Treatment Systems Serving Single Houses) — site-suitability/percolation testing implied for single houses.

Note: the chapter states this list of assessments "is not exhaustive."
