Patrick Glynn (the **"Maintainer"**). This policy explains how the name may and
may not be used. It is **separate** from the software licences: the
[AGPL](../LICENSE) and the [commercial licence](../COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md) cover
the *code*; this policy covers the *name and brand*. Open-source licences do not
grant trademark rights.

*Nothing here is legal advice.*

## You may, without asking

- State, accurately and without implying endorsement, that your project **"uses
  Dáil Tracker"**, is **"built on Dáil Tracker"**, or is **"compatible with Dáil
  Tracker"**.
- Use the name in **factual references**, articles, talks, tutorials, and reviews.
- Link to the project and quote its documentation with attribution.

## You may NOT, without written permission

- Use "Dáil Tracker" (or a confusingly similar name) as the **name of your own
  product, service, company, domain, or app**, or in a way that suggests your
  offering is the official project or is endorsed by or affiliated with the
  Maintainer.
- Use the name or logo as your **primary branding** for a commercial service —
  including a hosted/SaaS version, even one that is otherwise AGPL-compliant.
- Modify the logo, or use the name/logo in a misleading, disparaging, or
  unlawful way.

## Forks

You may fork the code under the AGPL, but a public fork must use a **different
name** and must not present itself as the official "Dáil Tracker". You may state
that your fork is *"a fork of Dáil Tracker"*.

## Commercial use of the brand

Commercial use of the "Dáil Tracker" name or logo (for example, to offer a
branded hosted service) requires a **trademark licence**, which can be included
in a commercial agreement. Enquiries: **{{LICENSING_EMAIL}}** /

## Why this exists

The trademark is an independent lever from copyleft: even where the AGPL permits
copying the code, it does not permit trading under our name. This protects users
from confusion and protects the project's reputation, while keeping the code
fully open.
