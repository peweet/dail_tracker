3. Presentation: Night Time Economy Advisor, Cork City Council. 
The vice chairperson welcomed Fiona Collins, the Night Time Economy Advisor in Cork City 
Council to the committee members.  
Fiona Collins presented to the committee highlighting the positive feedback from the public 
regarding the Night Time Economy (NTE). The NTE strategy is also accessible online. Fiona 
recommended the importance of making sure that NTE is included in policies, including the 
LECP 2024-2029. Fiona also spoke about various campaigns such as the Purple Flag, Safe 
and Sound, and Éanna and the magic of the night – “an initiative to help young people 
(especially children) feel more comfortable in the dark. This project also promotes 
appreciation for dark spaces, night-time biodiversity, and events that take place after sunset”.

10. AOB 
Dermot Crosby advised the committee about the Work Placement Experience Programme 
(WPEP). The WPEP is free of charge to employers if they can provide 30 hours of work 
experience to a jobseeker for 6 months. Intreo will pay the jobseeker each week, the single 
rate is €369. The employer is not obliged to hire the jobseeker at the end of work placement, 
but there are financial supports available to help the employer if they wish to do. The WPEP is 
available to employers in all sectors and many of the jobseekers are progressing into 
employment following placement. All the employers of the LCDC members are eligible for the 
WPEP. More information is available at www.gov.ie/wpep.
