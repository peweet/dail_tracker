## Residential density
- Minimum net density of **35 units per hectare** (net density) — to be applied particularly to greenfield sites or larger 'A'-zoned areas.
- Higher density of **50 units per hectare** (net density) will be encouraged in appropriate locations.
- Density per "Sustainable Residential Development in Urban Areas" Guidelines (2009).

## Separation / privacy distances
- A minimum clearance distance of **circa 22 metres** in general is required between opposing windows (consistent with the Apartment Guidelines 2020; per Table 12.4). Maximum provision may be increased for refurbishment schemes.
- New residential units of 5 storeys or more: an obvious buffer must exist from the rear garden boundary lines of existing private dwellings.
- Garden tables/structures should be located not less than 22 metres from the rear façade of the existing dwelling where overlooking footpaths/roads/open spaces.

## Public / communal open space
- **15%** public open space of site for residential development (general standard, Core Strategy figure 2.9).
- **25%** open space of total site area required on **Institutional ("INST") Lands**.
- Communal amenity space: in schemes in excess of 50 units, no more than 30% of communal amenity space [restriction]. 
- "Existing Housing Stock — Adaptation": at least **20%** open space of the overall site area.

## Car parking — Section 12.4.5
- Comprehensive car parking standards set out in Section 12.4.5; **four parking zones** established for the County to ensure adequate residential and balanced provision (Policy Objective T18: Car Parking Standards; Map T2).
- Detailed per-use ratios are in the Section 12.4.5 / Table 12.x of the source (two-column layout — see PDF).

## Cycle parking — Section 12.4.6
- Cycle parking standards per the Council's "Standards for Cycle Parking and Associated Cycle Parking Facilities for New Developments" (2018); to be refined during the Plan to align with the car-parking zones.

## Electric Vehicle
- Plan supports expansion of the public EV charging network; supports decarbonising of motorised transport. EV/LEV provision encouraged in new developments (aligned to national 2030 zero-emission targets).

## Driveways / front gardens — 12.4.8.3
- For a single residential dwelling, the maximum width of an entrance is **3.5 metres**.
- A minimum of **one third** of front garden areas should be maintained in grass or landscaped (urban greening / SuDS).
- Proper provision shall be made for sightlines at the exit from driveways onto the public road (in the interest of public safety).

## Site coverage / plot ratio
- Site coverage referenced qualitatively (extensions must not result in significant loss of landscaping / high site coverage). No single fixed numeric site-coverage / plot-ratio threshold located in the captured Chapter 12 text (treated zone-by-zone / design-led).

## Rural / one-off housing
- DLR is a predominantly urban/suburban authority; one-off housing in the County is regarded as urban-generated (Chapter 12 / Chapter 3). No rural one-off minimum site-area standard is set in this plan (no rural-generated housing need framework as in rural counties).
