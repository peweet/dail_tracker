A report from Mr. A. Dunney, Director of Services, Planning, Enterprise, Economic 
Development and Emergency Services and Ms. A. Aspell, Director of Services, 
Housing and Regeneration informed members that the Kildare Land Activation Office 
(KLAO) was established in Q4 2025 to provide a proactive and coordinated 
approach, rather than a reactive approach, to the delivery of housing units alongside 
and in tandem with physical and social infrastructure, particularly in the context of 
strategic urban expansion areas.  
   
The KLAO is a team within the Planning, Enterprise, Economic Development 
and Emergency Services Directorate. It is a multi-disciplinary team focused of 
the implementation of certain key development area masterplans in the 
County.  A Steering Group, consisting of the Director of Planning, Enterprise, 
Economic Development and Emergency Services, Director of Transportation, 
Mobility & Open Spaces and Director of Housing & Regeneration will govern the 
work of the team, particularly given that housing delivery requires coordination 
across these directorates in terms of mixed tenure housing, infrastructure delivery, 
parks, open spaces and utility coordination.  
   
The recently launched Government Housing Action Plan, Delivering Homes, 
Building Communities, requires each local authority to prepare a Housing Delivery 
Action Plan (HDAP), reflecting local need identified within the Housing Needs and 
Demand Assessment Framework, setting out how to increase new-build social 
housing, implement the Starter Home Programme (including affordable housing), and 
ensure the right mix of homes to respond to local need and priorities, 
including homelessness.  Direction is awaited from Department of Housing, Local 
Government  and Heritage in terms of the preparation of the Plan, 
including timeframe. The elected  members will be briefed further as soon as this 
information is to hand.   
 Currently the KLAO work programme concentrates on identified strategic 
urban expansion areas such as:  
• Naas Northwest Quadrant Master Plan, including liaising with 
consultants/developers/landowners/utilities etc. and conducting all stakeholder 
engagements in the advancement of this project.

• Progressing the work and delivery of the Confey Master Plan, again liaising

• Establishing and driving activation projects in collaboration with the National

Large-Scale Sport Infrastructure Fund (LSSIF)  
Understanding that the primary objective of the motion is to identify funding 
streams for the delivery of large-scale sports infrastructure, the most relevant 
established scheme is the Large-Scale Sport Infrastructure Fund (LSSIF).  However, 
the motion sets out also the need for land acquisition, but conditions of the LSSIF 
state that the purchase of land or buildings or of loan repayments for projects are 
ineligible under the Fund.  Accordingly, if funding is to be pursued under this, the 
existence of land either in Council ownership or where an applicant holds ownership 
or long-term lease with at least 25 years remaining is essential.  This is because 
funding goes to the  organisation that has legal title to property.  
  

 
 
 
 
Minutes of Kildare County Council meeting 15.12.2025                 Page 17 of 23 
 
The full LSSIF Guidance is available here and just a selection of criteria is set 
out below.    
Priority will be given to projects that are:   
• Identified as a priority within a local authority’s development plan.  
• Identified as a priority of a sport National Governing Body (NGB) in its strategy 
for the development of active participation, social participation and 
improvement of high performance in the sport.  
• Multi-functional in nature and cater for a number of sports.  
• Facilities that are open to the general public.  
• Prioritise the needs of disadvantaged areas and groups including people with 
disabilities.  
• Require funding for the development of capital infrastructure.  
• Have not yet started on site.   
• Are seeking a grant of at least €600,000 and identify confirmed capital 
partnership funding for the project.  
• Priority will be given to shovel ready projects i.e. where planning permission 
has been granted and the application shows the project can be delivered in a 
reasonable time period.   
  
Guidance further advises that priority is likely to be given to joint applications by 
a local authority and a national governing body of a sport.  In addition, it is 
a requirement that the applicant demonstrate they can contribute a minimum of 30% 
of the total project costs as match funding.  
  
More than one application may be made by NGBs and local authorities, but 
these applications must be listed in order of priority.  
  
  
The LSSIF is currently closed and, in the last round of funding in November 2024, 
3 Kildare-based projects were successful, with two in the Naas Municipal 
District obtaining a total of €4million in funding.  The Government has not yet 
announced a date for the reopening of the LSSIF stating as recently as October 2025 
that its immediate focus is ensuring project delivery under the 2024 LSSIF funding 
awards.

A report from Ms. C. Barrett, Director of Services, Transport, Mobility and Open 
Spaces advised members of the following:  
Outdoor Recreation Infrastructure Scheme (ORIS)  
ORIS grants are available to fund land, water and air-based activities in 
the countryside.  There are four (4) strands of funding available. These are:  
• Measure 1: Grant for small scale development / repair, marketing and 
promotion.  The maximum grant is €30,000.  10% match 
funding must be provided, and a maximum of seven (7) projects can 
be submitted by local authorities.  
• Measure 2: Grant for medium scale repair/upgrade and new trail/amenity 
development.  The maximum grant is €200,000 with 10% match funding.  A 
maximum of 3no projects is eligible.  
• Measure 3: Grant for strategic large-scale repair/upgrade and new strategic 
trail/amenity development.  The maximum grant is €500,000 with 10% match 
funding.  A maximum of one (1) project is eligible.  
• Project Development Measure: Grant for the detailed development of projects 
which would enable them to reach a standard for application under Measure 2 
or 3 of ORIS.  The maximum grant is €50,000 with 10% match funding.  A 
maximum of two (2) projects is eligible.   
  
Sports Capital Grant   
Funding for development of sports facilities up to maximum grant of 
€200,000.  Funding is also available for national or regional type sports projects up to 
a maximum grant of €500,000.  Grant aid is to maximum of 90% of the project cost.  
  
Capital Grant Scheme for Play and Recreation  
Grant funding of a maximum of €20,000 available for refurbishment of existing 
play and recreation facilities and/or incorporating natural play elements or new play 
and recreation facilities. 25% match funding is required.  Only one (1) application can

Urban Regeneration and Development Fund (URDF)  
The Urban Regeneration and Development Fund (URDF) was established under the 
management of the Department of Housing, Local Government and Heritage, as a 
key funding mechanism of Project Ireland 2040.  The key driver of this fund is to 
regenerate and rejuvenate strategic and underutilised areas and is of relevance to 
County Kildare in the context of towns with a population more than 10,000.  When 
the fund was originally established there were five (5) towns within this category 
in the county: Celbridge, Leixlip, Maynooth, Naas and Newbridge.  As a result of the 
outturn of Census 2022 two further towns within the county now fall within these 
criteria: Athy and Kildare.  Kildare County Council has received funding for projects 
under each of the URDF Fund Calls, with Call 3 being specifically related to address 
vacancy and dereliction.  Updates on relevant projects are included in the monthly 
Chief Executive report for the information of the elected members.  
   
Town and Cities Infrastructure Investment Fund  
Under the Programme for Government 2025: Securing Ireland’s future, 
the Government has indicated the establishment of the Town and Cities 
Infrastructure Investment Fund, which will replace URDF.  Details on the criteria 
relating to this fund  have not been received yet.  
  
Town Centre First Heritage Revival Scheme (THRIVE)  
THRIVE – Town Centre First Heritage Revival Scheme, supports locally 
developed plans that take an integrated strategic approach to the regeneration of our 
towns.  The scheme funds the renovation of disused heritage buildings with 
potentially St. David’s Castle being a suitable project for the next round of funding, as 
it is now in the ownership of Kildare County Council (a qualifying requirement for the 
fund).  Strand 1 and 2 have been published and projects confirmed with the council 
proposing an application for Strand 3 which may be announced in Q4 2026 or early 
2027.

The following motion in the name of Councillor Pádraig McEvoy was considered by

That Kildare County Council write to the Minister for Transport, the Minister for the 
Environment, Climate and Communications, the Minister for Housing, Local 
Government and Heritage, the Minister for Public Expenditure, National Development 
Plan Delivery and Reform, and the Oireachtas Members for County Kildare to 
express this Council’s concern regarding the National Development Plan Review 
2025 Sectoral Investment Plan for Transport, in particular: 
• the impact that delays to regionally significant light and heavy rail projects will 
have on achieving both housing-delivery and emissions-reduction targets in 
County Kildare; and 
• the impact that insufficient funding for walking and cycling infrastructure will 
have on public safety, public health, and traffic congestion in County Kildare; 
and to request that the relevant decisions within the Sectoral Investment Plan be 
reviewed and amended to address these concerns. 
 
The motion was proposed by Councillor McEvoy and seconded by Councillor 
Connolly. 
A report from Ms L Hanratty, Meetings Administrator stated that this was a matter for 
the members to agree.

Councillor McEvoy informed members that County Kildare is experiencing rapid 
population growth, but transport infrastructure is not keeping pace. Although the 
county has exceeded national housing targets—delivering 143% of its 2020 goal, 
particularly along rail corridors—major transport projects have fallen behind. The 
most significant example is the delayed DART+ South-West project, which is 
shovel-ready but repeatedly pushed back under the National Development Plan 
Review. 
The lack of timely rail investment is already leading to increased car dependency, 
longer commutes, worsening congestion, and higher emissions across the Greater 
Dublin Area. Rail infrastructure is most effective when delivered alongside housing 
growth, not years later when congestion is already entrenched. 
Active travel funding has increased since 2021, but delivery remains slow. Around 
40% of walking and cycling projects are not expected to be completed until after 
2031, leaving communities waiting years for safer streets and viable alternatives to 
driving. 
The core issue is not policy ambition but delivery timelines. Kildare has absorbed 
significant housing growth on the understanding that transport investment would 
follow. To meet climate goals, improve mobility, and support quality of life, DART+ 
South-West must proceed without further delay, and active travel funding should 
prioritise completing fewer projects more quickly—especially in fast-growing areas. 
Aligning transport investment with where people already live and travel is essential 
for reducing congestion, supporting climate action, and improving public safety in 
County Kildare.

and agreed by members present that the council write to the Minister for Transport, 
the Minister for the Environment, Climate and Communications, the Minister for 
Housing, Local Government and Heritage, the Minister for Public Expenditure, 
National Development Plan Delivery and Reform, and the Oireachtas Members for 
County Kildare to express this Council’s concern regarding the National
