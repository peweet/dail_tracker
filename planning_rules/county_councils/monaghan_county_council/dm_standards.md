## Residential Density (15.8.7 – 15.8.8)
- HDP 1: density of units within multi-unit residential schemes to accord with the Sustainable Residential Development and Compact Settlements Guidelines for Planning Authorities (2024) — **no fixed dwellings-per-hectare table in the chapter**; context-driven.
- Low-density residential on 'Proposed Residential B' lands (incl. serviced sites): "maximum density of 15 units per hectare (unless where site-specific circumstances can justify otherwise)."
- Part V (15.8.6): 20% social housing on residential/mixed-use zoned land.
- Dwelling mix (15.8.4): "In private housing schemes, normally a minimum of 10% of housing units shall be 2-bedroom units."

## Orientation / Separation of Dwellings (15.8.2)
- Qualitative only: houses should overlook streets/spaces, minimise overlooking/overshadowing, individual pedestrian access to rear garden generally provided. **No numeric separation/privacy distance stated in this chapter.**

## Open Space (15.8.12 – 15.8.14)
- Public open space per Sustainable Residential Development and Compact Settlements Guidelines (2024), Policy & Objective 5.1 (no fixed % in chapter).
- **Table 15.2 Recreational Facilities and Public Open Space Standards** (by dwelling-unit count; brownfield row noted):
  - 0-40 units: (none additionally required)
  - 41-150 units: Formal Play Area
  - 151-200 units: Formal Play Area + Playing Pitch
  - >200 units: Formal Play Area + Playing Pitch + Changing Facilities
- Private open space (15.8.13): per 2024 Compact Settlements Guidelines and Sustainable Urban Housing: Design Standards for New Apartments (2022) — no fixed m² in chapter.
- Relaxation (15.8.14) permitted for derelict/town-centre sites, protected structures, ACAs, proximity to public parks, etc.

## Childcare (15.8.15)
- Developments of 75 units or more shall provide one childcare facility (minimum capacity 20 child places).

## Rural One-Off Housing (15.9)
- Site Selection & Design Guidelines (15.9.2, Table 15.3): qualitative vernacular-style siting/design (shelter, established boundaries, minimum of one natural existing boundary in addition to roadside, not breaking ridgeline/shoreline, set back from road). **No numeric minimum rural site area stated** — rural site viability governed by EPA wastewater compliance (below).
- Wastewater (15.9.3/15.9.4/15.10): on-site treatment to comply with EPA Code of Practice — Wastewater Treatment and Disposal Systems Serving Single Houses (PE10) (2021).

## Car Parking Standards (Table 15.5, 15.24) — Maximum standards, selected
- Dwelling: 2 per unit.
- Apartment (1-2 bed): 1.5 per unit; Apartment (3-4 bed): 2 per unit.
- Nursing Home/Hospital: 1 per bedspace (incl. ambulance/service-lorry parking).
- Health Centres/Surgeries: 1 per staff member + 1 per consulting room.
- Retail/Service Station: 1 per 15 m² GFA.
- Office/Financial & Professional: 1 per 30 m² GFA.
- Schools: 1 per teaching staff + 1 per classroom (plus set-down/pick-up).
- Café/Bar/Lounge/Restaurant: 1 per 20 m² GFA.
- Industry: 1 per 50 m² GFA.
- Garage/Showroom: 1 per 100 m² GFA.
- Crèches: 1 per 6 children + 1 per staff.
- Hostel: 2 spaces per hole [sic — table cell offset]; values per source table.
- Up to **50% reduction** of Table 15.5 standards for infill/brownfield/derelict sites within designated town centres.
- Disabled/mobility-impaired parking: **one space in twenty-five** (1 in 25).

## EV Charging (15.24)
- "All residential developments with proposed communal parking and all non-residential proposed development shall normally provide EV car parking spaces at a rate of one space in five" (1 in 5).

## Bicycle / Scooter Parking (15.25)
- Required within site (covered/uncovered, long-stay/short-stay). National standards not yet available; provision to be in line with **Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown County Council "Standards for Cycle Parking and associated Cycling Facilities for New Developments" (January 2018)** or other applicable standards.

## Plot Ratio / Site Coverage / Building Heights
- Not tabulated numerically in Chapter 15 (the 'Proposed Residential B' serviced-site provision requires a Design Concept Statement specifying a maximum house-to-plot ratio, but no figure given). Building heights addressed via national guidelines elsewhere in the plan.
