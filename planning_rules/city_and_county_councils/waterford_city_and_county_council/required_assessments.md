Assessment triggers referenced in Volume 2 (note: Volume 2 is the DM-standards volume; the formal statutory environmental-assessment triggers — Appropriate Assessment, strategic EIA — are set out chiefly in Volume 1 and the plan's SEA/AA documentation. Volume 2 references the following operational triggers):

- **Road Safety Audit & Traffic Impact Assessment (Section 8.5):** Road Safety Audits required for development requiring a new vehicular access, or alteration of an existing access, onto National Primary/National roads; Traffic and Transport Assessment prepared per the Traffic Management Guidelines (DoT 2003) and the NRA/TII Traffic and Transport Assessment Guidelines (2014).
- **DMURS (Section 8.8):** mandatory for all urban roads/streets within the 60 km/h urban speed-limit zone (except motorways and, exceptionally, certain roads).
- **Flood Risk (Section 9.8 — Flood Risk Mitigation of Developments):** flood-risk mitigation requirements for developments; flood-zone-based controls (detailed flood-risk policy and Justification Test sit in Volume 1 / SFRA).
- **Architectural / Built Heritage (Chapter 11 of Vol 2, incl. 11.7 — Relaxation of Zoning Objectives for Protected Structures):** controls for Protected Structures and their settings; works to protected structures assessed against the Architectural Heritage Protection Guidelines.
- **Site suitability / wastewater (rural housing, Chapter 4):** safe access and acceptable wastewater provision required; rural dwellings require site assessment / percolation suitability (EPA Code of Practice; Sustainable Rural Housing Guidelines 2005).
- **Landscaping Plan:** detailed landscaping plan by a qualified professional required for relevant developments.
- **Tree Bonds / arboricultural protection:** may be required to protect trees during construction.

Note: Volume 2 enumerates the operational/transport/heritage/site-suitability triggers above. Appropriate Assessment (Natura 2000) and statutory EIA/EIAR thresholds are addressed in Volume 1 and the accompanying SEA/AA Natura Impact Report rather than in the Volume 2 DM-standards text captured here.
