## Setbacks / Building Line (33.2.1)
- Development normally not carried out in front of established building lines; "Where development is proposed along roads of traffic importance, increased setbacks may be" required. (No fixed metric setback table.)

## Separation / Privacy Distances (33.3.5)
- "In general, there should be a separation of at least 16 metres between opposing windows serving habitable rooms at the rear or side of houses, duplex units and apartment units, above ground floor level." (Below 16 m acceptable where no opposing habitable-room windows + privacy measures, or for protected structures / inner-core brownfield.)
- "A minimum of 2.3 metres shall be provided between the side walls of detached, semi-detached and end-of-terrace dwellings... A property boundary should occur mid-way along this separation."

## Public Open Space — Multi-Unit Housing (33.3.7)
- "A minimum of 10% and a maximum of 15% of the net site area shall be reserved for the provision of communal open space, landscaped to a high standard." (10-15% range does not apply where significant heritage/landscape/recreation/nature-conservation features require more; financial contribution in lieu possible per s.48.)

## Bicycle Parking Standards (Table 33.9, minimums)
- Housing: 1 private secure bicycle space per bed space, minimum 2 spaces per unit; 1 visitor space per two housing units.
- Offices: 10% of employee numbers (min 10, or 1 per car space, whichever greater).
- Schools: 10% of pupil registration, min 10; all sheltered.
- Other developments: 1 cycle space per car space or 10% of employee numbers.
- Shops: 1 stand per till/checkout.
- Public transport nodes: min 10 sheltered.
- Off-street car parks (incl. multi-storey): min 15 or 10% of total car spaces, sheltered.

## Car Parking Standards (Table 33.10) — ranges, selected (Town Centre TC1/TC2 | All other areas)
- House: 1–1.5 per unit | 1–2 per unit.
- Apartment: 0.5–1 per unit | 1–1.5 per unit.
- Hotel/Guesthouse: 1–1.5 per 5 bedrooms | 1–2 per bedroom.
- Office: 1–2 per 200 sq.m | 1–2 per 100 sq.m.
- Warehouse: n/a | 1–2 per 50 sq.m.
- Retail (Convenience): 1–1.5 per 30 sq.m | 1–1.5 per 20 sq.m.
- Supermarkets: n/a | 1–2 per 30 sq.m.
- (Full table covers schools, medical, community, data centre, etc. — see source.)
- Maximum standards exclude car-club bays, designated short-stay, on-street EV stations, accessible spaces.

## EV Charging Point Standards (Table 33.11)
- Residential multi-unit (new + major renovations) with private/visitor spaces: minimum **one EV charge point space per five car parking spaces**; ducting for every parking space.
- New dwellings with in-curtilage parking: install infrastructure to enable an EV recharging point.
- Non-residential with >10 private spaces: at least one recharging point; min one space per five equipped with a functional EV charging point.
- Publicly accessible car parking: at least one recharging point; min one per five equipped.

## Rural One-Off Housing (33.4)
- The chapter sets qualitative siting/design standards (vernacular design, boundaries, screening) and requires a **Design Statement for every single rural house** (except outline applications). **No numeric minimum rural site area is specified in this chapter** — rural site suitability is governed by EPA wastewater site assessment (below) and the rural housing settlement policy in other volumes.

## On-Site Wastewater (33.2.7) — rural site suitability
- Compliance with EPA 2021 Code of Practice Domestic Waste Water Treatment Systems (PE ≤10) and S.R. 66:2015.
- "a site assessment must be carried out for all planning applications involving provision of a new or retention of an existing on-site wastewater treatment system," by a suitably qualified professional, per the EPA Code of Practice (i.e. percolation/site-suitability testing).
- Discharge of domestic wastewater from single houses to surface water bodies not permitted.

## Other Quantitative Items
- Biodiversity Net Gain: planning applications on sites of **0.5 ha and over** shall demonstrate biodiversity net gain (incl. ecological surveys).
- Family flat / annex accommodation: "shall generally not exceed a gross floor area of 70 sqm."
- TTA may be required for retail developments over 1,000 sq.m; >1,000 sq.m new/additional gross floorspace triggers transport considerations.
- Service-station ancillary shop: up to 100 sq.m net sales area without separate retail assessment; over 100 sq.m assessed as retail.
- Larger rural houses (e.g. in excess of 200 m²) should be sub-divided into smaller elements.
- Advertising sign (specified type) maximum area 0.7 sq.m.
- Density: governed by reference to the Sustainable Residential Development and Compact Settlements Guidelines (2024); no fixed uph table in this chapter.
